## What Happened

### Oil crashes, rupee firms, equities sell off anyway

The day's organising macro reversed hard. Yesterday's "ceasefire being shot at" became a mostly-agreed 60-day understanding between the United States and Iran to extend the truce, reopen the Strait of Hormuz and open a negotiation window. Brent fell about 19% over May — its worst month since the pandemic — to $91.19, and the rupee firmed 1.1% to 94.99 while the dollar index sat flat. Yet the first post-holiday tape sold off: Sensex down 1.44%, Nifty down 1.5%, with metals, autos and consumer staples all off about 2%. The reason is the gap between commodities and stocks. Oil and the rupee priced the optimism; equities priced the binary, because the deal is unsigned, Iranian strikes continued Thursday, and the weather office cut the monsoon forecast to below-normal. Markets bought the relief in barrels and sold the uncertainty in shares.

### Fertiliser cohort hits fresh lows as the subsidy rises

The split read landed hardest on the fertiliser names. Coromandel International (COROMANDEL), a tier-one holding, fell 4.26% to a fresh 52-week low at ₹1,753.60 — a 2.8% underperformance against the market — as the weather office's below-normal monsoon call (about 90% of the long-period average) read straight through to weaker farm-input demand. Dhanuka Agritech (DHANUKA) fell 3.85% in sympathy, though it still holds a positive month. The offset arrived the same day: the union cabinet approved the Kharif 2026 nutrient subsidy at ₹41,533 crore, up 11.6%, with a richer sulphur and phosphate component and the retail price of the main fertiliser bag held flat. So the cohort faces a demand scare from the sky and a margin cushion from the treasury at once — the end-July cumulative rainfall print is what breaks the tie.

### A clutch of holdings decouples to fresh highs

Against a market down 1.5%, four names ran the other way. Garware Hi-Tech Films (GRWRHITECH), a holding, rose 6.47% to a fresh high — an 8% outperformance and now up 60% on the month, the biggest decoupler in the watched set, on no fresh filing. Acutaas Chemicals (ACUTAAS), also held, gained 4.40% to its own record on the specialty-chemicals re-rating, up 25% on the month. SJS Enterprises (SJS) added 3.25% to a fresh high against a falling auto index, and Radico Khaitan (RADICO) rose 2.31% against a falling consumer-staples index — a near-4% relative bid on premium spirits. None carried a same-day catalyst at the primary-source level; all four are momentum re-ratings whose monthly velocity is now the watch, not the entry.

### Inox Wind's audit notes flag a quality-of-earnings watch

A new forensic flag opened outside the portfolio. Inox Wind (INOXWIND), a tier-two wind-turbine maker, filed audited results carrying two auditor notes flagging specific risks — one on the recoverability of money invested in six special-purpose vehicles, one on contract-completion accounting for turbine supply and maintenance. The auditor kept a clean overall opinion; under the desk's rule, such a note is the soft-landing version of a problem, not its absence. Compounding it, fourth-quarter profit fell 54% year-on-year to ₹87.6 crore, and the company moved a ₹9.9-crore investment to group entities for ₹173.5 crore. Full-year profit still rose 42%, so the print is genuinely mixed — but the two notes plus the intra-group transfer are exactly the cluster that compounds across quarters. The baseline is set for any future research read.

## Cascade Watch

**The fertiliser-subsidy edge stepped up on a signed policy.** The cabinet approved the Kharif nutrient subsidy at ₹41,533 crore, up 11.6%, with richer sulphur and phosphate components. The fertiliser-subsidy regulatory edge fired into Coromandel International and Chambal Fertilisers, each stepping up a notch. Tomorrow's read: this is a margin cushion that buffers the input-cost squeeze that drove Coromandel's last-quarter profit down 76% — watch whether the cohort separates the subsidy gain from the demand fear.

**The monsoon agri-input edge sharpened across three names.** The weather office's below-normal seasonal call, about 90% of the long-period average, was read as a food-price upside risk. The monsoon agri-input edge stepped up into Dhanuka Agritech, Coromandel International and Varun Beverages. The mechanism splits: a hot, dry season extends the summer beverage window for Varun Beverages but caps farm-input volumes for the two agrochemical names. The end-July cumulative rainfall reading is the point where the cohort's split read resolves.

**The crude-collapse edge cut against the shipping cohort.** Brent fell about 20% on the month as the Gulf de-escalated. The crude-import-relief edge is a tailwind for the rupee and fuel-input names, but the same move is a headwind for the tanker trade, which earned its record year on disruption. Great Eastern Shipping (GESHIP) fell 6.11%, a third straight session of stress, as the disruption premium in freight rates unwound. Tomorrow's read: a signed deal plus Hormuz reopening would deepen the tanker de-rating even as it lifts the importers.

**The consumer-staples edge produced two relative bids, not a cohort sell.** The Nifty FMCG index fell 1.51%, and the sector-membership edge fired into Radico Khaitan and Varun Beverages. Yet Radico rose 2.31% against that falling index — a near-4% relative bid on premium spirits ahead of an investor-conference run. The mechanism here is dispersion, not contagion: the staples weakness is monsoon-and-rural-demand fear, which premium discretionary spirits sit above. The read is that the FMCG tape is sorting winners from the rural-exposed, not selling the shelf wholesale.
