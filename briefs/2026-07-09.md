## What Happened

### TCS print holds the line — patience on IT, not a re-rate
Tata Consultancy Services (TCS, the IT-cohort bellwether we track as a driver, not a holding) declared its June-quarter results after the close, opening the earnings season. The numbers came in soft-but-steady: revenue ₹72,275 crore, up 13.9% on the year and 2.2% on the quarter, though only about flat in constant-currency terms. Reported net profit was ₹13,420 crore, up 4.7%; stripping a one-off item it was ₹13,849 crore, up 8.5%. The number that mattered most was the operating margin, and it held at 24.0% — a touch better than the roughly 23.8% the sell-side feared from the annual wage hike, not the deeper cut the bears wanted. Deal wins (total contract value) were $9.5 billion, and the board cleared an interim dividend of ₹12 a share with a 15-July record date. The read-through for the book is on INFY (Infosys, T1): a muted-but-margin-defended TCS validates the frame that the whole IT cohort has been marked down into results on real-but-slow growth. That supports holding INFY into its own 23-July result, not adding on the print — the market's reaction tomorrow is a next-session input, and INFY's own number is the arbiter.

### A relief bounce, not a reversal — oil stopped rising and the fear drained out
The single most important thing that happened was a thing that did not happen: Brent held flat at $78.21 instead of extending Wednesday's near-6% spike. With crude paused and Trump saying he does not believe the US and Iran return to full-scale war, the acute panic drained straight out — India VIX fell 8.97% to 13.36, back near its one-month low, and the Nifty clawed back part of the loss to close up 0.34%. But this is repair, not resolution. The oil premium did not unwind; Brent is still up more than 9% on the week and the Hormuz overhang is unresolved, so India's oil-importer vulnerability is paused, not fixed. The honest framing is lower panic, unchanged overhang — and the Nifty is still down 0.88% on the week even after the bounce.

### Banks led back, IT and pharma split, and the day's clean faller was DRREDDY
Beneath the index the recovery was breadth-led and cleanly reflexive. The Bank Nifty led the bounce (+0.90%), a mirror image of leading Wednesday's fall, and the rate-sensitive corners ran hardest — real estate extended a fifth up-session (Nifty Realty +18.6% on the month, at a 52-week high) on easing US-rate expectations, and the jewellery cluster ripped, led by Kalyan Jewellers (KALYANKJIL, T2) up 18.4%. IT was the lone laggard as the cohort de-risked into TCS. The one clean, sourced faller was Dr Reddy's Laboratories (DRREDDY, not held), down 5.89% against a rising pharma index, after it disclosed that certain semaglutide batches were found out-of-specification on an active-ingredient issue and commercial supply will be delayed — a real dent on a flagship generic-diabetes product, not a market-wide signal.
