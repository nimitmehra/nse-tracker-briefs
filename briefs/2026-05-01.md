## What Happened

### IDFC First's Haryana fraud expands; auditor outranks the press release

IDFC First Bank (IDFCFIRSTB), the private-sector lender that emerged from the 2018 merger of IDFC and Capital First, filed its Q4FY26 board outcome to NSE on Friday, 25 April in a Reg 30 disclosure — the Indian counterpart to a material-event filing — with an investor presentation, a press release and the analyst-call recording broadcast on 30 April. The bank's own communications carried the framing they have used since the news broke — *"isolated branch event"*, *"normalised PAT plus 145 percent"* — but the auditor's Emphasis of Matter on Note 15, attached to the same filing, names a different story: multi-customer, multi-employee external collusion at the Chandigarh branch, the customer identified through a closure-and-transfer reconciliation as a Haryana Government department, exposure ₹645.59 crore, post-tax hit ₹483 crore. Reporting through the week from Tribune India and Business Standard has widened the operational scope to *"more than 200 high-value transactions"* across IDFC First and AU Small Finance Bank, with three Haryana state-government servants among five accused, a three-day Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) custodial-interrogation order issued on 27 April, and a formal Article 311(2)(b) dismissal of a Department of Disposal and Procurement superintendent. The auditor's qualification, the CBI custody order and the state-government dismissal are the primary-source artefacts; the IR voice is the framing. The next signals are deposit-flight evidence in the Q1FY27 monthly disclosures, any rating-agency commentary on internal controls from CRISIL, ICRA or India Ratings, and whether the Reserve Bank of India's monthly Bulletin breaks its silence on the episode. [CONFIRMED — Reg 30 disclosure to NSE, 25 April; auditor's report attached; Tribune India and Business Standard, 27–30 April]

### Waaree Energies' Q4 beat; the stock fell 11 percent anyway

Waaree Energies (WAAREEENER), the listed parent of T1 portfolio holding Waaree Renewable Technologies (WAAREERTL), reported Q4FY26 results on Wednesday evening that were operationally exceptional — revenue ₹8,480 crore, up 112 percent year on year; profit ₹1,061 to ₹1,126 crore across Business Standard, Upstox and *pv magazine India* framings, up 75 percent; full-year FY26 profit ₹3,884 crore, up 101 percent — and the stock fell 10.97 percent on Thursday, the largest single-day decline in the entire T2 cohort. Three things were priced into the drop: a ₹10,000 crore qualified-institutional placement approved by the board, which is the supply-dilution overhang the 24 April JPMorgan India downgrade had explicitly cited; the US Department of Commerce's preliminary 123.04 percent anti-dumping duty on Indian solar cells and modules of 24 April, with Waaree Energies named at a 123.07 percent margin and the final determination now formally calendared for 13 July; and the Hormuz-driven capital-cost pressure that has compressed rate-sensitive renewables for a fortnight. Portfolio holding Waaree Renewable, which is a domestic-only EPC and does not carry direct US ADD revenue exposure, fell a further 2.26 percent on perception-driven contagion rather than fundamentals — its own Q4 print on 24 April had revenue ₹1,102 crore, up 131 percent. The elevated regulatory-action trigger on the WAAREERTL node currently carries an expected_date of 8 July; the procedural revision to 13 July is queued for the next dedicated /update-graph-nse run. The next signal is whether parent Waaree Energies follows the QIP route in a pricing window that compresses parent and sister together. [CONFIRMED — Reg 30 disclosure to NSE, 29 April board outcome and 30 April investor presentation; *pv magazine India*, 27 April; US DOC release, 24 April]
