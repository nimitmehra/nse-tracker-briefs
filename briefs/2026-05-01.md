## What Happened

### Federal Reserve holds; rare 8-4 dissent reframes the FII-outflow regime

The Federal Reserve held the federal-funds target at 3.75 to 4.00 percent on Wednesday, 29 April, with **four members dissenting** — the first such split since October 1992. Stephen Miran dissented in favour of a 25-basis-point cut; Beth Hammack, Neel Kashkari and Lorie Logan dissented against an easing bias. Jerome Powell, the chair since 2018 whose chairmanship runs to mid-May 2026, signalled he will remain on the Board of Governors past his chair term. For Indian markets, a structurally divided Federal Open Market Committee — the Fed's twelve-member rate-setting body — sustains higher-for-longer US yields, dollar strength, and the foreign-institutional-investor outflow regime that has now drained ₹1.92 lakh crore CYTD, exceeding the entire CY2025 figure of ₹1.66 lakh crore in four months. The next signal is the June Reserve Bank of India Monetary Policy Committee meeting — the RBI's six-member rate-setting body — for which Goldman Sachs has pencilled 50 basis points of hikes through CY26 against JPMorgan's neutral view. [CONFIRMED — Federal Reserve press release, 29 April; NSDL FPI data; Business Standard, 29–30 April]

### IDFC First's Haryana fraud expands; auditor outranks the press release

IDFC First Bank (IDFCFIRSTB), the private-sector lender that emerged from the 2018 merger of IDFC and Capital First, filed its Q4FY26 board outcome to NSE on Saturday, 25 April in a Reg 30 disclosure — the Indian counterpart to a material-event filing — with the analyst-call recording broadcast on 30 April. The bank's communications stayed with the framing they have used since the news broke — *"isolated branch event"*, *"normalised PAT plus 145 percent"* — but the auditor's Emphasis of Matter on Note 15, attached to the same filing, names a different story: multi-customer, multi-employee external collusion at the Chandigarh branch, the customer identified through closure-and-transfer reconciliation as a Haryana Government department, exposure ₹645.59 crore, post-tax hit ₹483 crore. Reporting from Tribune India and Business Standard widened the scope to *"more than 200 high-value transactions"* across IDFC First and AU Small Finance Bank, with three Haryana state-government servants among five accused, a three-day Central Bureau of Investigation custodial-interrogation order on 27 April, and a formal Article 311(2)(b) dismissal — the constitutional provision permitting dismissal of a public servant without inquiry on expedient public-interest grounds — of a Department of Disposal and Procurement superintendent. The auditor's qualification, the CBI custody order and the dismissal are the primary-source artefacts; the IR voice is the framing. Next signals: deposit-flight in Q1FY27 monthly disclosures, rating-agency commentary on internal controls, and the RBI Monthly Bulletin. [CONFIRMED — Reg 30 disclosure to NSE, 25 April; auditor's report attached; Tribune India and Business Standard, 27–30 April]

### Waaree Energies' Q4 beat; the stock fell 11 percent anyway

Waaree Energies (WAAREEENER), the listed parent of T1 portfolio holding Waaree Renewable Technologies (WAAREERTL), reported Q4FY26 results on Wednesday, 29 April that were operationally exceptional — revenue ₹8,480 crore, up 112 percent year on year; profit ₹1,061–1,126 crore, up 75 percent; FY26 profit ₹3,884 crore, up 101 percent — and the stock fell 10.97 percent on Thursday, the T2 cohort's largest single-day decline. Three things were priced into the drop: a ₹10,000 crore qualified-institutional placement approved by the board, the supply-dilution overhang JPMorgan's 24 April downgrade had flagged; the US Department of Commerce's preliminary 123.04 percent anti-dumping duty on Indian solar cells and modules (Waaree Energies at 123.07 percent), with the final determination calendared for 13 July; and Hormuz-driven capital-cost pressure on rate-sensitive renewables. Portfolio holding Waaree Renewable, a domestic-only EPC with no direct US ADD revenue exposure, fell a further 2.26 percent on perception-driven contagion — its own 24 April Q4 print had revenue ₹1,102 crore, up 131 percent. The elevated regulatory-action trigger on the WAAREERTL node carries expected_date 8 July; the procedural revision to 13 July is queued for the next dedicated /update-graph-nse run. The next signal is whether the parent follows the QIP route in a pricing window that compresses parent and sister together. [CONFIRMED — Reg 30 disclosure to NSE, 29 April board outcome and 30 April investor presentation; *pv magazine India*, 27 April; US DOC release, 24 April]

### Maruti's record FY26 and Eternal's Blinkit inflection — what the non-portfolio tape did

Two non-portfolio Q4 prints on Tuesday, 28 April recalibrated the consumption-cycle read. **Maruti Suzuki India (MARUTI)**, the country's largest passenger-vehicle maker and the listed Indian arm of Suzuki Motor of Japan, filed FY26 full-year results that were the highest in its history — total revenue ₹1.74 lakh crore, up 20.2 percent; profit ₹14,415 crore; volumes 24.23 lakh units across approximately 19.7 lakh domestic and 4.48 lakh export units[^1] — even as the Q4 print showed PAT down 6.4 to 6.9 percent YoY to ₹3,590–3,659 crore on a clean mark-to-market hit (Q4 EBIT up 30.4 percent to ₹4,409 crore confirms the softness is treasury-side). The board declared a final dividend of ₹140 per share, its largest. **Eternal (ETERNAL)**, the Zomato + Blinkit holding company re-listed under the new name in 2024, reported Q4FY26 PAT up 346 percent to ₹174 crore on revenue up 196 percent to ₹17,292 crore, with the Blinkit quick-commerce arm posting a first-time positive adjusted EBITDA of ₹37 crore against a year-ago loss of ₹178 crore, 216 net-new stores added and 109 million transacting Indians on the platform in FY26. **Bajaj Finance (BAJFINANCE)** standalone PAT rose 23 percent to ₹4,839 crore on Wednesday, 29 April; **Bajaj Finserv (BAJAJFINSV)** consolidated PAT rose 5 percent to ₹2,538 crore. The auto-ancillary cohort (SJS, PRICOL, CEAT) carries the Maruti volume signal; the Blinkit inflection re-prices the retail-disruption thesis bears had been disputing; and the Bajaj prints set the NBFC growth bar against which any IDFC First read-through will compete next quarter. The next signal is Royal Enfield-parent Eicher Motors's monthly volume read on 1 May and the 6 May Blue Star Q4 board outcome. [CONFIRMED — Reg 30 disclosures to NSE, 28–29 April; Business Standard and BusinessToday, 28–30 April]

Blue Star (BLUESTARCO), the Mumbai-based room-air-conditioner and commercial-refrigeration maker, filed three director-resignation disclosures in NSE's category for changes to key managerial personnel within a roughly two-hour window on Thursday evening, 30 April — broadcast at 18:07, 18:16 and 18:21 IST — and the stock fell 3.06 percent on the same day's tape against a Nifty Consumption index down 0.89, the move arriving before formal Q4FY26 results rescheduled to a 6 May board meeting and a 7 May analyst call.
