## The Lead

The most important thing that happened this weekend was not on the stock screen — it was at the source of the petrol pump. The Iran crisis that had markets nervous on Friday resolved: the **Strait of Hormuz reopened** (the US lifted its naval blockade and stranded oil tankers began moving again), and over the weekend the US and Iran, meeting in Switzerland, agreed a **60-day roadmap toward a final deal** — including a commitment to keep Hormuz open, nuclear inspectors back at Iran's sites, and a US waiver letting Iran sell its oil again for 60 days.

Why this matters for every Indian, not just investors: **India buys about 85% of its oil from abroad.** When a war scare threatens the world's most important oil chokepoint, the price spikes and *everything* imported gets costlier — fuel, transport, the rupee, inflation. When that scare lifts, it all eases. And it did: **Brent crude fell about 11% over the week to ~$78 a barrel** (US WTI −13%). That is the single biggest tailwind an oil-importing economy can get — a lighter import bill, softer inflation, and support for the rupee, all at once.

On that relief, the market bounced back from Friday's IT-driven fall. The Nifty added 0.37% to reclaim 24,100. **But be honest about the size of it:** this was a *shallow* rebound, not a full recovery — the Nifty is up only about 1% on the week, and IT only partly clawed back Friday's crash. The relief is real; the all-clear is not.

One caveat worth keeping: this is a *roadmap*, not a signed peace. President Trump threatened fresh strikes even during the talks, and the fighting in Lebanon didn't fully stop. So treat cheaper oil as a 60-day window, not a permanent gift.

## Global Setup

The backdrop was a clean risk-on shift, and crude was the centre of it. The weekend's Iran resolution drained the war premium out of oil — **Brent ~$77.75 (−11% on the week), WTI ~$73.59 (−13%)** — which is the dominant global signal for India. Around it, the tone was constructive: Wall Street firmed into the weekend, Asian markets opened higher, and **gold eased to ~$4,199** as money trimmed the safe-haven trade it had piled into during the conflict. The one-line read for India: for once the global thread — cheaper oil and easing geopolitics — ran entirely in India's favour, the mirror image of Friday, when the global thread (Accenture's software warning) ran against it.

## Macro & Policy

Two supportive cross-currents and one open risk.

**Supportive — crude.** The 60-day US waiver on Iranian oil sales plus the Hormuz reopening mean more supply and a lower price. For an 85%-importer that is the cleanest macro positive there is: it eases the import bill, the current-account deficit, imported inflation, the rupee and — through all of those — the pressure on bond yields. It is the single most consequential thing in this brief.

**Supportive — the RBI's plumbing is working.** The Reserve Bank is deploying its ~$700bn reserves to slow the rupee's slide, and foreign investors have poured **over $2.2bn into Indian government bonds in roughly the last ten sessions** following the RBI's 5-June package to encourage dollar inflows. Cheaper oil plus that bond inflow is a genuinely constructive macro floor under the equity rebound.

**The open risk — the monsoon.** June has been the **driest in over a century — a 46% rain deficit nationally** (Maharashtra −85%, Gujarat −84%, central India worst-hit), threatening the kharif sowing window. The one relief: the monsoon **revived** this week after a two-week stall, with Arabian Sea moisture reaching Mumbai and the weather office expecting it to spread over the next 48 hours. Whether July catches up is the swing factor for farm incomes and agri-input demand.

**Watch next:** a four-session week (markets shut Friday 26-June for Muharram) with the monthly derivatives expiry on Thursday 25.

## Corporate News

- **Reliance / Jio Platforms (RELIANCE)** — Reliance rallied (~1.6%, brokerages reiterating buy — Motilal Oswal target ₹1,625) as investors digested its annual-meeting news, headlined by the **Jio IPO** filing: India's largest-ever, an all-fresh issue of around ₹38,000 crore, with the bulk earmarked to repay Jio's debt rather than cash out promoters. ## The Connections

*How the day's events ripple — the part most market reports skip.*

**Don't read the foreign-buying number as "foreigners are back."** Foreign investors did buy ₹4,859 crore of shares on Monday — but a meaningful slice was *mechanical*: a scheduled reshuffle of the FTSE India index took effect today (estimated >$600m of passive inflow), which forces index funds to buy certain stocks regardless of the news. So the honest read is: a calendar event *plus* a genuine oil-relief lifted a thin market — not a flood of conviction money returning. The bigger picture is still a record ₹2.2 lakh crore of foreign money that has *left* India this year; what matters is whether buying continues once this week's index reshuffle clears.

**The IT "recovery" is a clue, not a comfort.** If Friday's crash had been an over-reaction, IT would have round-tripped most of it on Monday. It didn't — it recovered only +0.74% on the index and is still red on the week. That tells you the market hasn't dismissed Accenture's warning that companies are *shifting* their tech budgets to AI rather than *growing* them. The worry about the size of the spending pie is still there; the real test is Indian IT's own July-quarter commentary.

**Cheaper oil is a tailwind with a clock on it.** The relief is tied to a 60-day waiver and a roadmap that can slip (Trump threatened fresh strikes mid-summit; Lebanon fighting persisted). For the firms that *buy* oil — paints, tyres, airlines, beverages — and for the rupee and inflation, this is a real cost easing; but it is a window to use, not a regime change to bank on. Watch the headlines on the Iran roadmap as closely as the price.
