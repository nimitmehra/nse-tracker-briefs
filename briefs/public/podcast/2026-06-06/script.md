Good morning. This is India Markets Brief from toroIQ. Your read on Friday's session.

The biggest thing India did on Friday was change a tax rule. The government scrapped the taxes that foreign investors pay on Indian government bonds, a move designed to pull overseas money into Indian debt. The rupee firmed almost one percent on the expectation of those inflows, even as the dollar was rising everywhere else. And it all landed on a brutal global day, with a chip and artificial-intelligence sell-off tearing through Asia. India barely flinched, because it owns very little of that chip cycle.

Here is where the market closed on Friday. The Nifty fifty finished at twenty-three thousand three hundred and sixty-six, down a slim zero point two one percent. Banks were the bright spot, with Bank Nifty up about a third of a percent, because that foreign-money package reads straight through to lenders. Information technology was the soft corner, down about one percent, a sympathy move with the chip sell-off in Asia, where Korea fell five and a half percent. But Indian IT sells software services, not chips, so the damage stayed small. Metals were the worst sector, down one point six percent, and that was a price story, not a demand story. A stronger dollar crushed gold, silver and copper worldwide, and Indian metal stocks just track those prices.

Here is the signal underneath the flat close. The rupee firmed even as the dollar rose against the euro and the pound. That is unusual, and it was India-specific. The bond market agreed, with yields falling sharply, so this was a bet on real foreign money coming in, not a global flow.

Now the two policy events behind that bet. On the supportive side, growth came in hot, at seven point eight percent in the March quarter, well above the seven point two that was expected. On the cautious side, the Reserve Bank of India held interest rates steady. But it raised its inflation forecast and trimmed its growth forecast, flagging a weak monsoon and high energy prices. So a rate cut is not coming soon.

Now the movers. The clean winner was Paras Defence, up seven point four percent to a one-year high. The company makes optical and electronic gear for defence. On Friday it won a fifty-three crore rupee order from Bharat Electronics, on top of a strong quarter where profit nearly doubled. A hot United States jobs report arrived at six pm Indian time, the market gave up on rate cuts, bond yields jumped, and the Nasdaq fell more than four percent. Indian shares never got to trade any of that. So Friday only proves India held up against the Asian move. Whether it shrugs off the bigger American move is Monday's question, not Friday's.
