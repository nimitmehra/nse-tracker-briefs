## Description

Indian shares fell for a third straight day on Wednesday, but this time the fear was visible. The Nifty slipped below 24,000 to 23,996 (down 0.79%) while the fear gauge, India VIX, rose 5.5% for the first time on a down day in this slide. The trigger was oil: an eleventh night of US-Iran strikes and a Hormuz tanker attack pushed Brent toward $95, a straight cost hit for a country that imports 85% of its oil, with an unsigned US tariff deadline looming Friday. Inside the market, a lesson: Bandhan Bank fell 17% despite a 35% profit jump on a forward guidance cut, while M&M Financial rose 8% on a 75% profit jump and its best asset quality in eight years.
