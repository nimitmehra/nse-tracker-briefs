## Description

India closed up half a percent on Tuesday, and the lift was made entirely at home. A new RBI window that pulls foreign-currency deposits into banks went live, cutting what banks pay for funding, so the public-sector bank index rose three point six percent and financials did all the index lifting. Abroad, North Asia's chip stocks ripped, a trade India barely owns, so India sat out the rally and made its own gain. Foreign investors kept selling, the rupee slipped, and domestic funds did the buying. Today's brief: why one policy lever moved a whole sector, and what Friday's inflation print will tell us.

## Key points

- The Nifty 50 closed at 23,242, up 0.52%, with almost all the gain from financials; PSU Bank +3.62%, Bank Nifty +2.09%. IT was the only red sector, down 0.48%.
- The RBI's FCNR-B deposit window went live: the central bank absorbs the full hedging cost on fresh 3-to-5-year non-resident deposits, lowering banks' funding cost. It sits on top of the June 5 bond-tax ordinance; together they pushed the 10-year yield to a four-week low of 6.90%.
- India decoupled from the Asia chip rally (Korea +8.18%, Taiwan +2.76%, Japan +2.17%) because its tech weight is software services, not chips. FIIs sold ₹4,566 cr; DIIs bought ₹6,159 cr.
- Movers: JNK India +15.70% on an Abu Dhabi gas-project order; a broad defence rebound led by Data Patterns (+10.07%) and Paras Defence (+7.71%); L&T Technology Services +7.98% on a unit sale, against a red IT sector.
- The Connections read: a funding-cost subsidy helps the lenders that actually use NRI dollars; affordable-housing financiers, which do not, sat the rally out.
- Watch Friday June 12: India May CPI (~4 PM IST) and the RBI weekly forex-reserves print (~5 PM IST). Last night's firm US close is a Wednesday input, not a driver of Tuesday.
