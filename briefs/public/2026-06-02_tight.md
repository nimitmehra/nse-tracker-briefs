## The Connections

*The cross-sector reads — what triggered, why, which sectors next.*

**The pharma non-move is the most diagnostic read of the session.** A $700 million upfront on a bispecific-antibody out-licensing deal is structurally the largest cohort-level innovation event of 2026 for Indian pharma. Monday's −3.63% Glenmark extension and the Pharma index continuing Friday's −1.50% drag tells you foreign capital is still valuing Dr Reddy's, Sun Pharma, Lupin, Cipla, Aurobindo and Glenmark as generic-formulations — not as discovery/biosimilar, even with a landmark deal on the wire. For an investor in any Indian pharma name, this is the single most important data point from Monday: the innovation premium is not being awarded.

**The IT bid was an AI catalyst, not a rupee story.** Persistent +5.18%, Infosys +4.29%, Coforge +4.18%, TCS +4.0% — that level of cohort lift on a day the broader market fell 0.70% needs a real catalyst, and the catalyst was Snowflake's overnight result reinforcing the global enterprise-AI-spend thesis. A weaker rupee HELPS IT (they earn dollars, spend rupees), so Monday's currency move added to the AI bid rather than fighting it — but the AI bid was the lead. Worth being precise on direction because the inverse is a common misframe.

**Cheaper crude relief is not flowing to OMCs.** Brent bounced 3% Monday but is still −17% over May — the sharpest monthly drop since 2020. By textbook math, BPCL, HPCL, IOC should have rallied across the month on falling input costs. They have not. The reason is the petrol/diesel hikes of ~₹5/litre since mid-May — government is keeping retail prices firm while crude has fallen, so the margin relief is collected as excise, not flowing to OMC P&L. For OMC investors, regulator-capped marketing margins are the binding constraint, not the crude price. (The structural FPI outflow story — CYTD ₹2.25 lakh crore — remains operative as backdrop; Monday's specific India-vs-EM divergence was domestic-driven, not capital-reallocation.)
