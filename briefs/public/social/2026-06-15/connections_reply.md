A few cross-currents from Monday worth sitting with.

The oil companies themselves did not rise the way cheaper crude suggests they should have. Energy was nearly flat while the broad market jumped. For the state oil sellers the crude-down read cuts both ways, because lower input cost is offset by thinner marketing margins and weaker prices at the upstream producers. The two roughly cancel. The non-move is the signal: the relief lands on the importer's inflation math, not on the oil sector's profits.

The other thread is the rupee. It climbed about 1 percent even though the dollar barely moved, so this was an India-specific bid built on the oil story, not a weak-dollar move. That normally hurts software exporters, who earn in dollars and spend in rupees, yet IT closed in line with the market. The likely reason is an offset, since global technology was strong overnight and supplied a bid that roughly cancelled the currency drag.
