The Nifty rose about 1 percent to 23,854 on Monday, with risers beating fallers roughly four to one. The trigger sat outside India: the United States and Iran signed a deal over the weekend to end their war and reopen the Strait of Hormuz, the world's busiest oil lane.

That sent Brent crude down about 5 percent to a three-month low near $83. Cheaper oil matters directly for India, which imports about 85 percent of its crude, because it eases the import bill and fuel-driven inflation at once. That widens the room the Reserve Bank has to keep cutting rates, so the money moved into the sectors that gain most from lower rates. Property led at +3.96 percent and autos followed at +2.60 percent, while pharma was the only sector to fall, as defensive names were sold to fund the rate-sensitive bet.

Aarti Industries rose 13 percent on a run of long-dated contract wins, with cheaper crude an added feedstock relief for a chemicals maker. Balkrishna Industries gained 9 percent as the de-escalation reverses the US tariffs that had halted its tyre exports.

One caution holds it back. The deal is signed but not yet working, with Hormuz mine-clearance put at 40 to 50 days, which is why Brent still sits about $16 above its pre-war level. The next test is the Federal Reserve's rate decision Wednesday and the formal Geneva ceremony on Friday.
