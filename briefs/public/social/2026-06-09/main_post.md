India's market went up today because of one decision taken at home.

The Reserve Bank opened a window for banks to raise foreign-currency deposits from Indians living abroad, and agreed to absorb the cost of hedging the rupee on them. That lowers what banks pay to bring in dollars, so it landed straight on the banks.

Bank Nifty rose 2.1 percent. The state-owned bank index rose 3.6 percent, all twelve names green.

The wider Nifty added just half a percent to 23,242, a thin bank-led green rather than broad buying.

This sits on top of last week's ordinance that scrapped the taxes foreign investors pay on Indian government bonds. The 10-year yield fell to a four-week low of 6.90 percent.

The one red corner was IT, down half a percent. Today's big rally was in Korean and Taiwanese chip stocks, and India's tech is services, not chips.

JNK India rose 15.7 percent, the top mover, on a waste-gas order for an Abu Dhabi project.

On Friday, India's May inflation arrives, the first read since this policy package.
