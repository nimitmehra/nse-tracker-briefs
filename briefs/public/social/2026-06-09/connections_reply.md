A few cross-currents from today worth sitting with.

One policy lever moved an entire sector, and how it spread tells you something. The deposit scheme cuts what banks pay to raise money abroad, so the market repriced every deposit-taker at once, thirteen of fourteen big lenders green. But the help is specific. Affordable-housing financiers do not fund themselves with deposits from Indians abroad, and they sat out the move while banks ran. A funding subsidy only helps the lenders that actually use that funding.

The bigger picture is that today's gain was home-made, and that is the point. Across Asia, money poured into chip stocks. India put up half a percent without any of that flow, because foreign investors were still net sellers and the rupee weakened on its own. The buying came from domestic funds and a domestic policy lever.

The question for the weeks ahead is whether the new bond and deposit rules start turning that foreign selling. That flow, not the chip rally, is what would lift India broadly.
