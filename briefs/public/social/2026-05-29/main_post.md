The news was good but the market fell. Oil crashed 20% this month and the rupee gained 1% on US-Iran de-escalation hopes. Both of these are good news for India but the Nifty dropped 1.5% anyway. The possible reason was the ceasefire is still unsigned, but more importantly the weather office cut its monsoon forecast to 90% of normal, reviving food-price fears.

In the broader market there was a sell-off. Nearly every index was in the red except for IT, which closed up 0.6%. While a stronger rupee hurts IT exporters, the green close might be a flight to safety on a risk-off day, not a currency story.

In the currency market the rupee jumped 1.1%. The broad dollar index was unmoved, hence this was an India-specific move. Governor Malhotra had said the RBI will do whatever is required and that the rupee appears undervalued.

On the earnings front, Asian Paints had the standout result with profit up 69% to ₹1,172 crore, but the stock ended flat on the broad sell-off. IndiGo swung to a ₹2,537 crore loss from a ₹3,067 crore profit a year ago and the stock fell 3.6%.

In corporate news, Solar Industries won a ₹1,076 crore defence-export order. It was the strongest piece of company news of the day.

The big moves up were three names. Netweb Technologies +14.7% to a record high on a ₹1,734 crore AI-server order, with its AI segment now 43% of sales, up from 15%. India Nippon Electricals +12.8% on a strong Q4 with profit up 48%. Shaily Engineering +12.3% as it scales injector-pen capacity to ride the global GLP-1 wave. On the other side, Natco Pharma fell 13.6% on a weak quarter.

Looking at next week, the RBI rate decision on June 3-5 is the big one. A hold is expected but the real signal would be whether the oil crash softens their hawkish tone. The India-US trade round on June 1-4 is the other one to watch.
