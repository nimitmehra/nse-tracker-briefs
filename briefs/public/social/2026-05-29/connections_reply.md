A few cross-currents worth thinking about from today.

The oil crash helps India but hurts the oil-shippers. Great Eastern Shipping fell 6% for a third straight session despite a record quarter. The reason is that the market is pricing in falling future freight rates from the cheaper crude, not the past results.

For fertiliser makers, the monsoon cut and the oil crash pull in opposite directions. Cheaper oil and a Cabinet subsidy hike ease their input costs, but the monsoon cut to 90% threatens demand. Weak rain means farmers buy fewer inputs. Coromandel fell to a 52-week low.

IT was the day's hideout but for defensive reasons, not a currency tailwind. A stronger rupee actually hurts IT (they earn in dollars and spend in rupees), so the green close was pure flight-to-safety on a risk-off day. The deeper worry on IT is unchanged, which is that AI permanently shrinks the work these firms get paid for.
