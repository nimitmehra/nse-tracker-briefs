Foreign investors turned buyers of Indian shares again, and it matters more than Monday's 0.66% gain. After pulling ₹49,340 cr out in June, they bought a net ₹708 cr across July's first three sessions, a fourth straight up day.

Rate-cut hope led it: real estate hit a six-month high on cheaper money. IT stayed red at fresh 52-week lows despite a weaker rupee on the week, a fundamental problem, not currency.
