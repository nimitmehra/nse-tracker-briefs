A few cross-currents from Monday worth thinking about.

The rupee is soft, but not because the central bank ran out of dollars. Last week's headlines flagged forex reserves falling $5.65 billion and read it as heavy intervention. It was almost all gold losing value on the books; the actual dollar assets barely moved, down about $150 million. So the currency's weakness is June's selling still working through, not the RBI spending its defence down. Friday's weekly reserves update is worth reading for the dollar-asset line, not the gold-inflated headline.

Real estate leading is the cleanest read on rate-cut hope. Cheaper crude, softer yields, an RBI holding rates, and a jump in institutional property money all point the same way. But the fear gauge sat near a low 11.8. When a leadership move happens on this little conviction, it is positioning ahead of earnings, not a crowd piling in. The results will decide if it sticks.
