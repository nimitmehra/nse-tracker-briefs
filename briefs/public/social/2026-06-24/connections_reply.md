A few cross-currents worth thinking about from Tuesday.

Don't read the fall as domestic panic: foreign investors were roughly flat in the cash market, and domestic institutions actually net-bought about ₹680 crore, the classic cushion that absorbs a foreign-driven sell-off, so this was an imported scare, not a vote against India.

The biggest moves split cleanly down the imported-versus-domestic line: the fallers were all global-beta, IT on the chip unwind and metals on a base-metals sell-off, while the gainers clustered in one domestic story, pharma, which tells you what was being sold and what was being sought.

Pharma's strength had several legs, which is the tell worth keeping: a real drug-shortage order, a defensive rotation out of cyclicals, and a weaker rupee that mechanically lifts overseas earnings, so it was more than a simple flight to safety.

The lesson for days like this: look abroad for the trigger and watch the global semiconductor tape, because the bounce-back, if it comes, will arrive from there and not from anything happening in India.
