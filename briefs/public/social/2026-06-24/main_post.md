The Nifty fell 1.16% to 23,824 on Tuesday, giving back Monday's bounce and more, but the trigger came from abroad, not from home.

The world's most crowded trade, AI semiconductors, de-risked overnight: South Korea's market fell 8-10% and tripped its circuit breaker twice, Japan fell 3.2%. When a position that size unwinds, foreign funds sell risk everywhere.

India has no large listed chipmakers, yet it was dragged along, the most foreign-sensitive corners first: metals fell 3.2% and IT 2.2%, the two worst sectors.

Here is the puzzle worth holding onto: India's own setup was fine, oil at a three-month low, a calm bond market, business activity still growing, and it fell anyway. For one day, the global tape beats local news.

Pharma was the only green, up about 1.8% to a record, on a real US-FDA cancer-drug shortage that sent the regulator to Indian suppliers, with a weaker rupee lifting their overseas earnings on top.

The biggest single fall was Vedanta, down about 8%, not on results but on supply: its promoter sold a ₹2,149 crore block of shares.

It reverses with the global tape, not with anything Indian, into a four-session week with Thursday's expiry before the Friday Muharram holiday.
