One cross-current worth holding onto into Monday.

Amid the weekend's bad news there was one small relief. US bond yields eased on Friday and the dollar softened, which on a normal day would support the rate-sensitive lenders that ran hard on Thursday. Repco Home Finance, Fusion Finance and M&M Financial all rallied that session on exactly that falling-yield bid.

But that relief is the smallest force on the board. It is a one-week move on an in-line inflation print, not a turn in the rate path, and it is modest against a live oil shock.

So those lenders become a useful gauge. If they hold their Thursday gains on Monday, the yield relief is doing its job. But if they give it back, that tells you the oil-and-rupee worry is overwhelming the relief, and it would be the cleanest read on which force is actually winning the open.
