On Thursday the Indian market made cheaper oil its single biggest bet. The Nifty barely moved, but underneath it the auto pack ran hard. Nifty Auto rose 2.25% and the airline IndiGo jumped 4.66%, all on the logic that cheaper fuel lifts demand and cuts running costs.

Over the long weekend, while India was shut, that exact bet came under threat.

The US-Iran ceasefire collapsed at the Strait of Hormuz. A tanker carrying two million barrels was hit, the US struck Iran, and Iran fired back at US bases in Kuwait and Bahrain.

So the cheap oil that powered Thursday's rally may reverse when India reopens on Monday. An oil spike would hurt the very autos and airlines that led, and it would weigh on the rupee.

And it does not arrive alone. A weekend selloff in global technology pressures Indian IT at the same time.

Both forces push the same way. Monday opens nervous, with oil the swing variable. Brent closed Friday at 71.99 dollars, before the strikes, so no settled level exists yet.
