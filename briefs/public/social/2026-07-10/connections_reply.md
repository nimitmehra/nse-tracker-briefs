A few cross-currents from Friday worth thinking about.

Friday's rise was domestic money, and that corrects the week's story. The narrative all week has been foreign investors returning to India after a huge exodus, and over several weeks that is genuinely true, but Friday itself does not support it, because foreign investors were net sellers of about ₹533 crore while Indian institutions bought ₹2,058 crore and carried the market up. The multi-week return is real but not linear, so a home-led up day is not proof that foreign money drove it.

And cheaper oil still isn't reaching refiners the way you might expect. Mangalore Refinery rose 8.56%, but not because crude fell, since Brent was flat on the day. What the market was really pricing is better refining spreads, the gap between crude cost and fuel selling prices, which is a margin story rather than a cheap-oil one. It is a reminder that oil down and refiners up are not the same trade.
