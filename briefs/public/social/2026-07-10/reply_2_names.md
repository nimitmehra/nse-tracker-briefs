A few names worth knowing from Friday.

The state-run banks opened the season strongly. Indian Bank's Q1 profit rose 10% to ₹3,273 crore with bad loans nearly halving to 1.86% from 3.01%, and the stock jumped 9.78%, while Bank of Maharashtra's profit rose 27% to ₹2,020 crore and it added 2.73%.

The one weak result was Elecon Engineering, where first-quarter profit fell about 60% to ₹70 crore as margins were crushed from 26.6% to 21%, but the stock fell around 6% intraday and then recovered almost all of it to close roughly flat as the risk-on session absorbed the miss.

Among the biggest gainers, Godrej Industries rose 16.28% on holding-company value-unlock chatter with no filing to confirm it, Zensar Technologies climbed 13.6% as the IT rebound met heavy short-covering, and Greaves Cotton added 11.12% after the rights issue of its electric-vehicle arm was fully subscribed.
