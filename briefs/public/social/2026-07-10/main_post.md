Indian shares rose a second straight session Friday, the Nifty up 1% to 24,207, as a strong earnings-season start pulled money back into beaten-down corners. Five things to know:

- The sold-off corners led back: IT flipped from Thursday's only red sector to Friday's leader at +1.96%, property was best at +3.49%, and bank results lifted the Bank Nifty 1.39%.

- The trigger was TCS's June-quarter numbers the night before, read as demand soft but not broken, setting a confident tone for software, banks and property.

- The fear gauge fell 8.3% to 12.25, near a one-year low, but the caveat is it was home-grown buying: local institutions net-bought ₹2,058 crore against small foreign selling.

- After the close, Reserve Bank data showed foreign-exchange reserves rose $7.26 billion to $674 billion, a sign of reserve building, not a rupee defence.

- Looking ahead, two clocks sit under the calm: the monsoon is now forecast below normal, under 94% of average, and the temporary 10% US tariff on Indian goods expires 24 July, unsigned.
