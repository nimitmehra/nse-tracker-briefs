India and the United States are close to an interim trade deal, and Indian shares rose on the hope of it on Wednesday. The Commerce Minister called the talks "very close" and the US ambassador said only about 1% is left, but nothing is signed, and the temporary 10% US tariff on Indian goods expires on 24 July.

That optimism, plus cheaper oil and a record June tax haul, pushed the Nifty 50 up 0.59% to 24,005.85 and ended a two-day slide. Domestic funds bought ₹3,159 crore and more than covered a fourth straight day of foreign selling.

Underneath the green, one corner kept falling. The IT index dropped another 2.01% to a fresh three-year low, the day's only red sector, while domestic-facing shares led the market up.

KPIT Technologies was the face of it, down 16.98% to a 52-week low after it warned that June-quarter dollar revenue will fall about 1% on a year earlier. That would be its first revenue decline in 23 quarters, as some European carmakers pulled back after their own profit warnings.

The day's biggest single move went the other way. Paisalo Digital rose 19.86% to a 52-week high after its promoters lifted their holding to about 46.7%, a signal of insider confidence in the small lender rather than an operating update.

After India closed, US bond yields jumped and the big emerging-market equity fund fell 2.32%. That is a Thursday input, not a Wednesday cause. India had already closed green against that weaker backdrop.

The calendar now runs to two dates. US jobs data lands Friday 3 July, and the India-US tariff deadline falls on Thursday 24 July, still unsigned.
