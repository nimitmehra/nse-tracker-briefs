A couple of cross-currents from Wednesday worth thinking about.

Cheap oil stopped being a theory and showed up in a company's own words. Brent is down about 30% over the June quarter, and Hindustan Unilever said its input costs should ease as crude falls. That comment was part of why consumer-goods shares led the market. The same relief flows wider, to paint-makers, tyre-makers and airlines, and it eases the inflation the RBI watches.

Property shares told a second story. They are usually sensitive to interest rates, so the jump in US bond yields after the close should have weighed on them. Instead realty led the market up 3.58%. That makes it a home-grown move, driven by domestic housing demand and the RBI's earlier rate cuts, not the global cost of money.

One thing to carry into Thursday: India closed green while emerging markets fell 2.32% overnight. That is relative strength for now, but if the weakness carries into the next session it turns from a footnote into a live drag.
