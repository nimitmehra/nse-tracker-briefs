A few cross-currents worth thinking about from Thursday.

The market played cheaper oil through the companies that buy fuel, not the ones everyone names first. The textbook winners are the fuel retailers and the paint makers on input-cost relief, yet they did not lead, because for the retailers a falling crude price also eats into inventory gains. The obvious beneficiary is not always the one that moves.

The rate trade was just as selective. A falling bond yield usually helps anything that borrows or lends, but the bid landed in the smaller, higher-beta lenders and skipped the large banks, which reads as a tactical momentum move rather than a broad re-rating.

And the calm hides a dependence worth holding onto. Hong Kong fell about 1.4% during Indian hours, yet the Nifty edged up, held by domestic buying alone. That depth is why India has stayed steady through a rough global stretch, but the floor rests on that buying continuing, so the day it tires is the genuine risk.
