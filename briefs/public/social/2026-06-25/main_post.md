The Nifty barely moved on Thursday, up 0.14% to 24,056, but underneath it the cheap-oil story stopped being a headline and became a clean trade you could see in the sectors.

The auto pack led: the Auto index rose 2.25%, the only sector up more than 1.5%, as Brent sat near four-month lows around 74 dollars and the market read cheaper fuel as lower running costs and stronger demand for vehicle makers. The airline IndiGo rose 4.66% on the same logic.

Samvardhana Motherson, the wiring-harness maker, broke to a fresh 52-week high on that auto bid.

The flip side showed up in ONGC, down 2.87%, because a lower crude price is exactly what shrinks an oil producer's revenue.

A second bid landed in the rate-sensitive lenders as the US 10-year yield eased, with M&M Financial up 5.69% leading a microfinance and housing-finance rally, while the big banks sat flat.

The flat index hid who did the buying: local funds bought 5,748 crore rupees of shares against a near-flat 384 crore from foreigners.
