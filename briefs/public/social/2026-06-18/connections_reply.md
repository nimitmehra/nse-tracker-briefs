A few cross-currents worth thinking about from Wednesday.

A production figure, not a fresh order, moved the whole defence shelf, which is why the index re-rated rather than one stock.

The flip side is that much of this cohort has already run very hard over the past three months, so a lot of that growth is now in the price.

Brent ticking back above $80 is a bounce, not a fresh fall, so the oil-cost relief for paint makers, airlines and oil marketers is old news already priced in.

A firmer rupee normally pressures IT, which earns dollars and spends rupees, but the move was tiny and positioning into tonight's rate decision pulled the sector up anyway.
