A few cross-currents from Thursday worth sitting with.

A weaker rupee should have helped IT, and on Thursday it did not. IT companies earn dollars and spend rupees, so a softer currency normally pads their margins. Yet IT was the worst sector anyway. When a cushion that should soften a fall gets overwhelmed, the worry about US tech demand was the stronger force, and the market was pricing that bigger structural concern rather than the day-to-day math.

The other thread is Friday's inflation print. The May number will reflect May's high fuel costs, so a reading near four percent is on the cards. But it may already be looking backward. Oil rose during Thursday's escalation, then fell sharply after the close, so the same data point can read as an inflation worry for May while pointing gentler for June if the crude relief holds. Better to hold both readings at once than treat one number as the verdict.
