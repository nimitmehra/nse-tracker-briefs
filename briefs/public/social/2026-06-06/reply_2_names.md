Earnings season is over, so Friday's action lived in single stocks. Three are worth knowing.

Hindustan Zinc fell around five percent on reports the government is considering selling part of its stake. The price drop matters less than the run of news. In eight days the company has had its chief's tenure extended only briefly, a search at its offices over a foreign-exchange matter, and now this. Three bad chapters in one week say more than any single one.

Suven Life Sciences fell almost eleven percent, the day's steepest drop. We searched for a cause and found none dated to Friday. It looks like profit-taking after a 77 percent run over three months, and we say that plainly rather than invent a reason.

Paras Defence rose seven percent after winning a fresh order from Bharat Electronics, closing at a one-year high. The cleanest gainer of the day.
