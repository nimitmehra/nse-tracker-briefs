India's June-quarter earnings are separating the strong companies from the weak, and on Friday that pulled the Nifty up 1.09% to 24,334 even as most of Asia fell hard. Five things to know:

- Record results from Federal Bank and beats across software were the two engines, lifting private banks and IT, while pharma was the corner that fell.

- The move was home-grown, not foreign conviction, as Indian institutions bought a net ₹1,018 crore while foreign investors sold a small ₹376 crore.

- India rose over 1% while Japan's Nikkei fell 4% and Korea's KOSPI 3.7%, decoupling upward on its own results, not a regional tide.

- The earnings story cut both ways, as CEAT fell 7.3% after profit dropped 96% on costlier rubber and crude, a reminder the oil premium is back.

- Looking ahead, June inflation ran hot at 4.38% and the temporary 10% US tariff on Indian goods expires next Thursday, unsigned, the largest scheduled event now a week out.
