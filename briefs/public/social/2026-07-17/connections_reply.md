A few cross-currents from Friday worth thinking about.

The oil premium is back, and you see it hit a company's profit. For weeks the market traded a cheaper-oil-helps-India story, but crude has quietly reversed to about $86, up 9% over the month. You do not see it in the refiners, since one day's move is too small, but you see it in a company that buys oil-linked inputs, where revenue can grow 22% while profit falls 96% as rubber and crude costs jump more than 20%. Watch the whole tyre and auto-parts group, where rising crude now shows up as a margin squeeze, not a relief.

India rose while Asia fell, and this time it was India's own story. That is the reverse of late May, when global money was leaving India for North Asia's chip trade. This session India decoupled upward on its own results and its own domestic buyers, against a weak regional market. The signal is that earnings season, not foreign flows, now drives the Indian market.
