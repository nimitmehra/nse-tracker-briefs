The Indian market fell today, and the reason came from outside India.

Iran fired a missile barrage at northern Israel on Sunday night. That pushed crude oil back up, and a nervous, sell-first mood spread across Asia on Monday. India imports most of its oil, so a higher crude price matters here.

The Nifty closed down about 1 percent.

That sounds bad until you look at the neighbours. Korea fell 8 percent. Japan almost 4. Taiwan three and a half. India lost just over one, the gentlest fall in the region.

The damage was concentrated in chip and AI stocks, the part of the market India barely owns. When that trade unwinds overseas, India sits outside the blast radius.

The same thin chip exposure that held India back when the AI trade was booming is the thing cushioning it now.

A worry imported from abroad is not the same as a problem at home.
