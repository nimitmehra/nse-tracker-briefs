On a frightened day, watch what a market is still willing to pay for.

It was not random. The stocks that rose all had something solid behind them. CCL Products jumped nearly 9 percent on strong results, revenue up 46 percent. Thyrocare, the diagnostics name, rose on its results and a dividend.

Money on a nervous day pays up for two things. Proven earnings, and defensiveness.

And it sells the opposite. The hardest falls were not collapses. They were stocks that had run up too fast giving the gains back. MTAR fell over 6 percent even though its profit had tripled, because it had nearly doubled in three months. IFCI gave back a sharp spike from last week.

A rising profit and a falling stock on the same day usually means the good news was already in the price.

Now the calendar takes over. India's inflation reading lands Friday, the first against the central bank's freshly raised forecast. With crude up and the monsoon looking weak, the food number is the one to watch.
