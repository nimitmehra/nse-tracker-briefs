A small puzzle from today worth sitting with.

Crude oil went up. On paper, that should help the companies that sell oil. India's biggest one, Reliance, is a producer.

It did not help. Reliance fell about 2 percent, and the wider energy sector dropped with it.

The reason is simple once you see it. When fear is driving the day, the flow of money out of risky assets wins over the fundamental story. A higher oil price is a good fundamental for an oil producer, but on a frightened day nobody is buying the fundamental.

The clean version of the oil story showed up elsewhere. IndiGo, the airline, fell about 2.7 percent, because higher jet fuel is a plain cost for anyone who flies planes for a living.

So one event, higher crude, helped no one it should have helped and hurt exactly who it should have hurt.

On a fearful day, follow the flows before the logic.
