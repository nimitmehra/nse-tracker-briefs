A few cross-currents worth thinking about from Thursday.

One filing re-priced an entire corner of the market without a single rupee changing hands at the exchange, because the offer is all existing shareholders selling down, so the value flows to whoever owns the stake.

India under-participated in a global relief rally, with the broad emerging-market equity ETF up 3.25% and Korea's Kospi up 2.06%, because the rally here rested entirely on local money.

The metal stocks ignored a falling metal price again, with the Nifty Metal index closing dead flat even as industrial metals were soft, a reminder that Indian metal equities now trade on domestic demand rather than the global commodity.

The hawkish Fed found exactly one door into India, software services, because exporters earn in dollars and are valued on long-dated future earnings, so higher US rates and a firmer rupee hit them from both sides at once.
