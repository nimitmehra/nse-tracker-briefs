A few cross-currents from today worth sitting with.

On a day of fresh military escalation, the textbook move is for gold to rally as money runs for safety. It did not. Gold fell two percent. That non-move is the signal. Today's fear was a specific one, that oil supply through the Strait of Hormuz could be cut, so the worry was priced through crude, not through havens. When fear is about energy and not about everything, gold sits it out, and so do the lenders that finance gold.

The other thread is what a flat index really meant. The headline held because large domestic funds bought what foreign investors sold. But under that calm number the broad market fell more than one percent. So if you own mid-cap and small-cap stocks rather than the index, "the market was flat today" did not describe your day. The flat close was a composition story, not a quiet one.
