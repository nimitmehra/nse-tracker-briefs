India's market opened firm this morning, then gave it all back to close flat.

Overnight, the June 8 pledge between Israel and Iran to stop attacking broke down, and the United States is now reported to have struck Iran directly.

The Sensex had been up about 600 points by midday, on hopes the oil risk was easing. The fresh escalation pushed crude back up and Europe opened weak, so that gain unwound. The Nifty closed almost exactly flat at 23,214.

The flat number hid a lot, though. Mid- and small-cap stocks each fell about 1.3 percent, and the index only held because domestic funds kept buying what foreign investors were selling.

Brent crude ended higher near 92 dollars, which is the wrong direction for a country that imports most of its oil.

In the sectors, fertilisers were the one green corner, helped by the monsoon advance lifting demand and by firmer global prices. Metals were the weakest, with aluminium futures down about nine percent.

Oil India fell 10 percent, giving back a recent spike on a remote deep-water gas find that is years from producing anything.

On Friday, India's May inflation arrives, the first read that captures this crude spike.
