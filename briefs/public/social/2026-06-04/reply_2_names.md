Foreign investors are now more one-sided than they were yesterday.

Their net short on Nifty futures went from about 2.3 lakh contracts on Tuesday to 2.59 lakh on Wednesday.

On top of that, they bought protection both ways — about a lakh contracts insuring against a fall, another lakh insuring against a snap rally.

In the cash market, they sold another 5,617 crore rupees of Indian shares.

The volatility index rose 6 percent to 16.3.

On Friday, four big events arrive in one window. The Reserve Bank rate decision at 10 AM. India's January-to-March GDP at the same hour. The Governor's press conference at noon. US jobs data at 6 PM.

If the Reserve Bank language leans dovish, the shorts get covered fast and the market jumps. If it leans hawkish, the down move extends — there aren't enough longs in position to absorb it.

Two days of selling has made the bet bigger, not safer.

Friday will tell us which side was right.
