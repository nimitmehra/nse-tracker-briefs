The headline doesn't tell you what happened in the market.

The Nifty closed only a third of a percent lower. That looks like a flat day. It wasn't.

Beneath the surface, money moved hard. Lenders rose — bank shares broadly, and state-owned banks ran almost 2 percent. Small and mid-cap names ran higher on specific corporate news. Pharma added a little. Autos held flat.

But consumer goods companies fell another 1 percent. The monsoon delay still has them under pressure, even though the rains formally arrived in Kerala today.

A flat headline with a 5 percent fall in one sector and a 2 percent rise in another is a rotation, not a quiet day. The market is repositioning, not resting.
