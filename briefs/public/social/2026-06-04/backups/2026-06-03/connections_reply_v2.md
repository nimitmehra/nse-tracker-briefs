Even after the technology services sector fell five and a half percent on Wednesday, the Nifty closed only a third of a percent lower.

The reason was hidden in plain sight. The state-owned bank index rose 1.7 percent. The broader bank index added 0.9 percent. Pharma added 0.3. Autos closed flat. One sector lost hard, several others rose steadily — the headline cancelled out.

Portfolio holders did not have that luxury. If you owned the tech bench, you had a hard day. If you owned the banks, you had a quiet good one.

Underneath, consumer goods companies fell another 1 percent. The monsoon delay still weighs on rural demand, even though the rains formally arrived in Kerala today.

A flat headline with a five-and-a-half percent fall in one sector and a 1.7 percent rise in another is a rotation, not a quiet day.
