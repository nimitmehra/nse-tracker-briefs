**On the supportive side**, the Kerala monsoon onset has finally arrived. The India Meteorological Department's June 2 update confirmed that the southwest monsoon was likely to set in over Kerala around June 4 — today, in IST terms — ending the seven-day onset delay that has pressured FMCG, fertiliser and two-wheeler cohorts since mid-May. The first national progress map is due in five to seven days, but the symbolic relief from the onset itself is already visible in agri-input names. **On the worrying side**, the Reserve Bank's three-day Monetary Policy Committee meeting that began Tuesday concludes Friday with the rate decision at 10 AM IST. The consensus going in is a hold at 5.25% for the third straight meeting, with the Business Standard poll of economists showing the median view has shifted from "next move is a cut" to "at least one hike this fiscal year." SBI Research came out Tuesday with a counter-view: no hike needed despite global uncertainty and rupee pressure. Some economists expect the RBI to lower its FY27 GDP forecast toward 6.5% from 6.9%.

The US-India yield spread at ~2.5 percentage points is the tightest in months and reduces the carry-trade pull into Indian sovereigns. The rupee's drift toward fresh lows is the visible result.

**To watch this week**, Friday June 5 remains the defining session: RBI policy at 10 AM, Q4 FY26 + full-year GDP from MoSPI at the same window, Governor's press conference at noon, and US Non-Farm Payrolls at 6 PM IST. ## The Market

The defining feature of Wednesday's session was a clean rotation out of IT and into financials. Nifty IT closed at 29,384.45, down 5.57% on the day — the second-worst single-session fall for the index in three months. Bank Nifty rose 0.88% to 54,185.95; Nifty PSU Bank ran 1.70%; Nifty Pharma added 0.33%; Nifty Auto was nearly flat at +0.05%. Nifty FMCG slipped 1.01%, extending its monsoon-driven weakness. Nifty Realty fell 1.39%, Nifty Metal lost 0.17%, Nifty Media slipped 0.59%.

The Nifty's headline drop of just 0.33% concealed a much harder session underneath. The 11-stock IT cohort accounts for roughly 13% of the Nifty by weight; a 5.57% sectoral move on that base alone took ~75 basis points off the index. The fact that the headline closed only 33 basis points down means financials, autos and pharma did real lifting in the other direction. This is exactly the kind of rotation that obscures positioning trouble in a single index line.

India VIX rose 5.97% to 16.28 — the most meaningful one-day jump in the volatility index in two weeks. The rupee weakened a further 0.15% to ₹95.695 even though the dollar index was largely steady. When the dollar doesn't move but the rupee softens, the move is India-specific — and the pattern is now consistent week-on-week. The yields-up move in US Treasuries (US 10Y at 4.49%) compresses the US-India spread, which mechanically reduces foreign appetite for Indian bonds.

FIIs sold ₹5,617 crore in cash on Wednesday (lighter than Tuesday's ₹8,363 crore but still material) and deepened their Nifty futures net short to about 2,59,253 contracts — up from Tuesday's 2,30,096. Simultaneously, they bought 1,08,134 put contracts and 1,04,781 call contracts: classic protective hedging stacked on top of cash selling. This is exposure reduction, not a panic short. But it is also a position that gets very lonely if Friday delivers anything that suggests "next move is a cut" — there are not enough longs in position to absorb a snap rally, and the put-call hedge is expensive to maintain.

One non-move worth flagging: Oil marketing companies again did not bid even though Brent has stabilised. The regulator-capped marketing margin continues to hold the input-cost relief at the refiner level rather than flowing it to the P&L. The same pattern has now persisted across three sessions; rate-sensitive REITs and NBFCs are also failing to extend on the soft US 10Y print from earlier in the week, with the move now reversed back to 4.49%.

## Corporate News

The single biggest corporate-news driver on Wednesday was speculation, not a filing — and it ran the day's top gainer. Multiple business publications reported that the National Stock Exchange is preparing to file its draft red herring prospectus by mid-June, the long-awaited concrete step toward a listing that has been pending for years. The mechanism is well understood: IFCI owns 52.86% of Stock Holding Corporation of India (SHCIL), which in turn holds a 4.4% stake in the NSE. IFCI is the cleanest listed proxy on the Indian exchange. The stock ran 12.26% on Wednesday to a 21-month high of ₹81.90 on heavy volumes.

The other clean corporate catalyst on Wednesday was a sell-side upgrade for **CarTrade Tech (CARTRADE)**, +9.29%. Kotak Institutional Equities upgraded the stock from SELL to BUY and raised the price target from ₹1,800 to ₹2,300. The brokerage's thesis was a higher valuation for OLX India (which CarTrade acquired and integrated last year), steady GMV growth, improving monetisation, and the view that CarTrade's deeply entrenched dealer database is a moat that limits AI-disruption risk in the auto classifieds vertical. The move is fundamental, not basket-driven — CarTrade is unrelated to the NSE listing-anticipation cluster despite the same-day move.

The other clean corporate driver was **Ola Electric (OLAELEC)**, +9.82% to ₹43.7. The company released May 2026 EV registration data on Tuesday evening showing 15,139 units, up 23% month-on-month — three times faster than the industry's 15% MoM growth to 170,000 total electric vehicle registrations. The company simultaneously approved a QIP issue on June 1 with a floor price of ₹37.74 per share (up to 5% discount permitted), and management commentary indicated Q1 FY27 quarterly volumes are tracking to nearly double Q4 FY26. **Ather Energy (ATHERENERG)** rose 6.40% on EV-cohort beta — investors taking the Ola signal and applying it across the listed two-wheeler EV basket.

**Wockhardt (WOCKPHARMA)**, +5.48% to follow Tuesday's −8.67% reversal, has now retraced about 65% of the post-FDA-approval profit-taking move from Monday's Zaynich announcement. The base thesis (US FDA approval, addressable market, royalty stream) is intact; the price action is volatile.

## The Connections

Two threads define Wednesday's session — and both compound rather than offset.

**Thread one: the Tuesday→Wednesday IT reversal is also a positioning reversal.** Tuesday's brief noted that the headline rally was narrow, concentrated in the four IT majors plus two NSE-IPO names plus one solar QIP plus one FDA approval plus one defence momentum trade. That observation, written for breadth concerns, turned out to also be the trade. The same IT cohort that bid up Tuesday on the HPE/Marvell signal sold Wednesday on profit-booking, a US yield back-up to 4.49%, and the realisation that the underlying US AI signal was a one-day cohort event, not a fresh structural re-rating. Two-session round trips at this size are unusual; they almost always reflect FII positioning rather than fundamentals.

**Thread two: the rotation reveals what the market is comfortable holding pre-RBI.** Bank Nifty +0.88%, PSU Banks +1.70%, Pharma +0.33%, Auto flat — these are the domestic-earnings, rate-stable cohorts. FMCG −1.01% is the contradiction: it should be a defensive holding, but the monsoon delay continues to suppress rural-demand expectations even though IMD confirmed the Kerala onset for today. The market is parking in lender names ahead of a rate decision because lenders trade off the *language* (neutral vs. hawkish stance) more than the headline rate, and the language is what Governor Malhotra is expected to lean hawkish on.

**The compounding picture.** Foreign positioning is now meaningfully more one-sided than it was 24 hours ago. Cash selling continues, futures shorts have deepened, and option hedging is layered on top. India VIX up 5.97% to 16.28 confirms the tension. The Tuesday-positioning bet for a Friday dovish surprise has not unwound — it has compounded. That asymmetry is the single most important read on the session.
