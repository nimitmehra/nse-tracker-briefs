The Nifty rose about 1 percent to 23,854 on Monday. The trigger sat outside India: the United States and Iran signed a deal over the weekend to end their war and reopen the Strait of Hormuz, the world's busiest oil lane, sending crude down about 5 percent to a three-month low near $83.

India imports about 85 percent of its oil, so cheaper crude eases the import bill and inflation at once, and gives the Reserve Bank more room to keep cutting rates. The money moved into the sectors that gain most from lower rates: property and autos led, while pharma was the only one to fall.

Aarti Industries rose 13 percent on a run of contract wins, and Balkrishna gained 9 percent as the deal reverses the US tariffs that had stalled its tyre exports.

One caution holds it back. The deal is signed but not yet working, with Hormuz mine-clearance put at 40 to 50 days, which is why crude still sits about $16 above its pre-war level.

The tests come fast. The Federal Reserve decides Wednesday, and the deal is formally signed in Geneva on Friday.
