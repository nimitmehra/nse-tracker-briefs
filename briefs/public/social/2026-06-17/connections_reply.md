A few cross-currents from Tuesday worth sitting with.

Cheaper oil is now a three-day-old story, so the genuinely new thing is that the foreign money looked to stop leaving. The distinction is not small. A rally where domestic funds cushion the fall is a relief bounce. A rally where foreign money actively returns is a sustained move. Which one this is depends on whether the buy-turn holds for a second and third day, so the next two days of flow data are the thing to watch, not the index level.

The other thread is the rupee against software exporters. A firmer rupee normally pressures them, since they earn in dollars and spend in rupees, yet IT did not soften. The reason was one deal: HCLTech took a stake in an Indian-language AI startup, Sarvam, and that stock-specific bid pulled the wider IT cohort up with it. The currency drag is still there underneath. It was simply overwhelmed for a day, and the next time there is no such catalyst, it will show through.
