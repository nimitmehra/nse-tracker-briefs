One cross-current from Tuesday worth thinking about.

The Delhi Cabinet approved its EV Policy 2026-2030, a ₹15,000 crore push that subsidises electric two-wheelers and bars new petrol and CNG two-wheeler registrations from 2028. One policy, two opposite verdicts inside the same auto pack.

For the electric-scooter makers it is a straight demand tailwind, and Ola Electric rose 8.4% while Ather climbed 5.2% to a record. For legacy petrol two-wheelers it is a direct threat, which is why Eicher Motors, the maker of Royal Enfield, fell about 4.75% the same session.

The auto index looked almost flat, up 0.24%, but that calm hid a clean split. Policy was not lifting or sinking the whole sector. It was repricing who sits on the right side of the electric shift.

When a single rule sends two firms in opposite directions, the index level tells you nothing. The split underneath is the signal.
