A flat index hid a narrow wreck. The Nifty slipped just 0.34% to 23,865 on Tuesday, but underneath, large-cap IT fell hard while most of the market rose. Nifty IT dropped 2.73% to a multi-year low, with Infosys and TCS both at fresh 52-week lows.

The trigger came from Accenture's weak guidance last week, plus a growing fear that artificial intelligence is eating into the outsourcing work these firms get paid for.

But this was not the whole market turning fearful. Breadth was positive. Midcaps and smallcaps closed green, and more stocks rose than fell. Money left IT and moved into domestic sectors.

The clearest tell was a non-move. A softer rupee and a green Nasdaq both usually lift dollar-earning IT exporters, yet IT fell anyway. When the two things that should have helped a sector can't, the weakness is about India's IT demand, not a global mood.

On flows, foreign investors sold ₹2,557 crore on quarter-end book-squaring while domestic funds bought ₹6,842 crore, more than absorbing the exit.

Q1 earnings open mid-July. TCS reports first on 9 July, and after Tuesday that result is the calendar's most-watched event.
