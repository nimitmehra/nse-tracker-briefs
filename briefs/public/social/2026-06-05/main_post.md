The Nifty closed up ten points. The fear gauge fell, even with a big decision a day away. No sector moved with conviction.

This is what a market looks like when it is waiting. On Friday morning the Reserve Bank announces its rate decision and India reports its GDP, both in the same hour. So traders parked their money and refused to take a side.

The one real move was Zee Entertainment, up 10 percent after winning the India broadcast rights to the next two FIFA World Cups. One company's good news, not a media story.

The rupee was the only live undercurrent. It slipped toward a record low even as the dollar eased, because foreign money kept leaving India.
