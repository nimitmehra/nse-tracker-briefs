Crude oil fell almost 3 percent on Thursday. Normally that is a clean win for India, which buys most of its oil abroad. Cheaper oil means a lower import bill and a firmer rupee.

Yet the market stayed flat and the rupee weakened anyway.

The reason is the war, not the oil. With fresh strikes around the Strait of Hormuz, a risk premium still sits under the price. One down day does not remove it. So the market discounts the relief against the chance it reverses.

When a piece of good news fails to move a market, the news that didn't happen is usually doing the work.
