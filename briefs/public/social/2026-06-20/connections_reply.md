A few cross-currents worth thinking about from Friday, and one from the weekend.

The money that left IT did not leave the market, it rotated into domestic, rate-sensitive names, with The Leela up about 8%, Piramal Finance almost 8%, and Tata Capital over 6%, so a single-sector shock quietly funded a domestic bid.

A headline drop of two-thirds of a percent hid a much wider split underneath, because one heavy sector falling hard can drag the whole tape lower even when nearly every other sector barely moved.

Two record-sized IPO filings landed in a single week, and a heavy pipeline of new paper for the second half competes for the same pool of money, which can cap a market even when individual company results are fine.

The swing variable into next week is the weather, with first-half-of-June rainfall running about 41% below normal and El Niño now formally flagged, the pattern that tends to weaken Indian rains and the farm and agri-input names that lean on them.

And into Monday, the one to watch is oil. Iran declared the Strait of Hormuz closed on Saturday, but the US Navy says traffic actually rose and Brent is still near $80, where a real closure would put it well above $100, so the market is treating it as rhetoric ahead of the US-Iran talks that opened in Switzerland on Sunday, not a supply shock.
