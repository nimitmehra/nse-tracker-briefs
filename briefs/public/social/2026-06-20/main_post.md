Indian IT cracked on Friday after Accenture cut its full-year guidance and fell about 18% overnight in New York, its worst day ever, which broke before India opened and hit our software names the same session.

The Nifty IT index closed down 3.65%, after sinking roughly 6% intraday, with Infosys down 8.19% to a near five-year low, even though no Indian IT company had reported anything of its own.

Accenture's actual message was the worry: companies are moving their tech budgets into AI rather than growing them, so this is about the size of the whole demand pie, not a one-company stumble.

The Nifty fell 0.64% to 24,013 and the Sensex shed 607 points, snapping a five-day streak, but strip out IT and the tape was flat, with 14 of 15 sectors closing within about 1% of unchanged.

Foreign investors actually net-bought about ₹4,859 crore of shares on a down day while domestic funds sold, buying non-IT large-caps and hedging with a Nifty-futures short rather than betting on a bounce.

Reliance filed Jio Platforms' roughly ₹38,000 crore IPO on Friday, India's largest ever and all new shares to cut debt, three days after the NSE's own ₹30,000 crore filing, lifting New India Assurance 13%.
