A few cross-currents from Thursday worth thinking about.

One oil shock hit the market in three separate places. Costlier crude pushed the state fuel retailers into quarterly losses as their fuel margins collapsed, swung a big airline to a loss on its fuel bill, and pressed the rupee toward its weakest level, which raises the cost of everything India imports.

A near-record-weak rupee usually helps IT, and on Thursday it did nothing. Software exporters earn in dollars and spend in rupees, so a weak rupee normally lifts them, but the overnight global tech sell-off cancelled that tailwind and traders waited for a marquee IT result due after the close.

Two results this week show the market pays for the future, not the past. A chemicals maker put up a record quarter and fell 8.5% because it guided softer ahead, and a private lender reported a 72% profit jump and fell 6% because it came from lower provisions, not real lending growth.
