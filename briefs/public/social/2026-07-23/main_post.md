Indian shares fell for a fourth straight day on Thursday, driven by the same force behind the whole slide: oil. Brent crossed $100 and the Nifty closed 0.53% lower at 23,869.60.

- The fall was orderly, the fear gauge barely up at 13.48, and India fell far less than the world.

- That $100 came after fresh tanker attacks off Saudi Arabia and the Strait of Hormuz, and India imports about 85% of its crude, so it hits the import bill and the rupee.

- Foreign investors sold ₹2,999 crore but domestic funds bought ₹2,947 crore, absorbing almost the whole exit, which is why India held up while the region was sold.

- Infosys reported after the close, a revenue beat but profit down about 9% on the quarter and its FY27 growth guidance trimmed to 1.5-3.0%, so the market's vote lands Friday.

- Looking to Friday, the US-India tariff window expires still unsigned, with an around-18% US tariff on Indian goods on the table.
