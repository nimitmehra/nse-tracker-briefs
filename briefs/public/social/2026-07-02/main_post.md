The rupee hit a fresh record low near 95.4 on Thursday, even as the dollar weakened and oil stayed cheap. A currency falling against a falling dollar usually means money is leaving, and foreign investors sold for a fifth straight day (net -₹312 cr).

The Nifty rose 0.71% to 24,175.70, but nearly all was one +4.64% IT bounce off a three-year low, Infosys +5%, banks flat. An oversold snap-back, not a trend turn, and not rate-cut hope.

After India shut, the US jobs report (weak hiring, hot wages) read hawkish, yields up and tech down. A Friday headwind against IT, never Thursday's cause.

Friday: US markets closed, so India trades thin; the RBI's weekly figures that evening show how hard it is defending the rupee.
