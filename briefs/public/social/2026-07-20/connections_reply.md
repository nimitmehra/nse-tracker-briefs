A few cross-currents from Monday worth thinking about.

The bank selling was a switch, not a verdict on the whole sector. The market marked down the expensive private lenders whose margins are shrinking and moved the same money into cheaper state-run banks posting triple-digit profit growth, so one bank index fell while another rose on the same day. Watch whether IDFC First Bank's result on Friday keeps that split alive.

The market read the margin miss as a warning for the whole cohort, not a one-quarter stumble. The counter-view from several brokers is that stronger deposit growth should bring funding costs down and ease the squeeze ahead, and whether that plays out is the read to track.

India fell while the broader emerging-market group rose, and that is the reassuring part. It is not foreign money fleeing but India decoupling downward on its own bank earnings, the exact inverse of Friday when India rose while Asia fell. Both sessions were driven by earnings, not flows.
