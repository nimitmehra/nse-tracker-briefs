India's biggest private banks disappointed on their margins, and that one theme dragged the Nifty down 0.39% to 24,238 on Monday. Five things to know:

- HDFC Bank and Axis Bank each fell about 5% after quarterly results showed shrinking lending margins, with HDFC Bank's at a record-low 3.26%.

- The worry is a margin squeeze across the whole private-bank pack, not a two-stock miss, as lower RBI rates reset loan yields faster than deposit costs.

- Money did not leave the market but rotated inside it, as state-run banks, cement and pharma all closed higher while domestic funds bought ₹1,312 crore against ₹1,121 crore of foreign selling.

- The oil premium is back after the US-Iran standoff at the Strait of Hormuz flared over the weekend, lifting Brent near $91 before easing to $88 and reopening imported-inflation risk for India.

- Looking ahead, Infosys reports Wednesday as the big software verdict, and the temporary 10% US tariff on Indian goods expires Thursday, unsigned, the week's biggest scheduled event.
