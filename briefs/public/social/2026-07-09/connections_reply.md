A few cross-currents from Thursday worth thinking about.

Oil eased but it did not crash, so the input-cost relief never came. The obvious trade on cooling tension is that cheaper crude lifts the fuel retailers, paint makers and airlines that run on it. It didn't happen, because Brent only stabilised near $78 and is still up about 9% on the week. With no actual drop in the oil price, there was no cost relief for those sectors to price in. The non-rally was the accurate read, not an anomaly.

And this was relief, not reversal. The fear gauge collapsed nearly 9%, but gold stayed firm and oil held its weekly gains, so the safe-haven premium is still priced in. Thursday repaired Wednesday's panic without resolving the cause. The Nifty is still down about 0.9% on the week, and the 24-July US tariff deadline on Indian goods is two weeks out and unresolved.
