Three names that carried Thursday.

Kalyan Jewellers jumped 18.4%, the single biggest move in the market, and mostly a snap-back. It had fallen 9% on 7 July even after a strong quarterly update (revenue up 38%, same-store sales up 28%), and Thursday reversed that overshoot as gold firmed. The operating update is the floor; the size of the jump is the overshoot correcting.

Swiggy rose 7.47% on two real developments. Its foreign ownership slipped below 50% for the first time as a listed company, a step toward local-control status that could let its quick-commerce arm hold its own inventory, and its food-on-train service expanded to 180 cities. The sell-side is split: JM Financial rates it Reduce with a ₹250 target.

Dr Reddy's fell 5.89%, the day's marquee faller, after it told the exchange that some batches of semaglutide, the blockbuster weight-loss molecule, were out-of-specification and supply will be delayed until it finds the cause. It says no patient-safety or filing impact, but it dents a flagship growth product right at launch.
