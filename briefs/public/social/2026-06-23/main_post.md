The Iran oil scare that spooked markets on Friday lifted over the weekend: the Strait of Hormuz reopened, and a US-Iran deal in Switzerland set a 60-day roadmap plus a waiver letting Iran sell oil again.

So Brent crude fell about 11% on the week to ~$78 — and for an economy that imports 85% of its oil, that easing of the import bill, inflation and the rupee is the cleanest tailwind there is.

On that, the Nifty rose 0.37% to 24,103 on Monday, recovering part of Friday's IT crash — but only part. The index is up barely 1% on the week, and IT clawed back very little of its fall.

The biggest moves were small-caps on hard catalysts, not the index: Kirloskar Oil locked a 20% upper circuit on an order to power AI data centres, and NOCIL jumped 20% on a fresh anti-dumping duty. Cipla led the Nifty itself on a Citi upgrade.

Reliance rallied as it filed for the Jio IPO — India's largest-ever, an all-fresh issue of about ₹38,000 crore, mostly to clear Jio's debt.

The honest caveat: this is a 60-day roadmap, not a settled peace — fresh-strike threats were made even during the talks. Cheaper oil is a window, not a permanent gift.
