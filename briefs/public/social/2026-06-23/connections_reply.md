A few cross-currents worth thinking about from Monday.

Don't read the foreign-buying number as "foreigners are back": a scheduled FTSE India index reshuffle took effect today, an estimated $600m-plus of passive buying that funds had to do regardless of the news, so a chunk of the ₹4,859 crore inflow was mechanical, not conviction — against a record ₹2.2 lakh crore that has left India this year.

The leadership told the real story: money rotated out of last week's safe-harbour winners — FMCG and consumer staples like Asian Paints, Titan and Nestlé fell — and into defensives and crude-sensitives, a measured rotation, not a risk-on chase.

The information technology bounce is a clue, not a comfort: if Friday's crash had been an over-reaction, the sector would have round-tripped it; instead it recovered barely a fraction, which tells you the market has not dismissed the warning that companies are shifting tech budgets to AI rather than growing them.

And into this week, two swing variables: the monsoon, a 46% June rain deficit now finally reviving, and the new oil window, tied to a 60-day deal that can slip — watch the crude price as the truth-meter on both the relief and the risk.
