Indian shares jumped on Friday, with the Nifty closing up about 2 percent at 23,622. The trigger came from outside India: reports that the United States and Iran had agreed a draft deal to wind down their conflict, including reopening the Strait of Hormuz, the world's busiest oil lane. Crude fell to a two-month low, which eases the biggest cost India faces as a large oil importer.

A second tailwind landed the same afternoon. May inflation came in at 3.93 percent, below the 4 percent economists expected, which calms the worry that the Reserve Bank would have to stop cutting rates.

The rally was broad and led by financials. Banks rose because lower oil and softer inflation both point to an easier rate path. The one sector that did not rise was IT, because a stronger rupee hurts companies that earn in dollars and spend in rupees.

IFCI was locked at its 20 percent daily limit on talk the National Stock Exchange is preparing its long-delayed listing. The two big truck makers, Tata Motors CV and Ashok Leyland, each rose about 10 percent, since cheaper fuel lowers fleet costs.

One caution sits over all of it. The deal is reported but unsigned, Iran called it speculation, and the real test is whether it gets signed in Geneva this weekend. If it slips, Monday could open lower.
