A few cross-currents from Friday worth sitting with.

Cheaper oil did more work than the headline showed. When crude fell on the Hormuz news, the relief did not stay in oil stocks. It travelled to truck makers, where fuel is the biggest cost, and to banks and property, where lower oil eases inflation and points to an easier rate path. One geopolitical headline rotated money across half a dozen sectors at once, which is why the rally was so wide. The catch is that the whole chain rests on a draft no one has signed.

The other thread is who paid for the rally. Foreign investors were still net sellers on Friday. It was domestic funds that did the buying. A rally the locals pay for tends to be sturdier than one foreigners chase, but it also means the foreign bid that would confirm the move has not shown up yet. Better to treat Friday as a relief bounce that still needs a second leg than as the all-clear.
