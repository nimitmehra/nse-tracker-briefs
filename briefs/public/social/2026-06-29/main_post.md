The oil shock the market feared all weekend never arrived. Over the long break the Gulf flared up, with US and Iranian strikes around the Strait of Hormuz, and India reopened Monday braced for a crude spike.

But on Sunday the US and Iran agreed to halt the fighting and talk. So Brent rose only about 1% to near 73 dollars, still a four-month low, the rupee held flat, and the Nifty slipped just 0.46% to 23,946. A scare that passed, not a shock that landed.

The real story sat underneath: a defensive shuffle. Money moved into pharma and healthcare, the two best sectors, helped by the US extending its pause on new drug tariffs to 9 July. It moved out of IT and autos.

Auto was the worst sector, down 2.08%, but it fell for a policy reason, not an oil one. Delhi approved its EV Policy 2.0, with a fat subsidy on electric two-wheelers and a planned ban on new petrol ones from 2028. Electric maker Ather rose 8.5% while the legacy petrol pack sold off.

Tuesday brings the Nifty and Bank Nifty monthly expiry stacked on June quarter-end, so expect heavier positioning.
