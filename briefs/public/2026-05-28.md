## Macro & Policy

The Reserve Bank of India's rate-setting meeting on June 3-5 now looks defensive. Economists polled by Business Standard expect the repo rate held at 5.25% for a third straight meeting, but most see at least one hike this financial year and an upward revision to the FY27 inflation forecast — driven by the West Asia supply distortions. April retail inflation was benign at 3.48%, but economists project May hardening to about 4.1% on food prices, and a below-normal, El Niño-linked monsoon is now flagged as a food-price upside risk. The easing cycle is, for now, off the table.

On trade, the next India-US round runs June 1-4 to finalise an interim agreement. An industry body (the US-India Strategic Partnership Forum) warned that end-July is the "drop-dead date" — India risks tariffs higher than its Asian competitors if no deal lands by then, against a reported cut in the US reciprocal tariff from 25% to 18%. Treat it as rhetoric until a signed text, Gazette or customs notification appears.

## The Connections

*Behind the tape: how the Gulf escalation ripples through Indian sectors.*

**The Iran escalation tightens fertiliser input costs — the opposite of the "cheap crude helps" read.** A wider Gulf conflict is usually read as a crude-price risk. For phosphatic fertiliser makers the sharper vector is sulphur, the binding input, whose supply is already squeezed by a Chinese export ban and now by the Iran-conflict disruption. The cost vector for the fertiliser cohort (Coromandel International, Chambal Fertilisers) points up regardless of where Brent settles. **Watch:** the sulphur spot price, not Brent.

**The Middle East order risk for engineering contractors moved from rhetoric to active conflict.** Indian transmission-and-distribution contractors — Engineers India, Power Mech, Transrail Lighting — carry Middle East order exposure that management had described only in words. Peer Kalpataru Projects put a number on it this week: a ₹200-300 crore quarterly revenue hit, 10-11% of its order book. **Why it matters:** the ceasefire violation puts that exposure on an active-conflict footing, not a negotiating-framework one.

**The monsoon and the Gulf escalation push the same inflation button — into next week's rate meeting.** A below-normal, El Niño-linked monsoon raises food-price risk just as the crude-and-fertiliser channel raises imported-inflation risk. **Why it matters:** both feed the upward FY27 inflation-forecast revision economists expect at the June 3-5 Reserve Bank meeting — the reason the easing cycle is off the table, a headwind for rate-sensitive lenders (housing finance, NBFCs).

**IT's weakness stays structural, not cyclical.** Nifty IT's recent multi-month lows reflect a repricing for artificial-intelligence-driven revenue deflation — the industry's own estimate is 2-3% a year, with 30-40% of services revenue exposed. **Why it matters:** for the sector's largest portfolio-relevant name, Infosys (INFY), the debate is value-versus-structural-decline, and a US-India trade deal with an IT carve-out is one of the few near-term supports.
