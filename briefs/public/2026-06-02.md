## The Lead

A strange Monday: broad emerging markets rallied — EEM +2.16% on the US session, EM Asia +2.18% — but the Nifty fell 0.70% to 23,382.60, a fourth straight losing session. The reason wasn't a global flow story; it was India-specific. Two factors drove the divergence.

**1. The US-Iran 60-day framework slipped again.** Trump returned the draft to Iran Sunday demanding tougher uranium and Hormuz terms. Brent bounced 3.07% to $94.88, reviving the inflation-pass-through worry into Friday's RBI rate decision — and adding pressure on the MPC to hold rather than cut.

**2. The monsoon scare hit FMCG.** Nifty FMCG fell 2.30% with ITC, HUL and Tata Consumer down 1.7-2.8%. ITC's heavy index weight in FMCG means when ITC moves the whole cohort moves with it — and the unchanged 90% LPA monsoon forecast plus weak El Niño risk kept the rural-demand concern alive into a week where consumer-staples positioning matters.

Add the standard MPC-week de-risk into Friday's quadruple-event day, and broad EM up + India down is the read.

## Global Setup

The global setup matters for India in one specific way Monday: **US 10-year yields were bid up to 4.47%** (2-year 3.62%, mild bear-flattening). The US-India yield spread is the binding constraint on FII bond inflows. With India 10Y at 7.088% (−3 bps Monday), the spread to US 10Y is ~2.6 percentage points — historically tight territory, leaving little room for FII rotation INTO Indian rates even if equity flows soften. That's the global piece that touches India today, and it's worth watching whether the US 10Y holds 4.45%+ this week.

The other global moves were noise from India's perspective. US equities ticked higher (S&P 500 +0.26%, Nasdaq +0.42%), Europe sold off (FTSE −0.68%, DAX −0.40%, STOXX 600 −0.76%), Asia opened Tuesday mixed (Nikkei −0.92%, KOSPI −1.25% as the AI-memory trade cooled, Taiwan TAIEX +0.63%). DXY firmed 0.28% to 99.19; gold eased 0.81% to $4,524. GIFT Nifty overnight suggests an opening close to Monday's cash close. EM was the one global piece that did matter as backdrop — EEM +2.16%, EM Asia +2.18% — and that thread is the divergence anchored in The Lead.

## Macro & Policy

The week's macro story breaks cleanly into three buckets.

**On the supportive side**, Monday delivered two solid macro releases. The Controller General of Accounts confirmed India's FY26 fiscal deficit landed at 4.4% of GDP — exactly on target — with net tax receipts of ₹33 lakh crore versus ₹30.87 lakh crore the prior year. Budget discipline held. Second, India's Manufacturing PMI for May was revised UP to 55.0 final from a 54.3 flash — a 4-year high in input-cost pressures, but with output and new-orders expansion the strongest among components. Demand stayed firm even with crude pushing input costs.

**On the worrying side**, May's GST collection came in at ₹1.94 lakh crore gross, up only 3.2% YoY — the slowest growth in several months. The headline is misleading. Import GST jumped 19.1% (gold and oil ticket sizes), but **domestic GST fell 2.6% YoY** — a clear deceleration the Indian press largely buried below the headline. The monsoon forecast stays at 90% of long-period average with weak El Niño risk unchanged. And the US-Iran 60-day framework, which had been "imminent" through last week, slipped again: Trump sent the draft back to Iran late Sunday demanding tougher uranium-disposition timing and Hormuz-access terms. Brent bounced 3.07% to $94.88 on that deferral, partially reversing May's −17% drawdown. The petrol/diesel hikes (~₹5/litre since mid-May) mean the consumer is not getting the crude relief — the exchequer is.

**To watch this week**, Friday 5 June is now a confirmed quadruple-event day: the RBI MPC rate decision at ~10 AM IST (consensus hold at 5.25% for the third straight meeting, with the FY27 inflation revision the substantive watch), Q4 FY26 + FY26 annual GDP from MoSPI at the same window (RBI projection 7.3%, government projection 7.4% — the 10 bps gap is the subtext), Governor Sanjay Malhotra's press conference around noon, and US Non-Farm Payrolls at 6 PM IST. Four big events in eight hours. The India-US Bilateral Trade Agreement Round 1 is on Day 2 of 4 in Delhi today, with any communique most likely Thursday-Friday.

## The Market

Monday's session carried a small headline (Nifty −0.70%) and a stark sector dispersion underneath it.

**Nifty IT was the day's standout at +3.27%** to 30,082, the lone green corner. Persistent +5.18%, Infosys +4.29%, Coforge +4.18%, TCS +4.0%, Tech Mahindra +3.43%, HCL Tech +2.0%, Wipro +1.5%. The primary driver, per the Tier-1 Indian business press, was the global AI bid carrying forward into Monday's Indian session — Snowflake's $6 billion AWS AI infrastructure deal (announced May 27) reignited enterprise-AI sentiment, lifting the global tech cohort across last week and into this Monday. US Fed positioning added a second tailwind. Indian IT rode that bid; this was **sector-cohort beta**, not a stock-specific event for any single Indian IT name. The rupee weakened modestly (USDINR 95.00, rupee −1.09%) which is a mild secondary tailwind for IT exporters, but the lead story was the AI catalyst, not currency.

**Nifty FMCG was the day's worst at −2.30%**. ITC, Hindustan Unilever and Tata Consumer all fell between 1.7% and 2.8%. Two pressures stacked: the unchanged monsoon scare (a below-normal season threatens rural demand), and ITC's heavy index weight dragging the cohort. **Nifty PSU Bank −1.85%** led financials weakness on MPC-week de-risk plus the ongoing FII trim in the banking cohort.

The broader market was materially weaker than the headline — **Nifty Midcap 100 −1.45%, Smallcap −0.88%**, both well below the Nifty's −0.70% — a clean signal that Monday's selling reached deeper than large-caps. The rupee told its own story: a 1.09% weakening on only a 0.28% rise in the dollar index means there was an India-specific weakening component on top of broad USD strength. FIIs net sold ₹3,912 crore in cash and ran a fresh −2,23,180 net short on Nifty futures — bearish positioning, but the cash pace was materially smaller than Friday's ₹21,106 crore MSCI spike, so the rhythm reverted to "normal heavy" rather than panic. DIIs absorbed ₹5,109 crore.

**The pharma non-move is the most important read of the session.** A $700 million upfront on a bispecific-antibody out-licensing deal is structurally the largest cohort-level innovation event of 2026 for Indian pharma. Friday's intraday Glenmark move had already priced the deal substance in advance. Monday's −3.63% extension and the Pharma index's continued drag through from Friday's −1.50% tells you foreign capital is still valuing Dr Reddy's, Sun Pharma, Lupin, Cipla, Aurobindo and Glenmark as a generic-formulations cohort — not as a discovery/biosimilar cohort, even with a landmark deal on the wire. For an investor in any Indian pharma name, this is the single most diagnostic data point from Monday: the innovation premium is not being awarded.

**The IT bid was an AI-catalyst story, not a rupee story.** Persistent +5.18%, Infosys +4.29%, Coforge +4.18%, TCS +4.0% — that level of cohort lift on a day the broader market fell 0.70% needs a real catalyst, and the catalyst was Snowflake's overnight result reinforcing the global enterprise-AI-spend thesis. A weaker rupee (USDINR 95.00, −1.09%) is a secondary tailwind for dollar earners. Worth being precise on direction: a weaker rupee HELPS IT (they earn dollars and spend rupees), so Monday's currency move added to the AI bid rather than fighting it — but the AI bid was the lead.

**Cheaper crude relief is not flowing to OMCs.** Brent bounced 3% Monday but is still down ~17% over May — the sharpest monthly drop since 2020. By textbook math, BPCL, HPCL and IOC should have rallied across the month on falling input costs. They have not. The reason is the petrol/diesel hikes of about ₹5/litre since mid-May — government is keeping retail prices firm while crude has fallen, which means the margin relief is being collected as excise, not flowing to OMC P&L. For OMC investors, regulator-capped marketing margins are the binding constraint, not the crude price.

**Broad EM up + India down was India-specific, not capital-reallocation.** The "India losing capital to the North-Asia AI trade" frame that explained the past two weeks did NOT operate Monday — KOSPI fell 1.25% Tuesday open after last week's record session, Taiwan TAIEX added only 0.63%, and broad-EM equity rose 2%+ (EEM and EEMA both north of 2.1%). India sat conspicuously below. The drivers behind Monday's India fall — Iran-deferral, MPC-week positioning, FMCG monsoon weakness, the FII outflow trajectory — all sit on India's side of the ledger. The structural FPI outflow story (CYTD ₹2.25 lakh crore) is still operative; Monday's specific divergence was domestic.
