## The Lead

A strange day: every macro signal was good for India, yet the market fell 1.5%. Oil crashed nearly 20% over May, the rupee firmed over 1%, and the bond curve barely moved. So why the sell-off? Three reasons stacked into one session — a mechanical MSCI index reshuffle pulled out roughly $800 million to $1 billion of foreign passive money at the close, the weather office held its below-normal monsoon forecast, and the US-Iran ceasefire deal never got signed.

## Global Setup

Wall Street was quietly higher (S&P 500 +0.22%, Dow +0.72%) and Europe flat. The Asian session was the story: KOSPI +3.55%, Taiwan TAIEX +2.51%, Nikkei +2.53% — fresh records in places — while Nifty fell 1.5% and Shanghai 0.7%. That split is not random risk-off; if it were, Korea and Taiwan would have fallen too. It is foreign capital concentrating in the Korea-Taiwan-Japan semiconductor and memory-chip cycle, at India's expense. Dollar flat, US 10Y 4.45%, gold $4,554. GIFT Nifty 23,953 (−1.05%) — pointing to a small leg lower at Monday's open.

## Macro & Policy

The day turned on two opposing forces — sort them and the picture clears.

**On the supportive side:** Brent at $91.19 is down about 17% for May, the sharpest monthly drop since 2020. For an importer of 85% of its oil, every $10 off crude is roughly 0.4-0.5% of current-account-deficit relief and a real break on inflation. The rupee firmed 1.1% to ₹94.99 even though the dollar was flat — helped by Reserve Bank Governor Sanjay Malhotra's "whatever is required" remarks and active RBI dollar-selling.

**On the worrying side:** the India Meteorological Department maintained its monsoon forecast at 90% of the long-period average, with an 84% combined probability of a below-normal-or-deficient season and rising El Niño risk. A direct hit to the rural economy and food prices, and the single biggest domestic worry of the day. It hit fertilisers, agrochemicals and FMCG hardest.

**Watch next week:** the Reserve Bank's rate decision on June 3-5 (a hold is expected; the FY27 inflation guidance is the real read), the India-US trade round on June 1-4, and the GDP print — rescheduled to June 5, the same morning as the RBI decision.

## The Market

A broad, orderly sell-off — every headline index red. Metals weakest at −2.0%, Auto −2.0% on profit-taking off a strong month, Consumption −1.9% and FMCG −1.5% on the monsoon worry, Energy −1.6% (ONGC −3.2% on the crude crash). Bank Nifty held up best at −1.1% — firmer rupee, flat curve, and Federal Bank's MSCI inflow cushioned the financials. The one green corner was IT at +0.6%, led by Tech Mahindra and HCL Tech — a flight-to-safety bid into a beaten-down cohort (Nifty IT is still −24% YTD). Worth remembering the point usually gotten backwards: a *stronger* rupee hurts IT (they earn in dollars), so the green close was defensive positioning, not a currency tailwind. India VIX rose 8% but stayed at 16 — an orderly retreat, not panic.

## The Connections

*Behind the day's move — what caused it, the mechanism, and which sectors next.*

**The headline was structural, not fundamental — and the mechanism is worth naming.** MSCI is the global index that large foreign passive funds track. When MSCI does its quarterly reshuffle and India's weight drops slightly, those funds aren't choosing — they HAVE to sell Indian stocks at the close to match the new weights. Friday's reshuffle added Federal Bank, MCX, NALCO and Indian Bank, dropped Hyundai Motor India, Jubilant FoodWorks, Kalyan Jewellers and RVNL, and pushed about $800 million to $1 billion of net India outflow through the bell (Nuvama Alternatives estimate, single desk). Layered on a Bakri-Eid-displaced F&O monthly expiry the same session, the calendar alone explains roughly a third to two-fifths of the day's foreign sell. Lesson: not every 1.5% Nifty fall on positive macro is a market signal; some are a calendar event the index can't see around.

**The non-move worth flagging — India's oil marketers did not bid on the Brent crash.** Brent is down about 17% for May. Textbook reaction: India's oil-marketing trio — Bharat Petroleum, Hindustan Petroleum, Indian Oil — should rally on cheaper input and lower under-recovery. They didn't; none was among the day's larger movers. The signal: marketing-margin caps and the political overlay on retail-fuel pricing are binding the relief — the math doesn't translate to earnings as cleanly as the chart suggests. Watch the next quarterly gross-marketing-margin disclosure.

**The monsoon downgrade transmitted cleanly through fertilisers, and the channel does not stop there.** Coromandel fell 4.3% to a fresh 52-week low, Dhanuka 3.9%, Deepak Fertilizers 2.6%, FACT 5.2% (compounded by a near-zero profit print). The same channel hit FMCG (HUL −2.0%, Tata Consumer −2.2%) and Consumption (−1.85% as an index) on the rural-demand read. Premium spirits (Radico Khaitan) decoupled — insulated from the staples-rural worry. Watch day-by-day Kerala monsoon onset through 15 June; that data series is the single most market-relevant macro print for the next four weeks.
