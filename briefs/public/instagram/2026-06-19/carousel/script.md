## Card 1 — Market (what + why)
- numlabel: NIFTY 50
- number: +0.34% to 24,168   [brief line 8, 18]   · cls: up
- headline: NSE filed to list itself
- why: The National Stock Exchange filed papers for its own ₹30,000 crore IPO, India's largest ever, valuing the exchange above ₹5 lakh crore. Insurers and asset managers that own NSE stakes re-rated on the news, which kept the Nifty green for a fifth straight day.

## Card 2 — The teaching idea (why an exchange listing re-rates the finance complex)
- kicker: Why this matters
- headline: A listing nobody got paid for
- why: The whole deal is an Offer for Sale, so existing owners sell about 6% of NSE and no new money reaches NSE itself. That is why the move showed up not in the exchange but in its shareholders, the insurers and asset managers like New India Assurance (+7.98%) whose NSE stakes are now marked at a ₹5-lakh-crore valuation. The flip side: rival BSE fell about 4.3%, now that it faces a much larger listed competitor.

## Card 3 — Top riser (+why)
- numlabel: BATA INDIA
- number: +16.42%   [brief line 67, 78, 102]   · cls: up
- headline: A new boss, re-rated
- why: Bata India, the footwear retailer, named Sanjay Rao, formerly a senior Nike director, as its new CEO from August. The market read it as a turnaround signal for a brand that has lagged, and the shares jumped 16.42%, reportedly the biggest single-day gain in about two decades.

## Card 4 — The honest catch (+why)
- kicker: The catch
- headline: A green day foreigners sold into
- why: The Nifty rose a fifth straight day, but foreign investors actually sold ₹855 crore of Indian shares after the hawkish US Fed, while domestic funds bought ₹3,130 crore and did all of the lifting. This rally is not foreign-backed, it is domestically carried, so the next few prints decide whether the foreign selling deepens or reverses.
