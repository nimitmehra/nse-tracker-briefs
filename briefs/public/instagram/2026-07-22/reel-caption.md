Indian shares fell for a third day running on Wednesday, and this time the fear finally showed up. The Nifty slipped 0.79% to 23,996, and the market's fear gauge rose 5.5%, its first climb on a down day this week. The trigger was oil: a fresh US-Iran flare-up and a tanker attack in the Strait of Hormuz pushed Brent toward $95, and for a country that imports most of its oil, that lands straight on the bill.

The unusual part: overnight US markets rose and emerging markets gained nearly 3%, yet India fell anyway. Not foreign money fleeing the region, but India going its own way, weighed by an oil-import bill its neighbours do not carry.

The cleanest gain was Data Patterns, up 12.7% after emerging as the lowest bidder on an about 1,300 crore rupee defence order from Hindustan Aeronautics. The biggest fall was Bandhan Bank, down 17% despite 35% profit growth, after it cut its forward profitability guidance. M&M Financial rose 8% the same day on 75% profit growth. The lesson: a bank's outlook moves its stock more than the profit it just reported.
