India's June-quarter earnings season is starting to separate the strong from the weak, and on Friday that pulled the Nifty up 1.09% to 24,334 even as most of Asia fell hard. Japan's Nikkei dropped 4% and Korea's KOSPI 3.7% during the same hours India rose, so India decoupled upward on its own results rather than a global tide. The engines were record numbers from Federal Bank, whose profit rose 37% to a decadal-low bad-loan book, sending the stock 6.86% to a 52-week high, and beats across IT, with eClerx up 12.5% and Tech Mahindra up 4.1%.

But one caveat matters: this was a home-grown rally. Domestic funds bought a net Rs1,018 crore while foreign investors were small net sellers, so earnings and Indian money, not a foreign return, carried Friday up. And earnings cut both ways: tyre-maker CEAT crashed 7.3% as profit collapsed 96% on costlier rubber and crude, a reminder that with Brent back at $86 the oil premium is back, not gone. Two clocks ahead: June inflation ran hot at 4.38%, complicating the RBI, and the US tariff cliff falls on 24 July.
