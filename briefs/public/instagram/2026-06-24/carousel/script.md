## Card 1 — Market (what + why)
- numlabel: NIFTY 50 · 23,824
- number: −1.16% to 23,824 · cls: down
- headline: A selloff made abroad
- why: The Nifty fell 1.16% and gave back Monday's bounce, but the cause was not Indian. The world's most crowded trade, AI semiconductors, de-risked overnight and dragged every risk market down with it. India has no big listed chipmakers, yet it was sold anyway. The fear gauge jumped 8.6%.

## Card 2 — The WHY (the durable concept)
- kicker: Why it matters
- headline: The global tape can beat local news
- why: Here is the puzzle worth keeping. India's own setup on Tuesday was good, oil at a three-month low, a calm bond market, business activity still growing. It fell anyway. On any single day, a global risk scare can completely override domestic fundamentals. Over weeks the fundamentals reassert, but over a day the global tape wins. When a foreign storm hits, the most foreign-sensitive corners fall first, here metals and IT.

## Card 3 — The biggest gainer (Cohance, the pharma wave)
- numlabel: COHANCE · top gainer
- number: +13% · cls: up
- headline: Pharma led the day
- why: Cohance jumped about 13%, the day's biggest gainer, as pharma was the only sector to rise, to a record. The US FDA is short of a cancer drug, ifosfamide, and asked Indian firms to supply it, lifting Piramal about 10% too. A weaker rupee helps their exports.

## Card 4 — The biggest fall (Vedanta)
- numlabel: VEDANTA · biggest fall
- number: −8% · cls: down
- headline: A supply event, not a fundamental
- why: Vedanta fell about 8%, the day's biggest single-stock drop, but not on bad results. Its promoter sold a 2,149 crore rupee block of shares at 292 rupees. A large insider sell-down mechanically pushes the price lower regardless of the business. The question for holders is simply whether more selling follows.

## Card 5 — What to watch + link
