## Card 1 — Market (what + why)
- numlabel: NIFTY 50 · 24,021
- number: +0.83% to 24,021 · cls: up
- headline: A recovery, on cheaper oil
- why: The Nifty rose 0.83% and erased Tuesday's fall. The driver was the price of oil collapsing, which eased the inflation worry and pulled bond yields down, lifting the banks and real estate. Foreign investors actually kept selling, but domestic funds bought far more and carried the day.

## Card 2 — The WHY (the durable concept)
- kicker: Why it matters
- headline: One cheaper input moves the whole economy
- why: India buys about 85% of its oil from abroad, so oil is the single price that touches everything. When the war premium drains out of crude, the import bill shrinks, inflation eases, the rupee firms and bond yields fall, all at once. That is why the rupee gained today even as the dollar rose. This is far more consequential than the 0.83% on the screen, and it plays out over months.

## Card 3 — The biggest gainers (textiles)
- numlabel: TEXTILES · top movers
- number: +8 to +15% · cls: up
- headline: An upgrade lit up textiles
- why: The day's biggest gainers were textile exporters. The brokerage Motilal Oswal began coverage of the sector and argued it has turned a corner after years of weak exports, sending KPR Mill, S.P. Apparels, Gokaldas and Indo Count up sharply. When a respected brokerage re-rates a whole sector, money chases every name in it.

## Card 4 — The biggest fall (the expensive corner)
- numlabel: POWER EQUIPMENT · biggest fall
- number: −6 to −7% · cls: down
- headline: The priciest stocks got sold first
- why: A cluster of high-flying power-equipment names fell together with no bad news on any of them. They had each run up 40 to 120% in months, to valuations with no room for error (one trades near 150 times earnings). On a day money moved into cheaper stocks, the most expensive corner was the first thing investors sold.

## Card 5 — What to watch + link
