India's biggest private banks let the market down on the one number that matters most for a lender: their margin on loans. HDFC Bank and Axis Bank each fell about 5% after June-quarter results showed shrinking net interest margins, and that one theme dragged the Nifty down 0.39% to 24,238 on Monday.

But money did not leave, it rotated. State banks jumped 2.78% as Punjab National Bank's profit more than tripled, up 213%; pharma rose, and domestic funds fully absorbed the foreign selling. The fear gauge fell, so this was orderly, not a crash. The cleanest gain was D.P. Abhushan, up 7.36% on a Q1 jewellery beat.

The real signal: as the RBI cuts rates, loan yields reset faster than deposit costs, so the spread narrows across the whole large private-bank pack, not just two names. Watch Infosys on Wednesday and the US tariff cliff on Thursday.
