## Card 1 - Market (what + why)
- numlabel: NIFTY 50
- number: 24,005.85 · +0.59%   [brief line 8]   · cls: up
- headline: A green day, split in two
- why: The Nifty rose 0.59% to 24,005.85 on Wednesday, but the calm number hid a two-speed session. Money left technology and moved into domestic-facing shares. The gap between the best sector and the worst was more than five percentage points on a day the index barely rose.

## Card 2 - Sharpest fall (+why)
- numlabel: KPIT TECH
- number: −16.98%   [brief line 77, 98]   · cls: down
- headline: IT's first revenue drop in years
- why: KPIT, which builds software for carmakers, warned its June-quarter dollar revenue will fall about 1% from a year earlier, its first decline in 23 quarters, as European carmakers pulled back after their own profit warnings. The stock hit a 52-week low and pulled Nifty IT down 2.01% to a fresh three-year low, the day's only red sector.

## Card 3 - Top riser / where the money went (+why)
- numlabel: NIFTY REALTY
- number: +3.58%   [brief line 40, 102]   · cls: up
- headline: Where the money went
- why: As IT sold off, cash rotated into domestic sectors. Realty led at +3.58%, with consumer goods up 2.08% and autos up 1.15%. The fuel was cheaper oil, Brent is down about 30% this quarter and Hindustan Unilever said its input costs should ease, plus a record June GST take of ₹1,94,812 crore and strong June car and two-wheeler sales.

## Card 4 - The consequential macro (unsigned trade deal)
- kicker: Why the market rose
- headline: A US trade deal, not yet signed
- why: The bigger reason for Wednesday's optimism: India and the US are close to an interim trade deal. Commerce Minister Piyush Goyal calls the talks "very close" and the US ambassador says only about 1% is left. But nothing is signed, and a hard deadline looms. The temporary 10% US tariff on Indian goods expires on 24 July. This is hope, not a done deal.

## Card 5 - What to watch + link
- kicker: What to watch · Thursday
- headline: TCS earnings and the tariff clock
- why: Two dates now matter. TCS reports on 9 July, the first real test of whether the IT selloff is justified. And the India-US trade deadline is 24 July, still unsigned. Nearer term, watch for a Bay-of-Bengal system around 3 July to revive a monsoon that closed June about 40% short. Full brief on Substack, podcast on Spotify and Apple.
