- headline: Defence stocks re-rated

## Card 2 — The teaching idea (why a milestone re-rates a cohort)
- kicker: Why this matters
- headline: One number moved a sector
- why: No single company won a fresh order. This was a production figure showing the whole make-in-India defence base is compounding, up 15.6% in a year. When the growth belongs to the sector and not one firm, the market re-rates every maker together, which is why the index moved. The caution: much of this cohort has already run very hard, so a lot of that growth is in the price.

## Card 3 — Earnings vs momentum (+why)
- numlabel: TRENT
- number: +7.07%   [brief line 63, 81]   · cls: up
- headline: A real beat, re-rated late
- why: Trent runs Westside and Zudio, and its Q4 result beat: revenue up about 20% to ₹4,937 crore, profit up 30% to ₹455 crore, margin 18.6% against roughly 16% expected. Wednesday was the market re-rating that beat, not fresh news. Earnings reward you slowly, momentum fast.

## Card 4 — The honest catch (+why)
- kicker: The catch
- headline: Foreign buyers barely showed up
- why: The rally is real but it is domestically carried. On Wednesday foreign investors bought just ₹102 crore of Indian shares while domestic funds bought ₹1,561 crore, about fifteen times as much. The foreign buy-turn has held, not yet firmed, so the next prints decide whether this is a real recovery or a domestic relief bounce.
