Indian shares fell for a fourth straight day on Thursday, and the cause was the one behind the whole slide: oil. Fresh tanker attacks near the Strait of Hormuz and in the Red Sea pushed Brent crude past $100 a barrel, up about 7%. The Nifty closed 0.53% lower at 23,869.60. For a country that imports most of its oil, that lands straight on the import bill and the rupee, which sat near its weakest level ever.

But India held up better than the rest of the world. Even as US and Asian markets were sold hard, domestic funds bought almost exactly what foreign investors sold, roughly 2,900 crore each way, soaking up the entire foreign exit.

The movers: Tanla rose about 10% on a clean quarter, one of the few large green names. Control Print fell the hardest, down about 13% after quarterly profit dropped 42%. The one idea worth keeping: one oil shock hit the market in three separate places, pushing state fuel retailers into losses, tipping IndiGo to a quarterly loss, and pressing the rupee near its weakest ever.

What to watch: Infosys reports after the close with a trimmed outlook, and the US tariff deadline on Indian goods both land Friday.
