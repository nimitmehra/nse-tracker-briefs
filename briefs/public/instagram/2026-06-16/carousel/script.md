## Card 1 — Market (what + why)
- numlabel: NIFTY 50
- number: +0.98% to 23,853.90   [brief line 8, 16]   · cls: up
- headline: One deal moved the whole market
- why: Over the weekend the US and Iran signed a deal to reopen the Strait of Hormuz, the world's busiest oil shipping lane. That sent oil sharply lower, and since India imports about 85% of its crude, cheaper oil eases inflation and leaves the Reserve Bank more room to cut rates. Money rotated into rate-sensitive sectors and lifted the Nifty, with risers beating fallers about four to one.

## Card 2 — The other force (oil, the day's spine)
- numlabel: BRENT CRUDE
- number: $82.73, down 5.27%   [brief line 9, 16, 32]   · cls: flat
- headline: Why cheaper oil is good news for India
- why: India buys most of its oil from abroad, so the price of crude sets the bill the country pays for fuel, freight and inflation. When Brent fell to a three-month low on the Hormuz reopening, that whole bill got lighter at once. The catch sits underneath: oil is still about $16 above its pre-conflict level, because the lane is signed open but not yet physically clear.

## Card 3 — Top riser (+why)
- numlabel: AARTI INDUSTRIES
- number: +13.06%   [brief line 56, 65]   · cls: up
- headline: The day's sharpest riser
- why: Aarti Industries is a specialty-chemicals maker, and it led the board after disclosing long-dated contract wins, a multi-year backward-integration tie-up and a roughly $150m supply deal running to 2030. Long contracts let a company re-rate because they make future revenue more visible, and cheaper crude is an added tailwind since oil is a feedstock for chemicals.

## Card 4 — Second riser (the trade-deal lesson)
- numlabel: BALKRISHNA INDUSTRIES
- number: +8.91%   [brief line 57, 72]   · cls: up
- headline: The textbook trade-deal winner
- why: Balkrishna is India's largest exporter of off-highway tyres, the kind used on tractors and mining gear, and US tariffs had frozen its American shipments. The weekend de-escalation directly reverses that export overhang, and cheaper crude lowers its rubber and carbon-black costs, which run 65 to 70% of what each tyre costs to make. One headline fixed both the demand side and the cost side.

## Card 5 — What to watch + link
- kicker: What to watch · this week
- headline: Signed is not the same as done
- why: The deal is signed but not implemented. Clearing mines from the Strait of Hormuz is assessed at 40 to 50 days, so watch Brent as the live gauge of whether the relief holds, the formal Geneva signing on Friday June 19, and the US Federal Reserve decision on June 16 to 17 that sets the global rate mood.
