- headline: A narrow green day
- why: The index closed higher, but nearly all the gain came from one corner. Nifty IT jumped 4.64% with all ten members green, bouncing off a three-year low. Banks sat flat and PSU banks fell, so one sector doing the lifting turned a 4.64% IT move into only a 0.71% index gain.

## Card 2 — Top riser (+why)
- numlabel: CANTABIL
- number: +11.33%   [brief line 60, 71]   · cls: up
- headline: A retailer on new stores
- why: Cantabil sells apparel through its own stores, so its growth is tied to how many it runs. A 30 June filing flagged five new stores in June for 667 total, and reiterated FY27 guidance of about ₹1,000 cr revenue at 28 to 30% margins. More stores means more selling floor, though the share pop ran ahead of the news.

## Card 3 — Sharpest fall (+why)
- numlabel: SAKSOFT
- number: −7.75%   [brief line 65, 81]   · cls: down
- headline: A small-cap IT name gives back
- why: Saksoft was the only red IT stock on a day the sector bounced. It had run up about 45% over three months, and money moved out of extended small-cap IT to lock in that gain. No bad news behind it, just profit-booking after a big run.

## Card 4 — The interesting thing
- kicker: The signal under the rally
- headline: The rupee at a record low
- why: The rupee slipped past 95.4 to the dollar, a fresh record low, even though the dollar was weakening worldwide and oil stayed cheap. Both of those usually lift the rupee. When a currency falls against a falling dollar, the usual cause is money leaving the country, and it showed up: foreign investors sold Indian shares a net ₹312 cr, a fifth straight day of outflows.
