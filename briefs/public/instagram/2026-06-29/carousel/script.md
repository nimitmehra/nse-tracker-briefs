## Card 1 — The oil shock that didn't arrive (explainer cover)
- numlabel: NIFTY 50
- number: 23,946 · −0.46%   [brief line 8]   · cls: down
- headline: The oil shock that did not arrive
- why: India spent the Muharram long weekend braced for a crude spike, after US and Iranian strikes near the Strait of Hormuz. It reopened Monday and slipped just 0.46% to 23,946. A measured dip, not a crisis. Here is what happened and why.

## Card 2 — Why the feared chain never fired
- numlabel: BRENT
- number: ~$72.80 · +1%   [brief line 9]   · cls: flat
- headline: Why the crisis did not land
- why: On Sunday the US and Iran agreed to halt the fighting and let ships through the strait. So oil rose only about 1% to near a four-month low instead of spiking. India imports about 85% of its oil, so a spike would have lifted the import bill and weakened the rupee. None of that fired. The rupee held flat at 94.53.

## Card 3 — The real story was defensive
- numlabel: NIFTY PHARMA
- number: +1.03%   [brief line 8]   · cls: up
- headline: Money moved to safety
- why: Under a calm headline sat a defensive shuffle. Money moved into pharma and healthcare and out of IT and auto. Pharma led at +1.03%, helped by the US extending its pause on new drug tariffs to 9 July, which eases the overhang on Indian generic exporters. When a reopen feels uncertain, money parks in the steadier corners first.

## Card 4 — The auto fall was really a policy story
- numlabel: NIFTY AUTO
- number: −2.08%   [brief line 8]   · cls: down
- headline: Not oil. Policy.
- why: Auto was the worst sector at −2.08% and looked like an oil casualty. Pull it apart and it splits in two. Delhi approved EV Policy 2.0, with a ₹30,000 subsidy per electric two-wheeler and a planned ban on new petrol and CNG two-wheelers from 2028. Ather, an electric-scooter maker, jumped 8.5%, while legacy petrol makers were sold. Policy is picking winners inside the pack.

## Card 5 — What to watch
- kicker: What to watch · Tuesday
- headline: Expiry, quarter-end, and oil
- why: Tuesday 30 June stacks the Nifty and Bank Nifty monthly options expiry on top of June quarter-end, so expect heavier positioning. The monsoon stays the home-grown worry, with June the driest in about 146 years. And Brent is defused, not closed, so oil is still the swing risk if the truce frays.
