# Carousel Script — 2026-06-20 (story: Friday 19-Jun IST close)

**Caption:** Indian IT cracked on Friday and it was an imported shock, Accenture cut its full-year forecast overnight in New York and Infosys fell 8.19% to a five-year low, dragging the Nifty down 0.64% to snap a five-day streak. Inside: why one US warning re-rated the whole sector in a morning, the real reason behind it (AI is moving tech budgets, not growing them), the puzzle that foreigners net-bought ₹4,859 crore on a down day, and the record IPO pipeline now building. ## Card 1 — Market (what + why)
- numlabel: NIFTY 50
- number: −0.64% to 24,013   [brief line 8, 18]   · cls: down
- headline: IT crash snaps the streak
- why: One US warning broke before India opened. Accenture cut its full-year revenue forecast and missed its quarter, and its New York shares fell about 18%, the worst day ever. That hit Indian IT the same session, Nifty IT closed down 3.65% after sinking roughly 6% intraday, dragging the market lower and ending a five-day winning run.

## Card 2 — Sharpest fall (+why)
- numlabel: INFOSYS
- number: −8.19%   [brief line 89, 111]   · cls: down
- headline: A five-year low, no own news
- why: Infosys is India's largest software exporter, so it is the most direct read-through to Accenture's warning, and it fell even harder than its own sector to a roughly five-year low near ₹1,035. There was no Infosys-specific bad news. When a cohort trades as one company's proxy, a warning re-rates all of them at once, before any has even reported.

## Card 3 — The WHY (the durable concept)
- kicker: Why it bit
- headline: AI is reallocating, not expanding
- why: The detail that matters is in Accenture's own words. It did not say AI demand is weak, it said companies are moving their tech budgets into AI rather than growing them. That is a worry about the size of the whole spending pie, not a one-quarter stumble, which is much harder to shrug off. The real test is Indian IT's own July-quarter commentary.

## Card 4 — The catch (foreigners bought a falling market)
- kicker: The puzzle
- headline: Foreigners bought a down day
- why: Foreign investors were net buyers of ₹4,859 crore of Indian shares even as the market fell, while domestic funds sold ₹1,160 crore. No contradiction, they did two things at once, bought non-IT large-caps as money rotated out of IT, and sold about 2.26 lakh Nifty futures as a hedge. Net cash buying plus a futures short is cautious and hedged, not conviction.

## Card 5 — What to watch + link
- kicker: What to watch · next week
- headline: A record IPO pipeline, and the rain
- why: Two record filings landed in one week, Jio Platforms' roughly ₹38,000 crore all-fresh issue (to repay debt, not cash out) and the NSE's ₹30,000 crore sale, lifting New India Assurance 13%. Heavy new supply competes for the same money. Also watch the monsoon, 41% below normal after an El Niño flag, and a four-day week before the Muharram holiday.
