## Card 2 — Why IT fell so hard
- numlabel: NIFTY IT
- number: −2.73%   [brief line 8]   · cls: down
- headline: Large-cap IT sold off
- why: Nifty IT dropped 2.73% to a multi-year low, with Infosys and TCS both hitting fresh 52-week lows. The trigger was Accenture's weak revenue guidance last week, plus a growing fear that artificial intelligence is shrinking the outsourcing work these firms get paid for. But the broad market was green, so this was a single-sector re-pricing, not a broad selloff.

## Card 3 — The tell that proves it is India's own problem
- numlabel: NIFTY IT
- number: −2.73%   [brief line 8]   · cls: down
- headline: The tell most recaps miss
- why: Indian IT fell even though two things that usually help dollar-earners went its way. The US Nasdaq closed green, and the rupee softened, giving exporters more rupees per dollar. When a sector ignores both of its usual tailwinds and still drops, the weakness is fundamental. This is about India's own IT demand, not global mood or currency.

## Card 4 — One EV policy split the auto pack
- kicker: What the rule means
- headline: Delhi EV policy picks winners
- why: Delhi approved a new EV policy effective 1 July, with subsidies on electric two-wheelers and a halt on new petrol and CNG two-wheeler registrations from 2028. Electric-scooter makers jumped, Ola Electric up 8.4% and Ather up 5.2%. The same policy hit Eicher Motors, the maker of Royal Enfield, down about 4.75% on its legacy petrol bikes. One rule, picking winners and losers inside autos.

## Card 5 — What to watch
- kicker: What to watch · Wednesday
- headline: Domestic money, and Q1 earnings ahead
- why: Domestic funds bought 6,842 crore rupees and more than absorbed foreign quarter-end selling of 2,557 crore rupees. Q1 earnings open in mid-July, with TCS first on 9 July, the first real test of whether the IT selloff is justified. And Brent closed the quarter down about 30%, a steady tailwind for an oil importer.
