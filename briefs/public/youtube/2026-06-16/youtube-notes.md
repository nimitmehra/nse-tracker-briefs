One signed deal powered the whole session. Over the weekend the United States and Iran signed a 14-point deal to end their roughly 107-day war and reopen the Strait of Hormuz, the world's busiest oil lane. That sent Brent crude down 5.27% to $82.73, a three-month low. For India, which imports about 85% of its crude, cheaper oil eases the import bill, the current-account gap and fuel-driven inflation all at once, which widens the room the Reserve Bank has to keep cutting rates. So the money rotated into rate-sensitive sectors and lifted the Nifty 50 to a 0.98% gain at 23,853.90, with risers beating fallers about four to one. The one catch: the deal is signed but not yet implemented, with Hormuz mine-clearance assessed at 40 to 50 days before tankers transit confidently.
