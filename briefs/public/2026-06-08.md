## The Lead

Iran fired a missile barrage at northern Israel on Sunday night, its first since the April ceasefire, and that pushed crude back up and triggered a risk-off mood across Asian markets on Monday. Brent rose 1.76% to about $94.73, which matters for India because it imports most of its oil. Indian shares fell with the region, the Nifty closing down 1.04% at 23,123, but the fall was contained: every major Asian market dropped far harder. The damage was concentrated in North Asia's chip-and-AI stocks, a trade India barely owns, so this was an imported sell-off, not an India problem.

---

## Global Setup

The session was set overnight and through Asian hours, not by Wall Street. Iran's Sunday-night strike spiked crude and gold (+0.47% to ~$4,357), and Asia opened heavy. The standout was North Asia's tech wreck: Korea's KOSPI crashed 8.29%, Japan's Nikkei fell 3.85% and Taiwan's TAIEX 3.48% — the delayed digestion of Friday's US AI-chip rout, made worse by a yen-carry unwind as Japan's 10-year yield ticked up. China was softer too (Shanghai −1.70%, Hang Seng −1.22%). Against all of that, India's −1.04% was the gentlest fall in Asia. One timing note that shapes tomorrow, not today: US markets recovered after India shut (Nasdaq +1.2%, S&P +0.8%) and the US 10-year eased to 4.52% — that bounce is a Tuesday input, it did not lift the Indian close.

## Macro & Policy

The macro tape moved against the inflation side of the Reserve Bank's balance, with no fresh policy action to offset it.

**On the worrying side**, the crude re-spike pushes directly on two of the three risks the RBI named at Friday's meeting — energy prices and the monsoon. Brent up near $95 lifts India's import bill and feeds fuel inflation. The monsoon firmed up the second worry: the weather office confirmed a below-normal season at about 90% of the long-period average, with roughly 60% odds of a deficient year and El Niño expected to develop. The rains also arrived late (Kerala on 4 June against a 1 June normal). Both are exactly the food-and-energy inflation tail the RBI flagged when it raised its FY27 inflation forecast to 5.1%.

**On the steadier side**, the rupee held at ₹95.71 despite the crude spike and the risk-off — a quiet show of resilience, plausibly cushioned by Friday's foreign-bond tax package and the RBI's backstop, though we record that as observation, not confirmed intervention. The bond market was calm: the 10-year yield eased 2.6 basis points to 6.95% on a small flight-to-quality bid.

**Watch next week:** three reads matter. US CPI on Wednesday 10 June (after Friday's hot jobs print, a hot reading hardens the "no Fed cuts" pressure on foreign flows). India's own CPI and IIP on Friday 12 June, the first inflation read against the RBI's new 5.1% path. And the US Fed decision on 16 June.

## The Market

It was a broad, orderly retreat — every NSE sector index closed red except a near-flat healthcare corner. The defensives held best: Pharma fell just 0.41% and Bank Nifty 0.79%, the relative shelter on a risk-off day. The damage sat in the high-beta corners — Realty was worst at −2.56% (rate-sensitive, and already the weakest sector over the past month at −9.5%), Metals −2.33% on the global cyclical washout, and Auto −1.85% as crude-cost fears hit OEMs and ancillaries. IT fell 1.23%, but that number hides a single stock: Wipro alone dropped over 8% on a mechanical buyback adjustment (more on that below).

The signal worth reading is what the fear gauge did. India VIX jumped 7.84% to 17.03 — a genuine repricing of risk, moving *with* the market rather than ahead of it, which is the mark of a controlled sell-off, not a panic. The rupee's flat hold against a softer dollar, while crude rose, says foreign money was not fleeing India specifically; this was regional contagion, and India was the side that bent least.

## The Connections

*How the day's events ripple across industries — the part most market reports skip.*

**India's small fall was the whole story, and it points to what India does and doesn't own.** It is easy to read −1% as a bad day; the real read is that India fell a fraction of its neighbours. Korea fell 8%, Japan 4%, Taiwan 3.5%, while India lost just over 1%. The epicentre was the AI-memory and semiconductor trade — chips, the names that led the global rally — and India simply does not carry much of it. So when that trade unwinds, India sits outside the blast radius. This is the mirror image of the months when the same AI trade pulled money *out* of India: the lack of chip exposure is a cushion now, just as it was a drag then. Watch whether the decoupling holds into Tuesday once India digests the US session.

**A crude-up day did not lift India's oil producers — flows beat fundamentals.** On paper, higher crude should help upstream names like Reliance and ONGC, which sell oil. It didn't: Reliance fell 2.15% and the energy sector dropped 1.64%. The risk-off selling and a separate slide in renewables and power-equipment names overwhelmed the crude tailwind. The lesson for the day: when fear is the driver, the flow wins over the fundamental. The clean crude read showed up elsewhere — IndiGo fell ~2.7% because higher jet fuel is an unambiguous cost for an airline.

**The winners told you what a nervous market is willing to pay for.** The names that rose against the day's selling were not random. CCL Products, Thyrocare, Caplin Point and Zydus Wellness are all consumer, pharma or diagnostics names with recent results or dividends behind them. On a risk-off day, money pays up for two things — proven earnings and defensiveness — and sells the high-flyers that ran on momentum (IFCI, MTAR, TD Power). The split between the gainers and the momentum-reversal fallers is the same trade read two ways.
