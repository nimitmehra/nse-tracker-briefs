## The Lead

After the close on Thursday, Tata Consultancy Services (TCS), India's largest IT company, opened the earnings season with a soft-but-steady result. Revenue rose 13.9% from a year earlier to ₹72,275 crore, but in constant-currency terms — stripping out the weaker rupee that flatters the headline — growth was just 0.4% over the previous quarter, so underlying demand was close to flat. Reported profit was ₹13,420 crore, operating margin held at 24.0% under wage-hike pressure, and new deal wins totalled US$9.5 billion. The board declared an interim dividend of ₹12 a share. Because it was filed at 15:52, after the market had closed, the share reaction comes Friday — but the read is set: cautious, not broken, tone for the whole sector into Infosys on 23 July.

## Macro & Policy

The day turned on the same force that broke Wednesday — the Iran situation — but this time in India's favour.

**On the supportive side,** the conflict partially de-escalated during the session. President Donald Trump said on the record that he does not think the US and Iran return to full-scale war and that Iran "badly" wants a deal; the US strikes that did land hit Qeshm Island and Bandar Abbas, not Tehran or nuclear sites, signalling a deliberately limited round if Strait of Hormuz traffic resumes. That drained the panic: Brent stopped rising, the rupee firmed a touch, and India VIX — the market's fear gauge — collapsed nearly 9% to 13.36. Business Standard also reported "early signs" of foreign investors returning to Indian equities after a roughly $60-billion, two-year exodus (a narrative turn to verify with the flow figures, not yet a trend).

**On the worrying side,** nothing is actually resolved. This is Trump's rhetoric, not a signed ceasefire — treat it as reported. Oil did not fall; Brent is still up about 9% on the week and gold stayed firm, so the tail-risk premium is still priced in. And the 24-July US tariff cliff on Indian goods remains an unresolved overhang two weeks out. Thursday was relief, not reversal — the Nifty is still down 0.88% on the week.

**Watch next:** the RBI's Weekly Statistical Supplement on Friday (forex-reserves draw), India's June inflation reading in mid-July (the first read after the oil scare), Infosys results on 23 July, the 24-July tariff deadline, and the RBI rate decision in early August.

## The Market

A relief bounce, led from the front by the same names that led Wednesday's fall. The Nifty 50 added 0.34% to 23,962.80 and the Sensex 0.31%, but the Bank Nifty did the heavy lifting, up 0.90% — banks had taken the worst of Wednesday's oil-shock selling, so they repaired the most on Thursday. India VIX falling nearly 9% to 13.36 is the clearest sign the panic hedge came off. The rupee firmed to ₹95.38 (+0.23%) on a flat dollar, and both foreign and domestic institutions were net buyers on the prior day's confirmed flows.

The lone red corner was IT: the Nifty IT index slipped 0.30% (Infosys −1.73%) as investors trimmed the sector ahead of TCS's after-close result — a soft cohort into a nervous result, not a fresh IT story. Pharma was the opposite lesson: the Nifty Pharma index actually rose 0.89% even though its heavyweight Dr Reddy's fell nearly 6%, which tells you the Dr Reddy's drop was a company-specific stumble, not a sector event.

One caveat on breadth: 9 of the 11 sector indices returned no clean daily figure this session (a data-feed gap), so only IT and Pharma are confirmed above. Individual stocks showed jewellery and electronics-manufacturing names up strongly, but the sector-index reads to confirm a broad move aren't available today, so we don't quantify it.

## Corporate News

- **Dr Reddy's Laboratories (DRREDDY)** — the day's marquee faller (−5.89%), and the story is in the primary filing, not spin. The company told the exchange that certain batches of **semaglutide** — the blockbuster GLP-1 weight-loss and diabetes molecule — were found out-of-specification because of an active-ingredient (API) issue, and that commercial supplies will be delayed until it finds the root cause (unavailable in India; Canada disrupted into late October). It says there is no patient-safety or regulatory-filing impact. Why it matters: Dr Reddy's is first to secure generic-semaglutide authorisation in India and Canada, so this dents a flagship growth product just as it launches — a real, non-reversible setback, not a passing wobble.

- **Swiggy (SWIGGY)** — rose 7.47% on two genuine developments. Its foreign ownership fell to 49.76% as of 6 July, pushing domestic ownership above 50% for the first time as a listed company — a step toward "Indian Owned and Controlled" status that could let its Instamart quick-commerce arm hold its own inventory, like rival Blinkit. Separately, its Food-on-Train service expanded to 180 cities, with orders roughly 3x higher year-on-year. Worth knowing: the sell-side is split — JM Financial rates it Reduce with a ₹250 target and doesn't expect the ownership transition before March 2027.

## The Connections

*Behind the session — what triggered the moves, why, and which sectors to watch next.*

**Oil de-escalated, but it did not crash — so the input-cost relief never arrived.** The obvious trade on easing tension is that cheaper oil lifts the fuel retailers, paint makers and airlines that live on it. It didn't happen, and the reason is precise: Brent *stabilised* near $78 rather than falling, and is still up about 9% on the week. Gold staying firm at $4,114 says the market is pricing "a limited round if Hormuz reopens," not "all-clear." With no actual drop in crude, there was no cost relief for oil-marketing companies, paints or aviation to price in — the non-rally is the accurate read, not an anomaly.

**The same unresolved-Iran premium that kept oil bid also lifted gold — and that fed the jewellers.** Gold rose 1.06% because the safe-haven hedge stayed on, and that is a direct revenue tailwind for jewellery retailers, whose stock is worth more and whose sales value rises with the metal. That is the thread running through the day's loudest up-cluster — Kalyan Jewellers, Tribhovandas Bhimji Zaveri and D P Abhushan all up together. Geopolitics to gold to jewellery is the chain; watch whether it holds if the Strait tension actually eases and gold gives back its premium.

**TCS's flat constant-currency number reframes the whole IT season.** The headline said +13.9% revenue growth, but almost all of it was the weaker rupee translating dollar earnings into more rupees — the underlying, constant-currency figure was +0.4%. That is the number that travels: it sets a "flat-to-soft demand, margins squeezed by wage hikes, deal wins steady" template for the cohort. Infosys, HCLTech and Wipro now report against that bar. The read into 23 July is cautious, not broken — and it is why IT was the one sector investors trimmed ahead of the result.
