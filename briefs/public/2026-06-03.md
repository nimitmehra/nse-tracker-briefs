## The Lead

A relief rally that wasn't a relief story. The Nifty rose 0.43% to snap a four-session losing streak, and 67 stocks closed up 3% or more. But the leadership was strikingly narrow: four IT names, two NSE-IPO positioning trades, one solar QIP, one FDA approval, one pre-earnings pop and one defence momentum name account for almost the entire top of the gainers list. Banks barely moved (+0.13%), FMCG and autos clustered around +0.7%, pharma fell 0.9%. The trigger came from overseas — Hewlett Packard Enterprise jumped 28-30% after-hours on $1.8 billion of fresh AI infrastructure orders, Marvell ran 18-25% on a Jensen Huang endorsement at Computex, and the S&P 500 closed at a fresh record above 7,600. Korea, Taiwan and Japan caught the wave; India joined late through one cohort, IT. With FIIs about 89% short on Nifty futures into Friday's quadruple-event day, this looks less like a sectoral revival and more like a market parked, waiting.

## Global Setup

The overnight cue was the US AI cohort doing the heavy lifting. Hewlett Packard Enterprise reported revenue up 40% YoY with $1.8 billion of fresh AI systems orders and raised full-year EPS guidance to $3.35-3.45 from $2.30-2.50; the stock ran 28-30% in after-hours trade. Marvell rallied 18-25% intraday after Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang, on stage at Computex, called it "the next trillion-dollar company." Broadcom added 5%. The headline US indices moved less — S&P 500 +0.13% to a record 7,609.78, Nasdaq +0.03%, Dow +0.45% — but the chip cohort underneath was explosive, and the Asian session ran with it: KOSPI +3.68% on the Samsung and SK Hynix AI-memory trade, TAIEX +1.35%, Nikkei +1.58%. For India, the read-through is narrow and specific: a US bid into AI infrastructure flows through to Indian IT services, helped by a weaker rupee (₹95.25, down 0.27%). The US 10-year softened to 4.45% — mildly supportive in theory, but the US-India yield spread is still around 2.6 percentage points, too tight historically to draw fresh FII money into Indian bonds.

## Macro & Policy

**On the supportive side**, Brent is still down about 12.5% over May — the heavy lift for India's import bill — and the rupee's mild weakening helped IT exporters. The Reserve Bank reportedly intervened in spot and forward markets to defend ₹94.70 intraday before letting the close go softer. Bank of Baroda's research desk pegged Q4 FY26 real GDP at 7.3%, in line with the Reserve Bank and 10 basis points below the government's 7.4% — the gap that becomes the read on Friday.

**On the worrying side**, the monsoon onset over Kerala has slipped again. IMD now expects a gradual onset between June 2 and June 4 with full strength only after June 5-6, and is warning the start will be "relatively mild" rather than a strong surge — the upper-level westerlies that mark a clean onset remain weaker than the threshold. That keeps rural-demand pressure on FMCG, fertiliser and two-wheeler cohorts into mid-June. The US-Iran 60-day framework slipped further: President Trump on June 1 sent the draft back to Iran asking for tougher uranium-disposition terms (the 440 kg highly-enriched stockpile) and a shorter Hormuz mine-clearance window. The Brent volatility premium stays embedded.

**To watch this week**, Friday June 5 is the defining session: the Reserve Bank decision around 10 AM IST (consensus a hold at 5.25% for the third straight meeting, with the median view now shifted from "next move is a cut" to "at least one hike this fiscal year" per the Business Standard poll), Q4 FY26 + full-year GDP from MoSPI at the same window, Governor Sanjay Malhotra's press conference at noon, and US Non-Farm Payrolls at 6 PM IST. Before that, India-US Bilateral Trade Agreement Round 1 is in Day 2 of a three-day session in Delhi (correcting yesterday's framing — the round is three days, not four — communique most likely Thursday evening). Day 3 of the ALMM List-II solar mandate and Day 3 of SEBI's 50:50 cash-equivalent margin rule both continue without dislocation flags.

## The Market

The session's defining feature was leadership concentration. Nifty IT closed up 4.23% — the only sector index materially above the headline. Everything else clustered between +0.8% and −0.9%. The lagging Bank Nifty (+0.13%) is the most revealing read — banks did not bid in sympathy with the global risk-on, fitting the pattern of a market saving conviction for Friday's Reserve Bank decision rather than chasing an overseas-driven rally.

The rupee weakened 0.27% to ₹95.255 even though the dollar index was flat (DXY −0.06%). When the dollar doesn't move but the rupee softens, the move is India-specific — and on Tuesday that was the FX mechanic translating the US AI rally into Indian IT (a weaker rupee lifts dollar-earning exporters' realisations). India VIX dropped 7.16% to 15.36 — fear came out, conviction did not return. FIIs net sold ₹8,363 crore in cash (heavier than Monday's ₹3,912 crore) and the net short on Nifty futures deepened to about 2,30,096 contracts. The long-short ratio sits at a three-month low of 0.11, meaning roughly 89% of foreign open positions in Nifty futures are short. Any positive surprise from Friday's events risks a short-squeeze. Domestic institutions absorbed.

Two non-moves are worth flagging. Oil marketing companies did not bid even though Brent is down about 12.5% over May — regulator-capped marketing margins are holding the input-cost relief as excise rather than letting it flow to refiner P&L. And rate-sensitive NBFCs and real estate did not extend even though the US 10-year fell 45 basis points — domestic yields are already priced for a Friday hold and the market wants the confirmation. Both non-moves point to the same thing: investors parking until Friday.

## The Connections

*The cross-sector reads — what triggered, why, and which sectors next.*

**Broad breadth concealed narrow leadership — and the narrowness matters more than the breadth.** Sixty-seven stocks closed up 3% or more, which sounds like a sectoral revival. But the top of the gainers list is mechanically four overlapping cohort trades: IT services on the overnight US AI rally (TCS, HEXT, NEWGEN, INFOBEAN), the NSE IPO positioning basket (JMFINANCIL, IFCI), one solar QIP (ACMESOLAR) and one FDA approval (CONCORDBIO), plus a pre-earnings positioning trade (SKYGOLD) and one defence-momentum name (ROSSTECH). Zero FMCG names, zero banks, zero autos, zero realty — pharma only through the single-stock FDA event. The diagnostic: a single-catalyst (US AI cascade) and positioning-driven (NSE IPO basket) relief rally, not a multi-sector re-rating. The lagging Bank Nifty (+0.13%) is the cleanest signal.

**The FII short-positioning sets up an asymmetric Friday.** Foreign investors are running about 89% short on Nifty futures — a three-month low in the long-short ratio. The cash side keeps selling (₹8,363 crore Tuesday). For Friday's quadruple-event day, this skews to upside if surprises break favourably: a dovish Reserve Bank tone, a GDP reading at the 7.4% government number rather than the 7.3% consensus, or a soft US Non-Farm Payrolls reading. Any triggers mechanical short-covering. The flip side — hawkish RBI, a GDP miss, or a hot US payrolls reading — would extend the down move without long positioning to absorb it. The market is leveraged short into a defining session.

**The monsoon delay compounds rural-demand pressure even as crude stays cheap.** A delayed and "relatively mild" Kerala onset means the agriculture-dependent demand cycle has less runway than the textbook calendar suggests. Fertiliser makers face the same paradox as last week — input costs softer (cheaper crude, easier sulphur supply) but output demand worse. Two-wheeler makers and tractor manufacturers face direct demand risk. For FMCG, the rural half of the volume mix does not get a Brent-decline tailwind. Oil marketing companies are a parallel paradox: cheaper crude does not flow to BPCL/HPCL/IOC because the government is collecting the relief as excise. The data point worth tracking next is IMD's first national progress map, due in five-to-seven days.
