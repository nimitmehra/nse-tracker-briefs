## Macro & Policy

The week's macro story breaks cleanly into three buckets.

**On the supportive side**, the US-Iran 60-day ceasefire framework reached draft stage over the weekend. Defence Secretary Pete Hegseth on Saturday: "any deal will be a good deal." Axios on Sunday: an announcement could come, though it is not finalised and could still fall apart. The framework includes a 60-day cessation, the Strait of Hormuz reopened with no tolls, US sanctions waivers, possible release of $12 billion in frozen Iranian assets, and IAEA-coordinated enriched-uranium destruction. The catch: Trump separately said he is "in no hurry" to sign. Second positive — China's Caixin Manufacturing PMI for May came in at 52.2 versus 50.8 prior and 51.0 expected, the strongest since 2020. For Indian metal names (NMDC, Tata Steel, JSW Steel, Hindalco) this is a marginal positive on iron-ore and steel demand. Third — India-US Bilateral Trade Agreement talks open in New Delhi today, a 4-day session. India is offering tariff cuts on agricultural inputs, tree nuts, processed fruits, wines and spirits. Any communique by Thursday will re-price the agri, chemicals and auto trade-policy backdrop.

**On the worrying side**, the monsoon forecast stays at 90% of long-period average with rising El Niño risk — unchanged from Friday, no fresh IMD bulletin over the weekend. Cumulative foreign-investor selling for the calendar year now stands at roughly ₹2.25 lakh crore — already past 2025's full-year figure. Petrol and diesel were hiked roughly ₹5 a litre each since mid-May, complicating the inflation-relief story from cheaper crude; economists expect 30-40 bps of pass-through.

**To watch this week**, Friday June 5 is now a quadruple-event day — the Reserve Bank's MPC rate decision at 10 AM IST (consensus: hold at 5.25% for the third meeting, watch the FY27 inflation-forecast revision), India's Q4 FY26 GDP and FY26 annual GDP from MoSPI on the same morning (RBI's prior projection 6.5% Q4 / 7.3% FY26), Governor Sanjay Malhotra's press conference around noon, and US Non-Farm Payrolls at 6 PM IST. Four big events inside eight hours.

One sectoral policy item kicks in today — India's ALMM List-II rule on solar PV cells becomes operative. Solar projects bid after 31 August 2025 must now source domestic cells. Structural tailwind for Waaree Energies, Premier Energies, Reliance's solar line and Tata Power's solar arm; headwind for import-dependent EPCs. If the domestic solar names don't rise materially today on the operative date, that non-move is itself a signal.

## The Market

Friday's tape was a layered sell-off, not a one-line story. The mechanical driver was MSCI's quarterly Standard Index rebalance, which took effect at the close. MSCI is the index that large foreign funds passively track — when it changes its India weights, those funds aren't choosing; they HAVE to sell and buy to match the new basket. The reshuffle pulled Federal Bank, MCX, NALCO and Indian Bank IN, and pushed Hyundai Motor India, Jubilant FoodWorks, Kalyan Jewellers and RVNL OUT. India's overall weight slipped slightly to ~12.3% from 12.4%. Nuvama estimated this triggered roughly $800 million to $1 billion of passive India selling in a single window. Federal Bank was the biggest inflow beneficiary (~$491 million); Hyundai the biggest outflow (~$281 million).

Stacked on top: F&O monthly expiry, which normally settles on the last Thursday but had to shift to Friday because Thursday was Bakri Eid. Two mechanical events on the same session. Add late-Friday wires that Vice-President Vance sounded less certain on Iran, plus the IMD monsoon cut — and you get sectors red across the board: Metal −2.02%, Auto −1.96%, Consumption −1.85%, Energy −1.58%, FMCG −1.51%, Pharma −1.50%. The single green corner was Nifty IT at +0.6%. Worth noting: a stronger rupee actually HURTS IT (they earn in dollars, spend in rupees), so Friday's IT bid was pure flight-to-safety into big, cash-rich exporters — not a currency story.

The rupee's 1.1% jump on a flat dollar was the day's clearest India-specific signal — RBI Governor Malhotra's "rupee appears undervalued" remarks plus Brent collapse plus Iran-deal hopes. India VIX rose 8% but stayed contained at 16.19 — orderly retreat, not panic. FIIs net sold ₹21,106 crore; CYTD tally now ~₹2.25 lakh crore.

## The Connections

*The cross-sector reads — what happened in one corner that matters elsewhere.*

**The MSCI rebalance worked like a forced trade, not a vote.** When MSCI changes its index, foreign passive funds have no choice — their charters force them to match the new basket on the day. The names being ADDED — Federal Bank, MCX, NALCO, Indian Bank — received the buy-side of that flow. The names being DROPPED — Hyundai Motor India, Jubilant FoodWorks, Kalyan Jewellers, RVNL — took the sell-side. None of this is a judgement on the companies; it's mechanical re-weighting. For the Indian retail investor: don't read Friday's moves on these eight stocks as fundamental signals. The flow is one-off and the next session's price will tell you whether the market thinks the new weights are wrong.

**Brent fell, but India's OMCs didn't celebrate.** Cheaper crude SHOULD help oil marketing companies — they import the input, sell the output, lower input cost equals fatter margin. But ONGC fell 3.2% Friday and Reliance fell 2.2%; over the month Brent is down 17% and OMCs have not rallied. The reason is the petrol/diesel price hikes — roughly ₹5 per litre since mid-May. Government is keeping retail prices up while crude falls, which means the margin relief isn't reaching shareholders — it's being collected by the exchequer as excise. For an investor in BPCL, HPCL or IOC, regulator-capped marketing margins are the binding constraint, not the crude price.

**The North-Asia AI-memory trade is accelerating into Monday, not stalling.** KOSPI +4.52% to a fresh record this morning, Samsung +3% to an all-time high, TAIEX +1.74%. Year-to-date, KOSPI is up ~90%; Taiwan's m-cap has just passed India to become the world's fifth-largest. Samsung plus SK Hynix together make up ~42% of Korea's index; TSMC is roughly 40% of Taiwan's. This is hyper-concentrated capital rotating into a single global theme (AI infrastructure → memory chips → foundries). India's structural problem is that it sits on the wrong side — no listed AI-memory equivalent — and the cumulative ₹2.25 lakh crore FPI outflow this calendar year reflects exactly that flow. Friday's Indian sell-off was partly mechanical, but it sits inside this larger pull.

**Glenmark's AbbVie deal sets up a pharma cohort question.** $700 million in immediate cash for an out-licensed bispecific antibody is the largest such transaction by an Indian pharma in 2026. For the cohort — Dr Reddy's, Sun Pharma, Lupin, Cipla, Aurobindo — the question is which other Indian pipelines have an out-licensing option. If the market re-rates Glenmark on the deal substance, expect cohort interest in names with discovery-stage assets. If Glenmark only gets a one-day pop, the read is that Indian pharma is still being valued as a generic-formulations cohort regardless of innovation news.
