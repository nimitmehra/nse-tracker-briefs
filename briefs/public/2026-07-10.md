## The Lead

Indian shares rose for a second straight session on Friday, with the Nifty 50 up 1.02% to 24,206.90 and the Sensex adding 828 points, as a strong start to the June-quarter earnings season pulled money back into IT, bank and property stocks. Tata Consultancy Services' results the night before read well for the whole software sector, and solid quarterly numbers from state-run banks, led by Indian Bank, opened the season on a confident note. With the Iran and oil scare still fading, the fear gauge India VIX fell another 8.3% to 12.25, close to a one-year low. One caveat matters: this was a domestic-money rally, because Indian institutions bought a net ₹2,058 crore while foreign investors were marginal net sellers.

## Global Setup

The global backdrop into Friday was calm and mildly supportive. The overnight US close was firm (the S&P 500 and Nasdaq both higher on Thursday night India time), setting a steady tone for the open, and crude stayed quiet — Brent flat at $76.25, still down about 17% over the month as the Iran risk premium keeps draining. Gold eased to $4,115 and the dollar was flat. The one global cloud is US interest rates: the 10-year Treasury yield ticked up to 4.57% ahead of a US inflation reading due Tuesday, a mild headwind India shrugged off. Two honesty notes: clean same-day figures for the big Asian markets weren't captured this run (the feed stamped them to the prior day), so we don't fold Friday's Asia into the drivers; and US markets trading Friday night, after India had closed, are Monday's input, not Friday's.

## Macro & Policy

The day's macro picture leaned supportive, with two slower-burning worries sitting underneath it.

**On the supportive side,** the Reserve Bank's Weekly Statistical Supplement (released ~5 PM Friday) showed foreign-exchange reserves *rose* $7.26 billion to $674.19 billion in the week to 3 July, reversing the prior week's draw. That is the opposite of a rupee-defence signal — it points to reserve accretion (both a gold revaluation and a genuine dollar-asset build), not the central bank spending reserves to prop up the currency. Alongside, the Iran and oil premium kept draining: Brent stayed flat near $76, and India VIX fell to 12.25, telling you the market has largely priced the event risk out.

**On the worrying side,** the weather turned into a fresh overhang. The India Meteorological Department's July outlook now sees nationwide rainfall below-normal (under 94% of the long-period average), dragged by weak low-pressure systems and evolving El Niño conditions, with the north-west and east spared but much of the rest short of rain. A weaker monsoon threatens rural demand, reservoir storage and food prices — the thread to watch for consumer, fertiliser and agri-input names. The second, bigger clock is trade: the temporary 10% US tariff on Indian goods expires on 24 July, and while Commerce Minister Piyush Goyal and the US trade office called their two-day review "substantial progress," nothing is signed — treat it as active negotiation, not a done deal.

**Watch next:** the US June inflation reading on Tuesday (which sets the tone for global yields), India's own June CPI due mid-July (the first read after the oil scare, with the monsoon adding food-price sensitivity), and the RBI's next rate decision in early August.

## The Market

The headline gain understates what actually happened, which was money moving back into corners that had been beaten down. The Bank Nifty did the heavy lifting, up 1.39%, on a run of strong state-run-bank results, and IT went from Thursday's lone red sector to Friday's rally leader, the Nifty IT index up 1.96% on the read-through from TCS's numbers. Property was the single best corner — the Nifty Realty index rose 3.49%, its top-performing sector, on earnings anticipation and fresh constructive brokerage notes. Breadth was strongly positive, with roughly 2,756 stocks rising against 1,316 falling.

The rupee firmed about half a percent to ₹95.37 even though the dollar was flat, an India-specific bid that fits the softer-oil, steady-reserves backdrop. The signal in the flows, though, is that this was home-grown: domestic institutions bought a net ₹2,058 crore while foreign investors were small net sellers (−₹533 crore). So the multi-week story of "foreign money returning to India" did not drive Friday — Indian money did. One data caveat: 9 of the roughly 11 sector indices returned no clean daily figure this session (a feed gap), so only IT and Realty are quantified with confidence above.

## Earnings

The day's notable results, biggest companies first — what they reported, regardless of how the share moved:

- **Indian Bank (INDIANB, market value ₹1.17 lakh crore)** — a clean Q1 beat that opened the PSU-bank season strongly. Net profit rose 10% year-on-year to ₹3,273 crore, net interest income grew 17% to ₹7,435 crore, and operating profit was up about 17% — but the standout was asset quality, with gross bad loans falling 115 basis points to 1.86% from 3.01%. Double-digit profit and credit growth plus a sharp clean-up is exactly the profile the market wanted from a state lender. The stock jumped 9.78% (its move is in Big Movers).
- **Bank of Maharashtra (MAHABANK, ₹64,809 crore)** — the second PSU-bank beat of the day. Net profit rose 27% year-on-year to ₹2,020 crore, net interest income was up 14.5% to ₹3,770 crore, and the cost-to-income ratio improved to about 35%. Landing the same session as Indian Bank, it turned two data points into a cohort signal. The shares added 2.73%, a modest reaction after a recent run.
- **Anand Rathi Wealth (ANANDRATHI, ₹35,592 crore)** — a strong wealth-manager result (reported 9 July): profit jumped 73% to ₹163 crore, revenue rose about 17%, and assets under management crossed ₹1 lakh crore for the first time. The shares rose only 2.44%, because after a roughly 23% run over the past month, a strong quarter was largely expected.
- **Elecon Engineering (ELECON, ₹11,442 crore)** — the day's weak spot. First-quarter profit fell about 60% to ₹70 crore as operating margin was crushed from 26.6% to 21%, despite 6% revenue growth. The stock fell around 6% intraday, then recovered almost all of it to close down just 0.35% as the risk-on tape absorbed the miss.

*Omission notes:* **TCS (Tata Consultancy Services)** reported after Thursday's close and was covered in full in yesterday's brief; Friday was its share reaction (up 0.95%, and the read-through that led the sector). **L&T Finance (LTF)** filed its Q1 board outcome at 17:55, after the 3:30 PM close, so it is a Monday input, not a Friday driver.

## The Connections

*Behind the session — what triggered the moves, why, and which sectors to watch next.*

**One IT result set the mood for the whole market, not just its own sector.** TCS's numbers the night before were read as "demand is soft but not broken, deal wins are steady," and that was enough to flip IT from the previous day's worst sector to the day's leader. But notice where the money actually went: the big IT winners were mid-caps like Zensar (+13.6%) and Newgen (+9.3%), not the large-caps, because a low base plus short-covering amplified the bounce. The same "earnings season is starting fine" relief also gave cover to banks and property. The real test of the template comes with Infosys on 23 July.

**A collapsing fear gauge did as much work as the earnings did.** India VIX falling to 12.25, near a one-year low, means the market has priced out the event risk that hung over it a week ago, and when fear drains, money flows back into the most rate- and sentiment-sensitive corners first. That is why realty led. The counter-intuitive part: property rose 3.49% *even as* the US 10-year yield ticked up to 4.57%, which is normally a headwind for rate-sensitive real estate. The read is that a domestic-demand and earnings-anticipation story overwhelmed the global cost-of-capital signal; watch whether that holds if US yields keep climbing into Tuesday's inflation reading.

**This rally was domestic money, and that is an important correction to the week's story.** The narrative running all week has been "foreign investors are returning to India after a $60-billion exodus," and over several weeks that is genuinely true. But Friday itself does not support it: foreign investors were net sellers of ₹533 crore, and it was domestic institutions, buying ₹2,058 crore, that carried the market up. The multi-week return is real but not linear — do not read a domestic-led up day as proof foreign money drove it.

**Cheaper oil still isn't reaching refiners the way you'd expect — MRPL rose on margins, not crude.** Mangalore Refinery jumped 8.56%, and the intuitive explanation (oil fell, so refiners win) is wrong here: Brent was flat on the day. This was the market pricing better refining spreads — the gap between crude cost and fuel selling prices — for the refiner cohort, a margin story rather than a crude-price story. It is a useful reminder that "oil down" and "refiners up" are not the same trade.

## What to Watch Tomorrow

- **India June CPI (release window from Monday 13 July)** — the first inflation read after the oil scare; with the monsoon now forecast below-normal, the food-price component is the watch.
- **US June CPI, Tuesday 14 July (6:00 PM IST)** — the key global reading India trades on next week; it feeds Fed-rate-path expectations and the US 10-year yield (now 4.57%).
- **Bank Q1 season continues** — after Indian Bank and Bank of Maharashtra, more state-run and private lenders report through the coming weeks; watch whether the clean asset-quality signal holds across the cohort.
- **RBI Weekly Statistical Supplement, Friday 17 July** — this one captures the oil-shock-week reserves move, so it is the real intervention-proxy read that Friday's reading pre-dates.
- **Infosys Q1 results, 23 July** — the second big IT result, measured against the flat-constant-currency bar TCS just set.
- **US–India tariff cliff, 24 July** — the temporary 10% US tariff expires; the interim trade deal is the market's largest scheduled binary, still unresolved.
- **The monsoon gate (end-July)** — whether the IMD's below-normal July call actually verifies, with kharif sowing and food inflation the read-through.
- **NSE monthly F&O expiry (30 July) and the RBI rate decision (early August)** — the next mechanical and monetary markers.
