## What Happened

### The Hormuz reopening drained the oil premium — the real fuel under the day
The weekend's defining event resolved the thread the Friday brief flagged. The first round of high-level US-Iran negotiations concluded early Monday in Switzerland (mediated by Qatar and Pakistan) with a 60-day roadmap to a final deal. The concrete, *signed* outcomes: Iran committed to free and open transit of the Strait of Hormuz for the 60 days (jointly administered with Oman thereafter); Iran agreed to invite IAEA inspectors back; and the US Treasury issued a temporary 60-day general license authorising the sale and delivery of Iranian oil (running to ~21-Aug). The US naval blockade had already ended on 18-June. The market read it correctly: the structural war premium is "well and truly over." **Brent fell to ~$77.75/bbl, −11% on the week** (WTI −13%). For India — an ~85%-crude-importer — this is the cleanest positive transmission there is: a lower import bill, a better current account, softer imported inflation, and rupee support, all at once. This is exactly the "watch Brent, rhetoric-not-reality" call carried from Friday, paying off. **The honest caveat (kept from Friday):** this is a roadmap, not peace — Trump threatened fresh strikes mid-summit and Lebanon fighting persisted, so the lower-oil trade is constructive, not one-way.

### The tape reclaimed 24,100 — but the rebound was broad and shallow, and IT only partly recovered
Nifty added 89.80 points (+0.37%) to 24,102.90; Sensex +291 to 77,094; breadth was firmly positive (~2,500 advancers vs ~1,688 decliners). The leadership was defensive-plus-cyclical: Media (+1.42%), Pharma (+1.24%), Healthcare (+1.05%), Oil & Gas (+0.92%) led; only FMCG (−0.41%) and Consumer Durables (−0.55%) closed red. The thing to see clearly: **this was not a round-trip of Friday's loss.** The Nifty is up only ~1% on the week despite Friday's −0.64% and today's +0.37%, and **Nifty IT recovered only partially** — a valuation-driven dip-buy (Infosys +1.95% to ₹1,071.90, the cohort up ~1.5% intraday off Friday's multi-year low), not a fundamental all-clear. Accenture's discretionary-spend caution is the standing overhang into the Indian-IT July quarter; the bounce bought the dip, it did not retract the derating.

### The FII inflow was part-mechanical, not pure conviction
FII were net cash buyers of ₹4,859.10 cr and DII net sellers of ₹1,159.60 cr. Two disambiguations matter. First, the news desk resolved a dating error in circulation: this ₹4,859 cr is **Monday's** provisional print, which several aggregators had mis-dated onto Friday's close. Second, and more important for reading it: **the FTSE India semi-annual rebalance was effective today**, estimated at >$600m of *scheduled passive* inflow (Nuvama; additions include Lenskart, Tata Capital, a Bharti Airtel weight-up). So a real chunk of the day's foreign buying was mechanical index-tracking that was knowable in advance — the brief does not attribute the whole number to Iran/crude sentiment. The 2026 YTD foreign-equity outflow still stands at a record ~₹2.2 lakh crore (NSDL); one strong, partly-mechanical day does not turn that.
