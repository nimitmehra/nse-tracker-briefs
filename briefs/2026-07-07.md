## What Happened

### India decoupled from a brutal Asia chip rout
A sharp global AI-chip selloff gutted tech-heavy Asia today — KOSPI fell 7.9%, with SK Hynix down 14.6% and Samsung 9.1% — on fears that Big Tech's AI capex leaves memory and chip supply glutted. India shed only 0.13%. That gap is the day's real signal: a financials-heavy benchmark with no chip exposure, cushioned by domestic flows, sat out a regional rout rather than catching contagion, making India the relative haven within EM-Asia for the session. For the principal the read is reassuring on the tail risk — this is money leaving Asian chip names, not an India-specific outflow — but it is not free. The one place the selloff spills onto Indian ground is the IT-services cohort, where a global valuation reset can compress multiples on INFY, TCS and their peers regardless of their own fundamentals, right as the Q1 season opens. That is a read-through to watch into the prints, not a same-day loss.

### Money rotated out of consumption and into IT under a flat tape
Beneath the −0.13% index the dispersion was violent and sector-specific. Trent (a Tata retail bellwether) fell 12.44% after its Q1 business update showed revenue up about 19% against a Street looking for 22–23% — a deceleration into a stock that had rallied ~23% the prior month on hopes of a growth pickup — with revenue per square foot down 12.2% flagging real store-productivity pressure. Kalyan Jewellers fell 6.95% on the mirror-image setup: a genuinely strong print (revenue +38%, same-store sales ~28%) whose premium multiple still got cheaper as expectations reset. Money left that richly-priced consumption corner and moved into beaten-down IT (+2.43%) ahead of TCS on 9-July. The portfolio owns none of these names directly, but the Trent miss is a consumption-demand signal that reaches the whole richly-valued retail cohort — worth carrying as a demand read-through, not a one-stock event.

### The two-sided buying spine narrowed
Yesterday's lead was FIIs and DIIs both net buyers with FPIs turning positive; today that flip did not repeat cleanly. Foreign institutions bought a net ₹393 crore in cash but simultaneously sold about 2.4 lakh Nifty futures — a hedged, profit-taking posture rather than clean risk-on — while domestic institutions flipped to net sellers at −₹383 crore. The two nearly offset, which is consistent with a shallow give-back after four green sessions. The read for the principal is that the foreign-support spine flagged yesterday is intact but thinner than it looked twenty-four hours ago; the rupee firming against a flat dollar (a small India-specific bid) is the one corroborating tell that foreign cash stayed marginally constructive. It changes no single position, but it lowers the conviction behind treating the flow backdrop as a clear tailwind.
