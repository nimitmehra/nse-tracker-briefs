## What Happened

### Affle's 2023 promise quietly recast as M&A

Affle India (AFFLE), the Mumbai-listed mobile-advertising and performance-marketing platform, filed a Postal Ballot Notice on Monday at 3:55 PM IST. The resolution proposes redirecting ₹297 crore — sixty-seven percent of net 2023 preferential proceeds still undeployed at thirty months — from in-house technology development to inorganic growth. E-voting opened Tuesday at 9 AM IST and closes June 10; the result is declared on or before June 12. Stacked on the ₹1,100-crore promoter warrant issue announced on May 9, total promoter-related capital sought over thirty months reaches ₹1,849 crore. Shares jumped 8.71 percent on Monday on the clean Q4 print. The board's 2023 investor case named tech build as the moat; this resolution is the formal acknowledgement that the moat is being bought, not built. Institutional dissent above twenty-five percent on the special resolution would reset the thesis.

The procedural channel was already calibrated when the appeal was delivered. Sunday's rhetoric reads, in retrospect, as a public layer over active policy. The jewellery cluster's Day-2 cascade is correctly priced on rhetoric plus action: Kalyan Jewellers (KALYANKJIL) down a further 9.27 percent, P.N. Gadgil (PNGJL) down 9.01, Senco down 8.52, Titan down 6.73. The sixty-day watch flagged yesterday now shifts. The question is no longer whether rhetoric becomes action; it is what the next escalation looks like. Three candidates: a customs-duty notification from the Central Board of Indirect Taxes, a Liberalised Remittance Scheme curb on gold ETFs, or a recalibration of the Tax Collected at Source on foreign travel.

Brent crude rose a further 3.77 percent in Tuesday overnight marks to $105.11, extending Monday's +2.98 percent gain.
