## What Happened

### An Iran oil shock ended India's decoupling story
A single geopolitical statement flipped the day. In the last trading hour, US President Trump said the ceasefire with Iran is "over," after Iran was accused of attacking three ships in the Strait of Hormuz; Washington launched fresh strikes and revoked the waiver that let Iran sell crude. Brent ripped about 6% to ~$78.9, a two-day surge near 8%. India — which imports about 85% of its oil — fell hard, the Nifty down 2.12% and worse than the US S&P's −0.3%. This is the clean reversal of the story that ran every brief for a fortnight: 24 hours ago India was the haven and oil was soft. Today India was the vulnerable oil-importer and crude was the shock. The timing matters — the statement hit intraday, so it is a valid same-session cause, not an overnight input.

### The crash was sentiment and futures, not an exit — both FIIs and DIIs bought
The most important number today is the one that contradicts the fall. Despite a selloff the wires framed as FII-outflow fear, the actual cash print was the opposite: foreign institutions bought a net ₹1,962.8 crore and domestic institutions bought a net ₹790.2 crore — both net buyers on a −2% day. The selling was concentrated in the last hour and looks sentiment-and-futures-driven, not a distribution of Indian cash equities. Two corroborating signals sit underneath: oil-upstream names rose against the session (ONGC +1.15%, the top Nifty gainer), confirming an oil-shock mechanism rather than a broad India outflow, and VIX's 26% jump to a still-moderate 14.68 reads as a sharp-but-orderly repricing, not a systemic cascade. The read for the principal is to treat the fall as a headline shock, not a change in the flow backdrop.

### Banks and the oil channel led down; IT and pharma cushioned it
Beneath the index the damage was cleanly oil-sensitive. The Bank Nifty led the fall (−2.51%) as high-beta financials sold off — Shriram Finance −4.91%, Bajaj Finance −3.08%, Kotak −3.04%, and the whole ICICI/Axis/HDFC/SBI complex down 2–3%. The oil channel did the rest: IndiGo −5.02% on jet-fuel fear (the worst Nifty-50 name), Maruti −4.04% on input-cost worry, and consumption staples like HUL −3.30% lower. The two defensives that returned a live number both outperformed — Nifty IT −1.37% and Nifty Pharma −0.97% — because dollar-earners with no oil sensitivity get a small tailwind from a weaker rupee. The book's lone bright dot was POCL (Pondy Oxides), the only T1 name up (+2.29%), decoupling on its own 21-July stock-split record date rather than anything macro.

## Cascade Watch

**The crude-import channel edge fired hard and set the whole day.** The cause was the Iran/Hormuz re-escalation lifting Brent about 6% to ~$78.9. The mechanism is the oil-import cost edge — India buys ~85% of its crude abroad, so a crude spike hits refiner and airline margins, the rupee and the current account together, which is why India's −2.12% was worse than the US −0.3%. Tomorrow's read: this is a spike, not yet a level; if Iran de-escalates the oil premium unwinds fast, but if crude holds near $79 the margin, rupee and inflation pressure sustains.

**The CARRARO Brent-under-$85 add clause moved further from firing — and that is not a bearish flip.** The cause is the same oil spike. The mechanism is the add-on-normalization edge on CARRARO (Carraro India): its bullish add trigger needs Hormuz to *resolve* and Brent to sit *below $85 for four sustained weeks*, and today's escalation breaks both legs at once — the Hormuz-resolution condition is now false and the sub-$85 window resets. No bearish trigger fires either; the bear kill-case needs crude above $100, and $79 is nowhere near. Tomorrow's read: the clause holds "watching," a conservative hold, and only a genuine Iran de-escalation plus sustained soft oil re-opens the add case.

**The high-beta financial edge amplified the fear, but the flow print undercut it.** The cause was the risk-off spike hitting rate-sensitive names hardest — the Bank Nifty led down 2.51%, with JIOFIN −5.38% as the high-beta amplifier and no own-name catalyst. The mechanism is the beta-amplification edge on fear spikes. But the counter-signal is the flow: FIIs and DIIs both bought the cash dip, so the fear was FII exit and the cash print was the opposite. Tomorrow's read: watch whether tomorrow's flow print confirms the dip-buying held, which would mark today as a sentiment air-pocket rather than the start of distribution.

**The dollar-earner defensive edge cushioned IT and pharma.** The cause was the same risk-off, from which IT (−1.37%) and pharma (−0.97%) both outperformed. The mechanism is the export-earner edge — no oil sensitivity, plus a small tailwind from a weaker rupee. Tomorrow's read: TCS reports tomorrow (9-July, post-market) and sets the IT cohort tone into the season; that is a next-session input, and INFY's own 23-July result remains the book's arbiter.
