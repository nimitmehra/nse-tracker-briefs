## What Happened

### The IT crash was the whole index — and it was a rotation, not a break

Nifty IT fell 5.57% to 29,384.45, and that single sector was the entire reason the Nifty 50 closed down 0.33%. Tata Consultancy fell 8.39% (the worst Nifty 50 stock), Tech Mahindra 6.31%, LTIMindtree 6.66%, Persistent 6.82%, HCL 5.19% and Infosys 3.79%. The cause is profit-booking after a roughly 7-8% three-session run, with rising US bond yields and firmer crude reviving Fed-higher-for-longer worries that delay US client deals. The "Jefferies says AI hits a quarter of IT revenue" line several outlets attached is old February-April analysis re-amplified to fit the price move, not a fresh note — treat it as an echo, not a catalyst. The proof it was a rotation: financials rallied the same day (Bank Nifty +0.88%, PSU Bank +1.70%), so money left IT and went into banks ahead of a predictable Friday rate hold.

### Enforcement Directorate searched Hindustan Zinc — group-wide across Vedanta

Hindustan Zinc (HINDZINC), a tier-two name, told the exchange the Enforcement Directorate ran a search at its premises from 1 to 3 June under the foreign-exchange law. Web checks confirm this was a Vedanta-group-wide action — the agency searched both Vedanta and Hindustan Zinc in the same probe. The company's filing says there is "no impact on financials or operations," but that is the defensible posture a company writes; the search itself is the fact that matters. There is no tracked Vedanta-group name beyond Hindustan Zinc in the book, so the direct read-through is contained, but a forex probe touching a group with a documented history of moving Hindustan Zinc cash upward via special dividends is a governance overhang worth watching. This is the day's top regulatory event on a tracked name.

### Dee Development's ₹300-crore raise came back clean

Dee Development Engineers (DEEDEV), now a tier-two name after the 2 June demotion, disclosed Wednesday's board outcome: a ₹300-crore preferential share issue at ₹502 a share. The promoter took ₹20 crore (his stake easing from 50.82% to 47.31%) and ₹280 crore went to 23 named domestic institutions — Kotak, WhiteOak, Ashoka, ValueQuest and 360 ONE among them. A registered valuer is named and the shareholder vote is set for 27 June. The worry going in was an opaquely priced issue stuffed with unrecognised offshore funds; none of that materialised — the round-tripping check passes and the capital-action arc funding the recent ₹593-crore order wins reads as transparent. The stock barely moved (+0.54%), having waited for the terms. The next checkpoint is the 27 June meeting, where the allottee list and price must hold.

### Friday's twin macro morning is the week's decision

The Reserve Bank's rate decision lands at 10 AM Friday alongside the fourth-quarter GDP release, and the bond market did its positioning on Wednesday. Economists are firmly expecting a hold at 5.25% for a third straight meeting with a defensive tone, and the one-year yield jumped 8.4 basis points to 6.17% — a sharp front-end repricing of that defensive read, even as the ten-year barely moved. GDP forecasts cluster at 7.0-7.3% with services seen up 9.1%. The duration signal sits at the short end and lands on the rate-sensitive book — Jio Financial lagged the bank rally on Wednesday (−1.10%) while India Shelter caught it (+1.04%), the usual non-bank-versus-bank split into a rate meeting.

### A fresh US tariff proposal landed mid-negotiation

The US Trade Representative proposed an extra 12.5% tariff on India (and 53 other countries) over an alleged failure to legally bar forced-labour-linked imports, and it landed as the US trade delegation was in New Delhi for day three of four. India categorically denied the allegation and will fold it into the broader trade talks; hearings are set for 7 July and a decision for late June or July. This is a negotiating-leverage move, not an enacted duty — read it as reported, not done. It adds a sentiment overhang for the export-leaning names in the book (Acutaas, Garware films, TD Power, Welspun, SJS), though none is specifically named in the forced-labour claim.
