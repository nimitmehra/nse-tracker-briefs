## What Happened

### Modi's Hyderabad appeal craters the jewellery cohort

Prime Minister Narendra Modi addressed an event in Hyderabad on Sunday with a seven-point public appeal anchored by a request that Indians refrain from buying gold for a year. The speech named India's record $72 billion gold-import bill of FY26 as a strain on reserves under the West Asia oil crisis. Monday's tape read the rhetoric as a probability-shift to an import-duty hike. Titan (TITAN), the Tata-group jeweller, fell 6.73 percent; Kalyan Jewellers (KALYANKJIL) lost 9.27, Senco 8.52, Sky Gold 6.66, P.N. Gadgil (PNGJL) 9.01. No Cabinet note has been circulated and no Finance Ministry draft has surfaced — the price action is on the rhetoric alone. The duty calibration that historically follows such appeals — the 2018 oil-price cycle is the precedent — is the sixty-day deployment-calculus watch.

### Hormuz reversal puts Brent back above $104, rupee at fresh record low

Brent crude rose 2.98 percent on Monday to $104.31 after Washington's weekend rejection of an Iranian peace proposal collapsed last week's framework-MoU narrative. The Strait of Hormuz has remained de-facto blocked since 28 February, a state Wikipedia and Al Jazeera continued to flag through the optimistic May cascade. The Reuters and Bloomberg India desks' deal-near coverage carried the 2024-26 framing caveat applied on review. Monday's reversal exposes that cascade as having over-weighted the optimistic side. The Indian rupee fell 1.13 percent to ₹95.32 — at the 52-week low, a clean break against a dollar index up only 0.30 percent. India VIX surged 10.17 percent to 18.55; the Sensex shed 1,295 points. The June Monetary Policy Committee now carries Brent-vigilance optionality the April -125 basis point easing cycle had foreclosed.

### Blue Star's Friday call validated within four calendar days

Blue Star (BLUESTARCO), the air-conditioning and engineering-services holding, posted a slowing Q4 print on Friday — consolidated revenue up 1.3 percent to ₹4,072 crore, FY26 profit-before-tax down 4.2 percent. The post-print management call on 7 May named Middle East crisis and currency volatility as headwinds and warned the 8-to-8.5 percent operating-margin band was unlikely to be sustained through FY27 and beyond. Monday's Hormuz reversal and rupee record low validate that call within four calendar days; shares slipped a further 1.87 percent to ₹1,661, taking the one-week move to -7.83 percent. The +35 percent commercial-and-data-centre booking surge from the Q4 release remains the structural offset, but the FY27 margin trajectory now reprices as the market hears the management call against the macro tape.
