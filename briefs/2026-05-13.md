## What Happened

### Brent, rupee and the FII tape break together

Brent jumped 2.74 percent overnight to $107.07. The rupee closed at a fresh fifty-two-week low of ₹95.62. Foreign portfolio investors sold ₹8,437 crore on Monday — the largest single-day outflow in eighteen days. Domestic institutions bought ₹5,940 crore; the absorption ratio fell to roughly seventy percent against the ninety-percent pattern of recent weeks. The Nifty 50 closed minus 1.83 percent at 23,379.55 — a fourth consecutive losing session, with roughly ₹16 lakh crore of market capitalisation eroded over four days. Coromandel International (COROMANDEL), the Hyderabad-based fertiliser maker, was the only Tier-1 portfolio holding to close green at plus 0.35 percent. The twelve-percent Kharif subsidy backstop is now absorbing the input-cost pressure on operational tape. The H2-reversal thesis the bulls built in April is now contradicted by Monday's flow data.

### Indian IT meets a new structural argument

The Nifty IT index fell 3.73 percent — the largest single-day sector cut since the cohort's bleed began. The trigger was OpenAI's launch of a Deployment Company, a $4 billion vehicle backed by Goldman Sachs and SoftBank. It proposes to take the enterprise-integration work that has been Indian IT services' core revenue base for two decades. Capgemini took an investment stake on the same day. Infosys (INFY), the Bengaluru services major, closed at a fresh fifty-two-week low of ₹1,140.30 — its lowest since December 2020. TCS and HCL Tech printed fifty-two-week lows alongside. Business Standard cast the move as the most serious structural threat the industry has faced since offshore outsourcing rose in the late 1990s. Infosys retains explicit partnerships with both OpenAI and Anthropic; the May 11–20 roadshow is the window for that lane to carry the revenue the disruption is taking.

### Jefferies sharpens the gold-pause clock; three Tier-1 filings land

Jefferies India framed Prime Minister Modi's Sunday gold-pause appeal as echoing the 2013 current-account-deficit crisis. That is the second institutional escalation in forty-eight hours, after Nomura's "tipping point" note. Jefferies mapped four policy paths: a customs duty notification, an 80:20-style import quota, a Liberalised Remittance Scheme curb on gold ETFs, and a Tax Collected at Source recalibration on luxury imports. The jewellery cohort fell for a third session — Kalyan Jewellers (KALYANKJIL) down 6.07 percent, D.P. Abhushan down 8.04 percent. Three primary-source filings landed on Tier-1 names. ICRA externally certified Affle's ₹499 crore of undeployed 2023 proceeds, corroborating the May 11 postal-ballot baseline. Intellect Design Arena (INTELLECT) won a thirty-seven-institution Canadian banking deal covering 262,000 members and $11.7 billion CAD. KEI Industries (KEI) re-submitted its May 7 Income Tax search disclosure after the exchange asked for verification.

### A tier-three audit cluster sits beneath the cascade

Vikram Solar (VIKRAMSOLR) broadcast its May 7 board outcome on Tuesday evening — five days late, and routed through the residual "Updates" category. The substance is heavier than the routing suggests. Kolkata-based GARV and Associates carried two material Emphasis of Matter paragraphs on ₹201 crore of disputed receivables. A new chief executive was appointed on the same day as the board meeting. A ₹3,726 crore Gangaikondan capex commitment landed alongside — nearly half the company's market capitalisation. Dixon Technologies (DIXON), at ₹49,586 crore of consolidated revenue, continues with S. N. Dhawan and Company — a mid-tier firm scaled below what that revenue would typically warrant. Inox India (INOXINDIA) by contrast filed clean Q4 results under SRBC and Company, an EY India member. Tier-three auditors clustering on the more aggressive capex announcements is the watchpoint for the next cycle.
