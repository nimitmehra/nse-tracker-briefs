## What Happened

### Oil and Iran finally put a bid under fear
This was the third down day in a row, but the first where the market paid for protection. India VIX rose 5.5% to 13.29 — on Monday and Tuesday it *fell* on down days, so there had been no defensive bid; today there was. The trigger is real and external: overnight brought the 11th straight night of US strikes on Iran, US Secretary of State Marco Rubio called Tehran "not serious" about talks, and Brent spiked about 4% toward $92–95 a barrel, a multi-week high. For a country that imports roughly 85% of its crude, a rising oil price is a live cost line — for tyres, auto-components, paints, logistics and airlines — and a current-account and rupee negative, with the rupee already sitting a whisker from its weakest-ever mark. Fourteen of sixteen sectors closed red; only FMCG and auto held green. This is not a "decoupling" or a flows-exodus — it is a straightforward cost-and-risk shock, and it is verified.

### Two June-quarter NBFC results told exactly opposite stories
The loudest lesson of the day came from two lenders that reported and moved in opposite directions. Bandhan Bank fell nearly 17% even though its June-quarter profit rose 35% to ₹502 crore and bad loans improved. The reason: management *cut* its forward return-on-assets guidance to 1.2–1.4% from 1.6–1.8% and effectively abandoned its 6.5% margin goal, citing deposit-cost pressure. A clean headline beat was destroyed by a forward guidance cut. Mahindra & Mahindra Financial did the mirror image: profit up 75% to ₹926 crore, asset quality at an eight-year low, and the stock rose 8%. The read-through for the book is direct — Jio Financial (a holding) drifted 2.2% lower with the whole financials complex, and India Shelter Finance, which we demoted out of Tier 1 today, fell 5.5% on the same cohort risk-off. Guidance, not the headline number, is setting NBFC prices right now.

### The 24-July tariff cliff is now two days out and unsigned
Underneath the oil noise, a scheduled binary armed further. The temporary US tariff arrangement on Indian goods lapses 24-July, now T-2, and the first-phase India–US trade pact is still at the "last 1%" of legal text with no signed document. One sourced report has interim-deal prospects actually *dimming*, and India's official line is "terms, not calendar." This bears directly on the book's US-exporting names: Garware Hi-Tech Films most of all, which fell 3% today into the anxiety, then Welspun Corp's India-facility export-pipe leg, and the IT cohort. It is a genuine two-day binary, and there is a real chance of no deal.

**The IT cohort is de-risking into Infosys' print.** The IT-services edge pulled Infosys down 2% ahead of tomorrow's 23-July result, even with the rupee at record-weak levels that normally help exporters — risk-off and a soft global-tech read overrode the currency tailwind. Infosys' guidance tomorrow is the arbiter for the large-cap IT read: a hold-or-raise resets the cohort, a cut deepens the drawdown.

**The monsoon edge stays half-open on Dhanuka.** Cumulative rainfall 23% below normal and kharif sown area down 6% year-on-year fired the monsoon edge onto Dhanuka — but sowing is *improving* on a mid-July revival, so it neither closed nor broke.
