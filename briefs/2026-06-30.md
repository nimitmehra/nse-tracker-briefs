## What Happened

### A flat index hid an IT-specific selloff, not a risk-off
The number to ignore today is the headline. Nifty closed −0.34% at 23,865.75, but that drop was almost entirely one sector: Nifty IT fell 2.73% to a multi-year low, with Infosys (INFY) down 3.50% to a fresh 52-week low and TCS off about 3.2%, both at the bottom of their ranges. The driver was Accenture's weak guidance read as a leading signal for the same global clients Indian IT serves, layered on AI-disruption fear and US-rates-stay-higher-for-longer ahead of US data later this week. Underneath that heavyweight drag the session was green: breadth ran positive at roughly 2,250 advances against 1,805 declines, realty and consumer durables led up, and midcaps and smallcaps rose. So this was a repricing of the export-tech growth premium, not a broad market exit — the benchmark just happens to carry enough IT weight to look red.

### Quarter-end showed in the flows, not in the index
This was a monthly futures expiry stacked on June quarter-end, and the mechanical fingerprint was in the money flow rather than the index. Foreign investors sold ₹2,557 crore, the biggest single-day sell of the recent run, and domestic institutions absorbed all of it and more with ₹6,842 crore of buying — classic quarter-end book-squaring and window-dressing, with domestic firepower cushioning the foreign exit. The proof it was mechanical and not fear-driven is the volatility gauge: India VIX sat flat at 13.6 through the whole expiry session, carrying no fear premium at all. Crude relief deepened in the background — Brent near $73, with the June quarter closing down roughly 30%, its largest quarterly fall since 2020 — but the Hormuz thread was explicitly not today's driver. The importer tailwind keeps building; the session was IT plus expiry mechanics.

### The Delhi EV policy that lifted Ather yesterday hit Royal Enfield today
The Delhi Cabinet approved EV Policy 2.0 today, a ₹15,000 crore programme that proposes to stop new petrol and CNG two-wheeler registrations in Delhi from April 2028. Yesterday this same policy lifted pure-EV names; today it cut the other way, with Eicher Motors (EICHERMOT, Royal Enfield's parent) down 4.75% on the demand-overhang concern for its limited electric-two-wheeler presence. On the other side, Maruti Suzuki rose 5.24% to lead the Nifty, but that was a separate story — a Jefferies upgrade to Buy with a target of ₹16,500 on better demand and lower input costs. For the book, the read-through to the T1 auto-ancillaries (SANDHAR, SJS, CARRARO) is second-order and ambiguous: a Delhi-only, 2028-dated registration ban is a narrow, long-dated signal, worth carrying as cohort sentiment, not a thesis-mover.
