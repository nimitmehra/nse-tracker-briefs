## What Happened

### Transrail's record year hides an income-tax search

The full-year headline buried the item that actually changes the thesis. Transrail Lighting (TRANSRAILL), the transmission-tower and power-distribution engineering contractor and a tier-one holding, posted a strong FY26 — revenue ₹6,880 crore (+30%), operating profit ₹421 crore (+28%), a record ₹16,361 crore order book. But the fourth quarter was weak, with revenue −4% and profit −24% to ₹97 crore on margin compression. The market read the quarter, not the year, and sold the stock 5.3%. The decision-relevant fact sits in the audit, not the tape: the statutory auditor attached an emphasis-of-matter note flagging an Income Tax Department search-and-seizure operation (24-28 March) that reached the offices, plants and the homes of certain directors and senior staff. No assets were attached and no amount surrendered, and the auditor's opinion is clean — but the company's "highest-ever" press release makes no mention of it. The operating numbers are fine; the overhang is governance and regulatory, and it is now the load-bearing watch.

### Crude collapses six percent — net positive for India

The day's dominant macro move was Brent crude falling 6.16% to $93.45, down 16% on the week, on reported hopes of a US-Iran framework to extend the ceasefire and reopen the Strait of Hormuz. India imports about 85% of its crude, so this is net positive for the import bill, the rupee over time, and downstream refining margins, and negative for upstream producers — Oil and Natural Gas Corporation fell 4.68% on a production miss compounded by the crude drop. The read is two-way and event-driven, not a durable disinflation impulse: the bond market did not move, with the 10-year yield flat at 6.996%, and crude has already whipsawed on fresh-strike headlines. Notably the rupee weakened 0.45% despite cheaper oil, which points to foreign-investor outflows rather than a trade-balance story. Treat the framework as reported rhetoric, not a signed deal.

### Strong Q4 prints, sold anyway — the priced-in tape

Three tier-one names printed strong-to-record quarters and were sold, which is the small-and-mid-cap tape telling you earnings are already in the price. Carraro India (CARRARO), the tractor-driveline maker, beat on every line — fourth-quarter profit +76%, full-year +48%, a ₹6.75 dividend — and fell 8.78%. Pondy Oxides and Chemicals (POCL), India's largest secondary-lead recycler, posted record results (quarterly profit +111%) plus a retail-friendly five-for-two share split and fell 6.37%. Tier-two shipper Great Eastern Shipping (GESHIP) reported a record quarter and fell 8.44% as falling crude pressures forward tanker rates. None of these is an operating disappointment, and the de-ratings began before the prints — but the contrast with Transrail, whose 5.3% fall sat on a genuine quarterly profit decline, is the teaching point: separate sell-the-news from a real miss.
