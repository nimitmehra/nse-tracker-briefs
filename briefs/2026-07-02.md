## What Happened

### IT reversed as hard as it fell — but it's a bounce, not a turn
Nifty IT jumped 4.64%, its best session in about fourteen months, doing nearly all of the index's lifting while Nifty Bank sat flat and breadth stayed mixed underneath. Infosys (INFY), the book's single IT holding, rose 5.64% to ₹1,040.90, back above ₹1,000 off yesterday's fresh 52-week low, with the whole large-cap complex moving together — Tech Mahindra +7.17%, TCS +4.31%, HCL Technologies +4.24%. The driver was oversold value-buying after four losing sessions, helped by a strong AI-spend read from Snowflake's results and rising US rate-cut hopes. But one green session does not reverse a sector still down about 30% for the year and 13% on the month; both the news and market desks read this as a technical rebound with no company catalyst behind it, and a bounce with no earnings under it can give itself back. The arbiters are the first Q1FY27 results — TCS on 9-July, then INFY on 23-July.

### WAAREERTL's big order is real — but it books at the parent, not the company we own
The two 30-June items on Waaree Renewable (WAAREERTL) — a 55% acquisition-close of Associated Power Structures and a ₹1,044.69 crore solar-EPC order — are real, and the news desk now reads them as confirmed on the company's own press release and trade press. The catch the filings desk caught: the order books at the parent, Waaree Energies, which filed it with NSE on 30-June, while WAAREERTL itself filed only a routine housekeeping certificate and no material-event disclosure of its own today. So the arbitration holds it at REPORTED for the listed entity we actually own — the strategic story is genuine, but it does not yet land in WAAREERTL's order book absent its own filing. None of this touches the separate regulatory watch, which stays elevated: the US customs ruling on parent Waaree is dated to about 8-July, and the EXIT-half plan stays live on an adverse outcome.

### SRM won the cleanest positive on the book — a rating upgrade with more headroom
SRM Contractors (SRM), the J&K and Himalayan-infrastructure EPC name held as a conviction position, got a one-notch credit-rating upgrade from CareEdge — long-term to CARE A from A-, short-term to A1 from A2 — filed with NSE at 12:46 IST. More useful than the notch was the money behind it: rated facilities were enhanced about 83%, to ₹409.5 crore from ₹223.5 crore. That is both a vote on improving credit quality and, more directly, more sanctioned capacity to carry an order book, consistent with SRM's recent run of wins. It reinforces the balance-sheet-and-execution thesis with no offsetting flag. The stock closed quietly, up 1.09% to ₹518.15, near its high.
