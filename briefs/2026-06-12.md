## What Happened

### A financials-led relief rally on a reported truce — but the deal is unsigned

Friday flipped Thursday's gloom into a broad risk-on session: Nifty 50 rose 1.99% to 23,622.90, the Sensex 2.30%, and the move was overwhelmingly broad — 18 of 20 T1 names green, the midcaps up 2.4% and smallcaps 2.8%. The trigger was a single overnight catalyst: the US and Iran reportedly reached a draft 14-point peace memorandum — a 60-day ceasefire, the Strait of Hormuz to reopen within 30 days, an oil-sanctions waiver — that could be signed this weekend in Switzerland ahead of the G7. Crude fell below $90, the rupee firmed against its recent stress, and India simply rode a worldwide relief trade (the Nasdaq had jumped 2.5% overnight on the same news). For the principal the read is a textbook fear-unwind, not an India-specific re-rating — but the whole rally rests on an unsigned document, so if the weekend signing slips, Monday gaps the war-premium back in.

### Crude below $90 is the mechanism — and the cleanest portfolio read-through

The lever under the rally was oil. Brent fell to about $89 (it touched a two-month low below $90 intraday) because the reported deal would reopen Hormuz shipping, and for an economy that imports roughly 85% of its oil, cheaper crude eases the import bill, the rupee, the subsidy math and input costs all at once. That is why financials, realty, autos and aviation led while the oil producers fell — ONGC dropped 2.53%, the one cohort that did not join the party, which is the tell that crude was the lever. For the book, the direct beneficiary is Varun Beverages, where crude and the PET it drives are the load-bearing input; the auto-ancillary names (Sandhar, SJS, Carraro) get the same input-cost tailwind. But the same trade caveats apply — traders bid cautiously because mine-clearance and idled-field restarts stand between a signed deal and actual supply normalising.

### The flow question stays open — and the rupee was the odd one out

One thing the rally did not settle is whether foreign money has truly turned. Friday's foreign and domestic flow split was unpublished at our write-time — the last hard reading is 8 June, when foreigners were still net sellers — so the relief rally is best read as sentiment and domestic-led, with a foreign-flow turn a hypothesis, not a fact. The clue against an easy flow story is the rupee: it weakened 0.57% even though the dollar was flat, an India-specific soft spot on an otherwise strong day, with foreign-flow or oil-payment timing the likely candidate. Financials led precisely because they are the most-sold cohort of the year-long foreign selling, so a relief bounce lifts them hardest mechanically — but whether that is one-day short-covering or genuine re-engagement needs the flow print before anyone calls it a turn.

The earnings story is a yes; the price is a no. T2, do-not-buy here.

## Cascade Watch

**The crude-input edge flipped from conflict-premium to relief tailwind.** The cause is the reported US-Iran draft truce that would reopen the Strait of Hormuz, which unwound the war-premium that had built Thursday. The mechanism is the crude-to-consumer edge: Brent falling below $90 eases the input-cost line for the packaging name (Varun Beverages) and the auto-ancillary names (Sandhar, SJS, Carraro), and the graph fired the crude-to-Varun-Beverages link plus the auto-ancillary cohort links on that signal. Tomorrow's read: whether the weekend signing lands — a slip re-spikes crude and reverses the whole tailwind in a single Monday session.

**The financials-cohort edge was the rally's lead engine.** The cause is the same relief turn, but the transmission ran through India's most-sold cohort. The mechanism is the financial-services cohort edge: a relief rally mechanically lifts the names foreigners have been dumping all year hardest, so Nifty Bank surged ~3% and the edge fired into Jio Financial and India Shelter Finance, the two financials read-throughs in the book. Tomorrow's read: whether this is genuine foreign re-engagement or one-day short-covering — the flow print, still unpublished, is the arbiter, and the rupee not rallying is a quiet argument for caution.

**The IT-services edge inverted — from drag to no-show.** The cause is the same global risk-on that lifted everything else; overnight US tech rebounded hard (the Nasdaq up 2.5%, semis leading). The mechanism is that the relief trade moved money out of defensives into domestic cyclicals over export IT, so Nifty IT was the lone red sector (−0.09%) and Infosys sat the rally out flat. Tomorrow's read: IT is now decoupling from the broad market on the rupee — a weaker rupee usually helps IT exporters, but the move out of defensives overrode that this session; watch whether the cohort finds its own bid or keeps lagging.

**The auto-ancillary input edge gave the cohort a quiet bid.** The cause is the crude relief reaching the input-cost line of the ancillary complex. The mechanism is the auto-ancillary cohort edge, which the graph activated on the crude-down signal — but the transmission was muted by an offsetting move: Sandhar fell 3.08%, the lone real T1 laggard, on profit-taking after a +40% one-month run, a technical unwind rather than a thesis crack. Tomorrow's read: the input-cost tailwind is real and one-directional, but it is a slow-burn margin help, not a same-session catalyst.
