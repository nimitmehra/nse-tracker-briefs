## What Happened

### Q1FY27 earnings are sorting the winners from the losers
The Indian market closed up 1.09% to 24,334.30 with the Sensex adding 965 points, and the driver was not a macro tailwind but the Q1FY27 results season starting to separate quality from the rest. On the winning side, Federal Bank posted a record June quarter — net profit up 37% to ₹1,177 crore, bad loans at a decadal low — and ran 6.9% to a 52-week high; Jio Financial's own result carried a 156% profit jump; and eClerx, the day's biggest single mover, beat and rose 12.5%. On the losing side, CEAT's profit collapsed 96%. Private banks and IT led, and with volatility still near its lows the read is clean: earnings-appetite buying, and the season now moves individual names, not the index level.

### CEAT's 96% profit crash is a live warning to two tracked names
The sharpest signal of the day was not a winner but a loser: CEAT (CEAT, tyres — a tracked T2 name) reported a June-quarter profit down 96% to ₹4 crore even as revenue rose 22%, and the stock fell 7.3%. The cause was not weak demand but cost — natural rubber and crude-derivative inputs inflated by the West Asia conflict, plus a foreign-exchange hit on its overseas arm. That is the important read-through: the geopolitical premium the market keeps treating as a Brent headline is now landing as a margin line in auto-component profit statements. Two tracked T1 names — SJS Enterprises (SJS) and Carraro India (Carraro), both auto-ancillary, both reporting Q1FY27 imminently — sit in the same input-cost channel. Their margins are the thing to watch when they print.

### The tariff clock is now one week out, and still unsigned
Underneath the earnings noise, the single largest scheduled event drew closer without resolving. The temporary 10% US tariff arrangement on Indian goods expires 24-July, now one week away, and at tonight's close the first-phase India-US trade pact is still parked at the "last 1%" of legal text — no signed document, with a US trade-office deadline on related investigations landing 20-July. It is the biggest scheduled binary into late-July, and it bears directly on the book's US-exporting exposure: Garware Hi-Tech Films (GRWRHITECH, polyester film) is the most tariff-levered T1 name, with Welspun Corp (WELCORP) and the IT cohort also exposed. A market this calm into an unsigned deadline is either pricing a resolution or under-pricing the tail.

## Cascade Watch

**The auto-input-cost channel got its first hard read this quarter, and it said "margin squeeze."** The cause was CEAT's June-quarter profit crashing 96% on rubber and crude-derivative inflation driven by the West Asia conflict. The mechanism is the auto-ancillary input-cost edge: the same natural-rubber and crude-linked cost pressure that gutted CEAT runs through the tracked tractor-driveline and decorative-parts names. The effect is a live read-across to SJS (T1) and Carraro (T1) — both report imminently, and their June-quarter margins are the thing to watch. Note that this edge is not yet modelled in the graph; it is a narrative causal chain today, flagged for the next research scope.

**The private-financials earnings edge carried the index.** The cause was two strong bank prints landing together — Federal Bank's record 37% profit jump to a 52-week high, alongside JIOFIN's own 156% profit surge. The mechanism is the private-financials cohort-earnings edge, where a season of beats lifts the whole complex and the sector-beta names with it. The effect showed today: Nifty Bank up 1.63%, JIOFIN up 3.11%, private banks up around 2.1% on web-sourced marks — the financials cohort is delivering into the season and doing much of the index's work.

**The tariff-cliff edge is arming toward a 24-July binary.** The cause is the unsigned first-phase US trade pact one week from the tariff deadline. The mechanism is the US-export-exposure edge: an adverse or missed outcome reverts most Indian US-bound goods to higher tariff rates. The effect is a two-sided setup on the most-levered T1 names — Garware Hi-Tech Films (polyester film) directly, Welspun Corp's Americas pipe book and the IT cohort by extension. Tomorrow's read: 20-July's trade-office deadline is the first tell; 24-July is the binary itself.

**The monsoon–agri-input edge loosened for the first time in weeks.** The cause was July rainfall reviving and narrowing the cumulative deficit even as the headline stays 20% below normal. The mechanism is the monsoon agri-input cohort edge on DHANUKA (BUY pipeline): a recovering monsoon eases the rural-demand and agrochemical-volume worry the entry case leans on. The effect is that the DHANUKA gate stays adverse but less one-sided than a week ago — the deployment case neither advances nor breaks, and the end-July cumulative reading remains the arbiter.
