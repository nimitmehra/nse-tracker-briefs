## What Happened

### Eicher delivers a record print and books a new plant

**Eicher Motors prints a record FY26 and announces a ₹2,500 crore Andhra greenfield.** Eicher Motors (EICHERMOT), the Royal Enfield maker and 54.4% owner of VE Commercial Vehicles, closed Friday at ₹7,359.00, up 5.41% on the day and the strongest single-session gain in Nifty Auto. The Friday board meeting cleared Q4 revenue of ₹6,080 crore (+16% YoY) and FY26 revenue of ₹23,408 crore (+24%); FY26 profit reached ₹5,515 crore (+17%). Royal Enfield crossed 1.23 million annual motorcycles for the second year running. The board recommended a final dividend of ₹82 a share, up from ₹70 last year, against an unmodified opinion from S.R. Batliboi. The same evening's press release confirmed the ₹2,500 crore Andhra Pradesh greenfield announced on May 18, supplementing the Cheyyar capex toward a 20 lakh-unit target from today's 14.6 lakh. The Volvo Financial Services joint venture signed last week is the financing layer; Friday's print supplies the operating cash to support it. The cleanest structural Tier-1 catalyst in the window.

### Varun Beverages secures bottling rights through 2049

**Varun Beverages extends its PepsiCo bottling licence by ten years.** Varun Beverages (VBL), the Mumbai-based PepsiCo bottler now spanning India, sub-Saharan Africa and Sri Lanka, hit a fresh 52-week intraday high of ₹541.20 on Friday before settling at ₹536.55. The structural news is the renewed exclusive bottling and trademark licence with PepsiCo, now extended to April 2049 from the previous 2039 horizon. The ten-year extension adds two more PepsiCo strategic-planning cycles to VBL's revenue-visibility floor. Q1 CY26 revenue printed ₹6,574 crore (+18%) with profit of ₹879 crore (+20%); the Twizza acquisition (South African soft-drinks bottler, closed earlier this year) is complete, and the pending close of Crickley Dairy (South African dairy-beverages business) is the next platform plank. The Africa platform plus the bottling extension together rebuild the long-duration cash-flow narrative the March selloff had cracked. The pullback from the intraday high is healthy consolidation, not direction change.

### Belrise calls a Sunday board to clear a ₹2,000 crore share sale

**Belrise Industries clears a ₹2,000 crore institutional share sale before Monday's open.** Belrise Industries (BELRISE), the Pune-based auto-component supplier that listed in May 2025 via promoter sale, held a rare Sunday board meeting to clear an institutional share sale of up to ₹2,000 crore alongside its first audited FY26 print. FY26 revenue closed at ₹9,509 crore (+15%) with adjusted profit of ₹502 crore (+41%); the Q4 EBITDA margin compressed seventy basis points year on year to 11.4%. The board also cleared a corporate guarantee for a EUR 3.3 million external commercial borrowing for a newly-named defence-and-aerospace subsidiary the IPO prospectus had not led with. The ₹0.55 dividend is token; the institutional share sale (a qualified institutions placement, the standard route for raising fresh equity from mutual funds and FIIs without a public issue) is the substance. This is the first dilutive issue post-listing, and the postal ballot dispatched the same evening puts the timeline on the principal's desk before any analyst note lands.

### Engineers India bear case shrinks

**Engineers India's audit flag turns out to be ten-year-old Angola housekeeping.** Engineers India (ENGINERSIN), the public-sector engineering consultancy, recovered 3.56% on Friday to ₹223.70 as the substance of Thursday's auditor note flagging a specific risk became readable. The doubtful receivables flag that took the stock down 7.78% on May 21 resolves to a single ₹59.43 crore Angola invoice from FY 2016-17, formally reclassified after a decade of staleness; the auditor's opinion is unmodified, not qualified. Yesterday's brief framed this as a candidate audit-qualification distress signal under the desk's Lesson 11 pattern. That framing was wrong. The market overshot on Thursday and is now digesting the substance. The HRRL change-order claim remains pending client approval and is the residual housekeeping watchpoint. The one-month drawdown of 7.9% is still intact, but the systemic-governance reading has decisively weakened.

### US-India tariff regime carryforward was outdated

**The US-India tariff overhang is materially smaller than recent briefs assumed.** Recent briefs carried the April 2025 26% US reciprocal tariff as a standing overhang on Indian exporters. Web search this window confirms three corrections. The US Supreme Court struck down the reciprocal tariff framework in February 2026. The February 2 US-India bilateral trade deal lowered India's reciprocal tariff from 25% to 18% in recognition of India halting Russian oil purchases. A May 7 federal court strike-down removed the residual 10% baseline for select plaintiffs. Net: the current tariff burden on Indian exporters to the US is approximately 18%, not 26%. This materially reduces the export-cohort headwind framing applied across Acutaas Chemicals, Carraro India, SJS Enterprises, Inventurus Knowledge Solutions and Emcure Pharmaceuticals in prior briefs. The headline soft-data constraint (HSBC Flash PMI's nineteen-month low on new export orders) remains binding; it is the Hormuz demand overlay doing the work, not tariffs.
