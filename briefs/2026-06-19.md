## What Happened

### Indian IT crashes on Accenture's guidance cut

The day's defining event was imported. Accenture, the global bellwether for offshore IT services, cut its full-year revenue-growth guidance to 3–4% (from 4–5%) and missed on the quarter, and its New York shares fell about 18% — its worst single day on record. That guidance cut broke before our open, so it was a same-session driver here, not an overnight aftershock: Indian IT gapped down and stayed down. Nifty IT closed −3.65% after sinking roughly 6% intraday, the sharpest single-session fall in months. The load-bearing nuance is in Accenture's own commentary — large AI-transformation demand is strong, but AI is reallocating IT budgets, not expanding them. So this reads as a demand-cycle hit to the discretionary-spend recovery the cohort needs, not a company-specific stumble. The book's read: a standing headwind, with the next own-name checkpoint Infosys's July quarter.

### Infosys is the worst Nifty stock, near a five-year low

Infosys, the portfolio's IT holding, took the heaviest blow of any large-cap — it closed near ₹1,035, down about 8.2% on the verified close, the single worst Nifty 50 performer and a fresh roughly five-year low. It underperformed even its own crashing sector by some 4.5 percentage points, because as the largest-cap offshore exporter it is the most direct Indian read-through to Accenture's discretionary-demand warning. There is no Infosys-specific filing or governance event here — the filings desk confirms nothing material was disclosed; this is pure sector-demand beta. Tata Consultancy fell about 6.5% to near a six-year low and the mid-cap peers fell up to 8% in sympathy. The honest framing is that the de-rating has a structural component until Indian IT's own July-quarter commentary on deal pipelines and discretionary spend either confirms or denies the softening.

### Two record IPO filings in one week point to a supply wall

Beneath the IT gloom, the structural story was primary-market supply. Reliance announced at its annual meeting that Jio Platforms filed draft papers Friday for India's largest-ever IPO — a fresh issue of roughly ₹27,500 crore with no promoter selling, every rupee earmarked to repay Jio's overseas borrowings due in 2028. That lands three days after the National Stock Exchange filed its own roughly ₹30,000 crore issue, which is the mirror image: an all-promoter-sale where the exchange itself receives nothing, with the State Bank the largest seller. Two record-breaking filings in one week signal a heavy second-half supply pipeline that is a real drain on secondary-market liquidity to watch. There is no portfolio pure-play, but the NSE sale already lifted New India Assurance (a selling shareholder) +13.23% on the day.

## Cascade Watch

**The Accenture guidance cut ran cohort-wide through Indian IT.** The cause was Accenture's full-year guidance cut to 3–4% and its record 18% New York fall, which broke before our open. The mechanism is the offshore IT-services demand edge: Indian large-caps trade as the proxy for Accenture's discretionary-services demand, so the warning re-rated the whole cohort at once — Infosys about −8.2%, Tata Consultancy about −6.5%, the mid-caps up to −8%. Tomorrow's read: this reverses partially only if the demand scare proves Accenture-specific rather than industry-wide, and the real test is Indian IT's own July-quarter commentary on deal pipelines and discretionary spend.

**Money rotated out of IT and into financials, hospitality and capital goods.** The cause was the same IT shock; the mechanism was the rotation edge — with IT uninvestable for the session, cash moved into rate-sensitive and domestic names. The NBFC cluster ran hard (Tata Capital +6.18%, Piramal Finance +7.74%, Poonawalla +5.49%), hospitality bid (Leela +8.00%, ITC Hotels +6.08%), and the grid / capital-goods cohort lifted (TARIL +10.07% on fresh transmission orders, plus a clutch of T&D names up 5%-plus). Tomorrow's read: this is rotation, not fresh conviction — watch whether the financials bid holds once IT stabilises, with India Shelter the only portfolio name catching the move.

**The water-EPC order edge lifted VA Tech Wabag to a 52-week high.** The cause was a genuine order win — Wabag landed a ₹1,000 crore-plus desalination contract in Kuwait, its first entry into that market. The mechanism is the order-book-accretion edge: a new-geography mega-order on a stock already riding the GCC water-EPC theme drove it +8.88% to a fresh high. Tomorrow's read: this is a contract award, not yet recognised revenue, and a clean positive datapoint on a tracked name — the filings desk holds it at REPORTED pending the primary disclosure on the feed re-run.

**The Hormuz reopening drained the oil-disruption premium, but not cleanly.** The cause was the US-Iran understanding taking effect on the 19th — stranded tankers began exiting Hormuz, producers lifted force majeure, and the naval blockade ended. The mechanism is the crude-to-input edge: cheaper oil eases costs for paints, beverages and airlines and supports the rupee, which is why VBL's input thesis stays intact. But the in-person talks were abruptly postponed and fresh Lebanon fighting flared, so Brent actually rose 0.93% on the day to $80.59 even as it fell sharply on the week. Tomorrow's read: the structural premium is gone, but the residual headline risk (postponed talks, Lebanon) means the lower-oil trade is not one-way.
