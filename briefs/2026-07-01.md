## What Happened

### The trade deal became the market's actual driver
The number that moved the tape today was not an earnings print — it was a negotiation. Commerce Minister Piyush Goyal and US Trade Representative Greer reviewed progress in New Delhi, and the read across desks was that the US-India trade deal is now text-complete, with only final legal and tariff details left. The catch is the gate Goyal named: the deal enters force only once India secures a clear tariff edge over peer manufacturers like Vietnam, Thailand and Bangladesh, and the US Section-122 10% tariff expires 24-July. That dated, broad, still-unsigned catalyst is what carried Nifty back above 24,000 at +0.59%. It is the most consequential thing on the board because it reprices the whole export cohort at once, and it is why today's rotation had a risk-on floor under it rather than fear.

### IT fell to a 3-year low for a third straight day
The vivid story sat on the other side of the tape. Nifty IT dropped another 2.01% to a fresh 3-year low, now down about 42% from its December-2024 peak, and it was the only heavyweight sector in the red on an otherwise green day. Infosys (INFY), the book's single IT holding, closed at a fresh 52-week low of ₹985.30, down 20.8% on the month — a serious thesis drawdown, though it actually held up slightly better than the index it sits in. The drivers are structural, not a one-session wobble: an AI-disruption re-rating of the labour-arbitrage model, a KPIT Q1FY27 profit warning on a European-automaker slowdown read across the sector, and cautious client-spend commentary into the mid-July print season. This is a watch-for-entry-discipline item, not a signal to act — the binding checkpoint is INFY's own Q1FY27 result on 23-July, with the trade deal a latent offsetting tailwind if it enters force by 24-July.

### Crude relief and a strong macro spine drove the domestic bid
The green corner had real fuel behind it, not just sentiment. Brent fell 2.19% to $71.32 on US-Iran Doha de-escalation and resumed Gulf flows, leaving crude down roughly 23% for the quarter — a direct input-cost relief that let FMCG lead at +2.08%, with Dabur +5.34%, Nestle +3.46% and Hindustan Unilever +3.01%. The macro tape corroborated the mood: June GST came in at ₹1,94,812 crore, up 13.9% year-on-year, and June auto wholesale was strong with Maruti +19.3% and Mahindra +37% on record SUVs. But the internals carry an honest caveat — the GST beat was import-led while the domestic component grew only 6.5%, and June Manufacturing PMI slipped to a 3-month low of 54.2 on the weakest export orders since March-2023. So the read is a firm-but-not-accelerating domestic demand picture, with the export-facing cohort quietly softening underneath.

**The crude-import edge deepened and drove the FMCG rally directly.** The cause was Brent down 2.19% to $71.32 on US-Iran de-escalation and resumed Gulf flows, with the quarter closing about 23% lower. The mechanism is the input-cost-and-rupee edge: cheaper crude relieves packaging, logistics and paints, letting consumer firms hold price and protect margin. Tomorrow's read: this is now a structural cost tailwind for OMCs, paints, tyres and FMCG, not a fear input, and it only re-arms as a risk on a fresh Strait shock.

**The monsoon edge tightened on DHANUKA without flipping a trigger.** The cause is the first-week-July Bay-of-Bengal low delivering heavy Kharif-supportive rain, set against a full-July below-normal forecast. The mechanism is the agri-input demand-swing edge on a Kharif-bound name.
