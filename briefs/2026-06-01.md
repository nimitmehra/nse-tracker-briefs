## What Happened

### Iran flipped hawkish and the macro overlay re-priced

The Friday tape's relief read is gone. Iran on Monday suspended indirect communications with the US over Israel's expanded operations in Lebanon and Gaza, declared the Strait of Hormuz fully closed until those operations stop, and the US CENTCOM struck Iranian radar and command-and-control sites at Goruk and Qeshm Island — both along the Strait itself. WTI crude jumped roughly 7% above $94 a barrel intraday; Brent settled $95.14, up 3.36%. Every macro chain that touches energy input cost re-prices to the hawkish side — Brent, sulphur, rupee, the consumer price path, the rate-meeting framing, and the Middle East order tail for the engineering contractors. The portfolio's direct hit lands on Coromandel International (COROMANDEL — sulphur supply), Engineers India (ENGINERSIN — Middle East order tail) and the wider EPC cohort; the partial offset is the durable Cabinet-approved Kharif fertiliser subsidy. The de-escalation tail that Friday's tape had retired is now actively negative.

### The monsoon went to a 11-year low and the FMCG cohort cracked

The weather office downgraded the June-September monsoon forecast to 90% of long-period average — down from 92% earlier — which is the weakest reading since 2015's 87% El Niño year. June rainfall is expected below normal across most of India; El Niño is weak in June and moderates-to-strong by September. The Monday tape priced the food-inflation upside immediately. Nifty FMCG fell 2.30% — Hindustan Unilever down 2.87%, Tata Consumer down 2.83%, ITC down 2.65% — and PSU Bank fell 1.85% as the rate-meeting framing hardened. The portfolio's most-exposed names are Varun Beverages (VBL — partial urban-summer-demand insulation against rural-FMCG drag) and Blue Star (BLUESTARCO — hotter-summer cooling-demand window helps, rural discretionary weakens). The framing compounds with the Iran fuel-cost push into Friday's rate decision.

### IT was the only green sector and Infosys led the portfolio

Nifty IT rallied 2.66% on the close (intraday peak +3.79% / +1,001 pts to 30,082) on the back of strong US Snowflake and Okta earnings — a clean signal that US corporate AI and cloud spend is robust. Infosys (INFY) closed +4.29% — the portfolio's clearest positive Monday print and the biggest single-day move on the name in months. Tech Mahindra +3.71%, TCS +1.66%, Persistent Systems +5.18% and Coforge +4.18% rounded out the cohort lift. Friday's MSCI mechanical drag on Infosys is more than reversed. The mechanism is rupee-defensive plus US-export concentration plus AI-spend resilience — read it as cohort safe-haven flow on a risk-off tape, not yet a fundamental IT-spend thesis turn. The structural AI-deflation overhang has a meaningful counter-datum from Snowflake-Okta; one Monday print does not settle it.

### Dee Development calls a Wednesday board on a preferential issue

Dee Development Engineers (DEEDEV) — the tier-one promotion from Friday — filed the Regulation 29 board-meeting intimation for Wednesday 03 June to consider a preferential equity fundraising plus the consequential shareholder approval. That ties the Saturday cluster together: the ₹386.86-crore BPCL piping order delivering to February 2028, the same-day insider trading window closure, and now the planned capital raise. The arc reads as a coherent transparent capital-action sequence funding execution on the BPCL win — not a distress arc. The Wednesday outcome is the top read of the week — the issue size as a share of post-issue capital, the allottee names, the pricing band and the end-use disclosure decide whether this stays a benign growth-capital raise or escalates to a fund-mobilisation forensic flag. Welspun Corp (WELCORP) added a separate fact today — up 2.38% on a fresh ₹700-crore US large-diameter pipe order taking the global book to ₹25,350 crore.
