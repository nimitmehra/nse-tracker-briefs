## What Happened

### Foreign selling stopped — and the flow flipped to buying
The single most consequential change since Thursday is in the flows, not the session. Foreign investors turned net buyers, roughly ₹708 crore across the first three July sessions, reversing the multi-session sell-streak that led the 2-July brief; today alone foreign institutions bought a net ₹243 crore while domestic institutions added ₹3,791 crore, both on the same side for once. That flip is the macro spine under an otherwise unremarkable +0.66% Nifty — a broad, orderly advance rather than one sector's rip, with volatility pinned near its floor and crude still soft. For the principal the read is that the foreign-outflow pressure flagged as the standing risk has, at least for now, released; it does not change any single position, but it lowers the deployment friction that a persistent seller was imposing on the whole book.

### The IT Q1FY27 season opens under a guidance-cut cloud
The results calendar that decides the book's one IT holding is now dated and carries a warning. Tata Consultancy Services reports first on 9-July (with an interim dividend, record date 15-July), then Infosys (INFY) on 23-July — the checkpoint that matters. Nomura's pre-season note pre-marks FY27 growth "anaemic" and expects INFY to trim the upper end of its guidance by about 50 basis points, HCL Technologies by about 100; a single-desk call, so it is a magnitude input, not the conclusion. INFY sat almost flat at ₹1,042.20, still near its 52-week low with the whole cohort at fresh lows and Nifty IT the day's only red sector. The read is unchanged from Thursday: the thesis stays under pressure into the print, and the 23-July result — not the intervening sessions — is the arbiter. No add.

### WAAREERTL's feared US-customs ruling already landed — and it missed the listed company
The adverse event prior briefs watched for "around 8-July" turns out to be stale. US Customs and Border Protection issued its determination on **23-June**, and it landed on the separately-listed parent Waaree Energies, not on the Waaree Renewable (WAAREERTL) we hold: it found solar-cell duty evasion routed through Vietnam and Malaysia (cash deposits up to 271.28%) but **declined to apply adverse inferences**, acknowledged no Chinese-cell modules were exported from Indian facilities, and Waaree says the US business is unaffected. Because the adverse outcome that would have fired the EXIT-half plan for the listed entity never materialised, the graph de-escalated the regulatory watch from elevated back to watching and cleared the stale 8-July date. The read: stand down the ruling watch; the second leg (an income-tax charge) stays open, so the trigger holds at watching, not resolved.

### The rate-cut-hope bid ran through realty while the bank giants split
The day's leadership was rate-sensitive. Nifty Realty rose 1.81% — up 18.6% on the month, by far the strongest sector — on a broad, unconcentrated bid: Fed-cut expectations lowering global discount rates, crude easing toward the low-$70s cutting construction input costs, and institutional inflows into the sector all compounding ahead of Q1 developer results. Underneath the flat-looking banks, the two private-bank giants split hard on their Q1 business updates — HDFC Bank rose 3.6% on ~15.4% loan growth while Kotak Mahindra fell 3.89% on deposit growth read as soft — which is the whole reason Nifty Bank (+0.61%) lagged the index. The read-through is the same soft-rates, soft-oil regime that helps the rate-sensitive and input-cost cohorts, with no portfolio name directly in the leadership.
