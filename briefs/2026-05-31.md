## What Happened

### Friday was an index-rebalance day, not just an Iran day

The dominant Friday narrative flipped over the weekend. The MSCI May rebalance went effective at Friday's close and pushed foreign investors into a one-day net sale of ₹20,637 crore — the largest single-day foreign sell ever recorded — while domestic institutions absorbed it with a record ₹16,260 crore buy. NSE cash turnover hit an all-time high of ₹2.87 trillion. The May derivatives expiry, shifted onto Friday by the Bakri Eid holiday, piled positioning churn onto the same session. Infosys (INFY), HDFC Bank, Reliance, Hyundai and Hindustan Unilever were named on the passive-selling victim list — a mechanical-flow explanation for late-Friday weakness independent of the Iran headline. The rupee's 1.10% slide against a flat dollar index fits the same outflow signature. The $800 million to $1 billion magnitude estimate stays a single-desk range; the ₹20,637 crore record is the confirmed primary tape.

### The Iran understanding stays unsigned, and Washington toughened the demand

Over the weekend the US-Iran 60-day truce moved the wrong way. CBS News reported that President Trump "recently edited" the draft. He added Strait of Hormuz reopening, enriched-uranium constraints and an unfreezing of Iranian assets. Axios reports those additions "did not go down well in Tehran." The US defence secretary warned the military is "ready to resume combat in the Gulf if needed." Brent closed Friday near $91 and bounced 1.30% Sunday evening to $93.25, still 18% below its peak — a small post-walk-back rebound, not a trend break. The sign-off binary is now live rather than receding. That keeps four threads two-sided into Monday: the rupee, the sulphur input behind Coromandel International (COROMANDEL), the Middle East order tail for the engineering contractors, and the inflation framing for the June 3-5 rate meeting.

### Three Monday-open catalysts land at the same time

A signed solar policy hit, a four-day trade round and a record-quarter results window all open today. The renewable energy ministry's domestic-cell sourcing mandate (ALMM List-II) takes effect today — squeezing pure solar developers who must now buy domestic cells at higher cost on older low-bid power deals, and rewarding cell manufacturers. The four-day India-US trade round begins today in Washington with Brendan Lynch and Darpan Jain, with a proposed tariff cut from 25% to 18% on the table; the commerce minister calls the deal "close" but flags competitor-tariff clarity as a precondition. And Friday June 5 will land twin macro events on the same day — the RBI rate decision and the fourth-quarter GDP print, which the statistics office rescheduled from May 29. The week is dense; today's open is the smaller of those bets.

### Dynacons, Dee Development and Glenmark hand Monday three corporate catalysts

Two tier-one prints landed clean and one tier-two deal landed material. Dynacons Systems & Solutions (DSSL) held its Saturday board meeting and filed full-year revenue ₹1,424 crore (+12%), EBITDA up 38% to ₹152 crore on margin expansion from 8.84% to 10.65%, and profit ₹84.8 crore (+17%) on an unmodified audit opinion — a clean print and the thesis-strengthener. Dee Development Engineers (DEEDEV), the tier-one promotion from Friday, won a ₹386.86-crore Bharat Petroleum piping order with delivery to February 2028, locked the 5% upper circuit, and shut its insider trading window ahead of a June 27 special shareholder meeting — the agenda is not yet disclosed. Glenmark Pharmaceuticals (GLENMARK) filed full-year profit ₹1,362 crore and disclosed an AbbVie out-licensing deal worth $700 million upfront and up to $1.925 billion potential — roughly 9% of market capitalisation in immediate cash — alongside a ring-fenced ~₹323-crore US generic-drug price-fixing settlement that de-risks a multi-year overhang. None of the three caused Friday's tape; all three are Monday's read.
