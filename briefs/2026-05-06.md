### Lloyds Metals lays down 310 percent revenue, then opens a Bougainville chapter

Lloyds Metals and Energy (LLOYDSME), the integrated iron-ore-to-pellet-to-DRI-to-steel operator anchored at Konsari and Surjagarh in Maharashtra's Gadchiroli district, filed Q4FY26 results on Tuesday evening that materially exceeded both the prior quarter and the consensus framing carried into the print: revenue of ₹4,977 crore (up 310 percent year on year), EBITDA of ₹1,679 crore at a 33.7 percent margin (up 1,058 basis points), and PAT of ₹1,066 crore (up 426 percent), against an FY26 EBITDA of ₹4,673 crore measured against the ₹5,500 crore thesis-upgrade threshold — the gate has NOT cleared on an annualised basis (the trigger remains active, awaiting the May 15 quarterly print to confirm the trajectory). Auditor Todarwal & Todarwal LLP issued an unmodified opinion. Five further board items landed in the same outcome: a ₹700 crore non-convertible debenture tranche under the previously approved ₹2,500 crore limit, an additional ₹2,500 crore enabling NCD authorisation taking total NCD headroom to roughly ₹5,000 crore, the re-appointment of two independent directors for second five-year terms, an Articles of Association modernisation, and — the most thesis-shaping item — the acquisition of equity in Lloyds Panguna Metals and Energy Limited (a Papua New Guinea step-down subsidiary held through Lloyds Global Resources FZCO, the company's existing UAE-incorporated wholly-owned subsidiary) for the purpose of engaging with Bougainville Copper Limited on the Panguna Mine, the copper deposit in the Autonomous Region of Bougainville that has been closed since the 1989 civil war. The Reg 30 cover text frames the engagement as a *"long-term cooperation and mining agreement"* and describes the new entity as a *"dedicated project and engagement vehicle"* — open-ended language that preserves flexibility on consideration, equity stake percentage, and timing milestones, none of which were specified.
