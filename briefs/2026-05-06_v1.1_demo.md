## What Happened

### Coforge celebrates a wedding

**Coforge celebrates a wedding.** Shares of the IT-services firm jumped 8.8 percent on Wednesday — the day's largest single-stock move, almost eight standard deviations above its weekly distribution — as three filings ratified its absorption of Cigniti Technologies, a Hyderabad testing specialist. The amalgamation took effect on May 5 after the National Company Law Tribunal's sanction; the fourth-quarter board outcome followed; a record date of May 16 was set for the share swap. The interim dividend was deferred, lest incoming Cigniti shareholders be cheated of it. Sudhir Singh, chief executive since 2017, has framed the post-merger arc as lifting Cigniti's EBITDA margin from eleven percent to nineteen over six quarters — that is now the verifiable promise. One day's tape vote, however, is not yet a re-rating.

### Lloyds Metals reports, then wagers

**Lloyds Metals reports, then wagers.** The integrated iron-and-steel firm posted a thunderous quarter: revenue up 310 percent to ₹4,977 crore, margins fattening 1,058 basis points to 33.7 percent, profit more than quintupling. The market shrugged — shares slipped 0.4 percent, having already run 60 percent in three months. Beneath the print, two longer-dated questions opened. The board approved equity in a Papua New Guinea vehicle to court Bougainville Copper, owner of a mine shut since civil war in 1989; consideration, stake and timing went unspecified. A separate disclosure outed Thriveni Earthmovers — the counterparty to a related-party transaction nodded through on April 29 as "ordinary course of business" — as a promoter-group affiliate. The vote, retrospectively, was not arms-length. India's market regulator will now want a closer look.

### Stagflation regime unwinds in a session

**Yesterday's stagflation pricing reversed across every axis.** Brent eased 1.6 percent to $108.13 — the week's minus 8.4 percent the cleanest read yet that the Hormuz war-premium has decompressed since Iran's May 4 strike on Fujairah. The rupee strengthened for the first daily session of the run, to ₹95.09 from Tuesday's record low. The dollar index fell the same amount, putting USDINR-DXY into daily alignment for the first time since late April. India VIX cooled to 17.56, its first sub-18 print of the cycle. Four sector indices led on a flat-Nifty day — PSU Bank, Auto, IT, Bank — with nine of eleven sectors closing positive. The Goldman-JPMorgan-Bernstein bear trio's $110-plus Brent thesis is now formally on trial. One day's mean reversion, however, is not regime exit.

### Hindustan Zinc — three events in seventy-two hours

**The HINDZINC chapter sharpens.** Vedanta's five-way demerger took effect on May 1, splitting the parent's aluminium, power, oil-and-gas, steel and residual businesses; the residual still holds 60–63 percent of HINDZINC. Six hours later came a SEBI administrative warning letter dated April 30, citing related-party-disclosure failures across two non-consecutive years, FY22 and FY25. A day before that, four independent directors had quit within an eighteen-minute window. The credit market followed — CRISIL placed Vedanta's ₹4,500 crore non-convertible debentures on rating-watch-developing. The cadence is no longer coincidence-shaped; it reads as a regulator-and-board response to a corporate-restructuring event of the highest order. The next test is the successor-CFO appointment expected by May 30; whether the name comes from outside the Vedanta bench or inside it will tell.

### L&T sells off on a Middle East quarter

**Larsen and Toubro fell 3.4 percent — the day's largest single-name decline.** The conglomerate's fourth-quarter profit slid 3 percent on the year despite revenue rising 11 percent to ₹82,762 crore and an order book that hit a record ₹7.4 trillion. The disconnect was geographic. International revenue is 53 percent of L&T's mix, the West Asia portfolio is at the centre of that mix, and the Middle East conflict has begun to disrupt project execution. *Business Standard* carried that framing verbatim from management. The tape voted accordingly. Whether the disruption is a one-quarter event or a multi-quarter overhang is the question for the July call.
