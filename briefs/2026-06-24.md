## What Happened

### The recovery's cause was the collapsing crude risk-premium — real supply, not rhetoric
The session was made by oil. The Iran/oil-relief window the desks have tracked since late February **materialised into actual supply**: Washington granted Iran a **60-day licence to sell oil internationally**, tankers are **resuming Strait of Hormuz transit**, and Gulf producers (Kuwait lifting force majeure, ADNOC resuming) are raising output. Brent fell **−4.54% to $73.58 — the lowest since before the 28-Feb strikes, down ~28.9% on the month.** For an oil importer this is the single most consequential, *durable* positive available: it eases the import bill, the current-account math, inflation, and the rupee (which strengthened +0.43% to 94.29 even as the dollar index firmed — an India-specific move). It directly lifts OMCs, aviation (IndiGo +4.95%), paints and tyres (crude-derivative inputs). This is the Lead by consequence — broad and lasting — outranking the one-day index pop in shelf-life.

### The banks led on falling yields — the RBI FCNR window is a mid-June backdrop, not today's trigger
The same-day driver of the bank-led rally was **falling bond yields** (1Y −10.2bps, 10Y −5.3bps) — the rate-sensitive read-through of the crude/inflation relief — plus the risk-on recovery and the DII bid. The often-cited RBI FCNR(B) measure is a **17-Jun rule** (banks may lend to non-residents against FCNR(B) deposits, extending the 8-Jun special swap window, fresh deposits to 30-Sep, RBI bearing the hedging cost): a real, supportive **balance-of-payments defence** (RBI defending the rupee via the capital account, not rates) — but it is a week-old backdrop, NOT a 24-Jun catalyst. Crediting it as "today's trigger" (as the desks initially did) is a causal-timing error; the same-day catalyst was the yield move. The whole financial cohort responded: ICICI +2.64%, HDFC Bank +2.39%, Axis/Kotak/IndusInd up to +2.7%, Bajaj Finance +2.97%, Shriram Finance +2.58%; Nifty Bank +1.69% (1M +5.17%, the strongest large-cap pocket), Private Bank +1.85%, FinServ +1.54%. Falling yields (1Y −10.2bps, 10Y −5.3bps) added a rate-sensitive tailwind that also lifted Realty +2.17%.

### No paradox today — and IT's bounce is recovery, not reversal
Tuesday was the EM-rotation paradox (good local news, market fell on an imported global storm). **Today is its clean inverse:** the global tape stabilised (KOSPI +3-4% on Samsung's ₩90tn buyback + SK Hynix's accelerated US ADR — Korea-specific short-covering after the double circuit-breaker), and India added two of its *own* positive legs (RBI + Iran/oil). Good news, market up, internally consistent — no paradox to flag. **The discipline note:** Nifty IT +2.05% was the top sector but is a *reflexive bounce within a downtrend* (IT still 1W −4.31% / 1M −4.88%; Infosys +2.65% on the day but −8.73% on the week) — on trade-deal optimism (a possible signing before 24-Jul, **rhetoric not a signed text — REPORTED, not action**) + global tech rebound.
