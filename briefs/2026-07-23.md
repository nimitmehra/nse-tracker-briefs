## What Happened

### Oil above $100 puts a fourth down day on the board
This was the fourth straight down session, and it was a clean, external oil shock. Overnight brought fresh Houthi drone-and-missile strikes on two Saudi tankers in the Red Sea and Iranian strikes on two UAE tankers in the Strait of Hormuz, plus Kazakhstan halting its CPC export line — and Brent crossed $100 a barrel intraday for the first time since May, up about 5–6% on the day. For a country that imports roughly 85% of its crude, that is a live cost line for tyres, auto-components, paints, aviation and logistics, and a straight negative for the current account. The rupee sagged to a fresh record low near 96.56, and foreign funds sold ₹2,999 crore, offset almost exactly by domestic buying of ₹2,947 crore. The graph moved the crude trigger from elevated to active. One honest wrinkle: gold fell 2.2% even as oil surged, so this is two channels running at once — Iran on oil, the dollar and rates on metals — not a single clean war scare.

### Infosys printed after the close, so the vote is Friday's
Infosys, a portfolio holding, released its June-quarter result at 16:20, after the 15:30 close — so today's −0.45% is a pre-result move, not a verdict, and Friday's session is where the market votes. The quarter itself beat: revenue rose 14% to ₹48,211 crore, profit rose 12.2% to ₹7,769 crore, and the operating margin held at 21.1% with a strong $3.6 billion of large-deal wins. But the company narrowed the top end of its FY27 growth guidance to 1.5–3.0% from 1.5–3.5%, a modestly cautious read on discretionary demand, and named Ashiss Kumar Dash as chief-executive designate to succeed Salil Parekh from 1-April-2027. It is an orderly, insider succession with an eight-month runway, not a distress exit — but a beat-the-quarter-trim-the-outlook result on the season's marquee IT name is the swing factor for the whole services cohort tomorrow.

### The 24-July tariff cliff is now one day out and still unsigned
The temporary US tariff arrangement on Indian goods expires tomorrow, 24-July, and there is still no signed pact and no published text — the deal sits at the "last 1%" of legal drafting, targeting an 18% reciprocal tariff. A second clock runs in parallel: the broader reciprocal-tariff suspension was extended to 1-August, so there are two dates, not one. This bears most directly on Garware Hi-Tech Films, the book's most tariff-levered name, where the damage is already booked — a recent quarter showed revenue down 8.2% and profit-line pressure attributed specifically to US tariffs. A no-deal lapse extends that pressure; a signed 18% pact caps it. It is a genuine one-day binary with a real chance of no deal.

### Waaree Renewable fell 7% on a strong-but-softening quarter
Waaree Renewable Technologies, a holding, was the worst Tier-1 performer, down 7.44%, on its first APSPL-consolidated June quarter. The headline was strong — revenue up 53% to ₹924 crore and profit up 38% to ₹119 crore year-on-year, with an unexecuted order book above ₹5,300 crore. But sequentially the quarter softened: revenue fell 16% and profit 24% versus the March quarter, and the operating margin slipped to 18.77% from 19.49%. The market booked profit. This is a sell-the-news on a richly-valued renewable-EPC name where a +38% profit result no longer clears the bar without margin expansion. The long-cycle order-book story is intact; the drop is multiple compression plus risk-off beta, not a thesis break.
