## What Happened

### Friday's twin macro morning is the week's decision

The Reserve Bank's rate decision lands at 10 AM Friday alongside the fourth-quarter GDP release, and the bond market spent Thursday positioning the opposite way to Wednesday. Economists are firmly expecting a hold at 5.25% for a third straight meeting with a neutral stance — Gita Gopinath and SBI Research both flag a pause despite the rupee pressure. The one-year yield fell 5.1 basis points and the ten-year 3.1 basis points, a front-end rally that prices a comfortable hold rather than a hawkish one; the proximate cause is the crude collapse, which relieves the fuel-price inflation worry the committee was weighing. GDP is seen near 7.2% for the quarter and 7.4% for the year. For the rate-sensitive book — Jio Financial and India Shelter — the front-end relief is the constructive read into the meeting.

### Crude reversed the Hormuz premium — the physical floor is finally lifting

Brent fell 2.88% to $94.99 and WTI 3.36% to $92.79, reversing three sessions of supply-shock gains, as Reuters and CNBC reported Hormuz shipping traffic has picked up over the past two weeks with some vessels moving under US-military coordination. This is the corroboration Wednesday's ceasefire talk lacked — the easing now shows in both the diplomatic tone and the shipping data, not just rhetoric. For India, which imports about 85% of its crude, a 3% Brent drop eases the import bill, refiner margins and the current-account pressure that has been building. It also reads through as input-cost relief for the fertiliser and engineering-contractor cohorts (Coromandel, and Engineers India and Power Mech in the book), and it should mechanically soften the rupee into Friday's data.

### The rupee keeps weakening even as crude and the dollar fall

The rupee slipped 0.54% to 95.775 against the dollar — but the dollar itself fell 0.29% on the day, so this is India-specific pressure, not a broad dollar move. With crude collapsing too, the usual import-bill relief channel should have helped the rupee; instead it weakened, which points to foreign-investor selling dominating. The flow drag is structural — foreign portfolio outflows are past $26 billion year-to-date, already above last year's full record, and the central bank can lean against that but not reverse it. This is the cleanest cross-asset signal of the day and the one to watch alongside Friday's decision; a weak rupee is a mild margin tailwind for the export-led names (TD Power, Acutaas) but a sentiment overhang for the broader book.

### IRCTC hit a five-year low — a thesis checkpoint, not an earnings break

Indian Railway Catering & Tourism (IRCTC), a portfolio holding, traded to a five-year low this week, down roughly 60% from its all-time high, with Business Standard running a "is the monopoly story over?" framing. The fall is perception-driven — the market has shifted IRCTC from a high-growth digital platform to a mature, heavily-regulated business and compressed the multiple accordingly — not an earnings break: the most recent quarter grew profit 15.6% and revenue 18.4%. The stock bounced 3.27% Thursday on the calm session, an oversold relief move with no fresh own-name datum. The read for the principal is that this is a narrative-deterioration checkpoint worth a hard look at whether the regulated-monopoly multiple has further to compress, not a reason to act today; the Tatkal and dynamic-pricing levers are the regulated-revenue swing factors.

### Two BUY-name positives — Power Mech's Adani order, Dhanuka's buyback

Power Mech Projects (POWERMECH), a BUY-rated holding, rose 4.03% to a fresh 52-week high on a confirmed ₹266.26-crore operations-and-maintenance order from an Adani group company for a Maharashtra thermal plant. It is a five-year annuity, the counterparty named and explicitly flagged as not a related-party deal — exactly the recurring, higher-margin revenue the de-risking thesis rests on. Separately Dhanuka Agritech (DHANUKA), also BUY-rated, opened its sixth buyback Thursday: a tender at ₹1,400 a share, a 28% premium to the market price, closing 10 June. A buyback at that premium opening at the cyclical trough is a credible capital-return signal that supports the agrochemical recovery read, though the thesis still lives or dies on how the June-September monsoon actually prints.
