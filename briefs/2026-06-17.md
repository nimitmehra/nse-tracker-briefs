## What Happened

### Defence re-rates on a production milestone

The standout was a broad defence re-rating, not a single stock. The Ministry of Defence announced that FY26 indigenous defence production hit an all-time high of ₹1.78 lakh crore, and the whole order-and-indigenisation cohort lifted on it. Paras Defence led the defence names with a +6.41% close at a fresh all-time high, helped by its own ₹80.28 crore DRDO order for a precision optical air-defence system; the producer's feed had logged an intraday spike to about +18% that faded into the close, so the +6.41% close is the figure used here, and the multi-period returns in the table carry that same intraday-print caveat. while Hindustan Aeronautics rose 4.81%, Bharat Dynamics 5.91%, Garden Reach 5.83% and Bharat Electronics 3.02%. There is no direct defence holding in the book, but the read-through reaches the portfolio's edge: Engineers India, the PSU engineering-consultancy name, rose 4.71% riding the same public-sector-capex bid. The graph logged a defence-sector node candidate but did not create it this run — it stays a watch surface, not yet a tracked macro node. The honest read is that this is a sector momentum event on a real government datapoint, worth tracking for a clean entry, not a same-day deployment trigger.

### Trent's 7% jump is a real earnings beat

The cleanest single-stock catalyst of the day sat just outside the book. Trent, the Tata-group retailer, rose 7.07% to ₹3,102.80 as the top Nifty gainer on a Q4FY26 result that beat estimates: the operating margin widened 263 basis points to 18.6% against roughly 16% expected, and net profit grew 30% to ₹455 crore. Volume ran about five times its 20-day average. Citi kept a "sell" rating but flagged strong demand across categories — and on our discipline the result is the cause, the broker note is colour, not the driver. This is a thesis-strengthener for a discovery-grade consumption name, not a portfolio holding. It is worth a research look on the next print, but the stock is richly valued and the entry question is price, not story.

### The FII buy-turn arrived — but feebly

Yesterday's most-watched thread resolved, and the answer is sobering. After thirteen straight sessions of selling, foreign investors turned net buyers on 17-June — but only by ₹101.59 crore in the cash segment, against domestic institutions buying ₹1,561.40 crore. That ₹102 crore is a rounding error against a 2026 year-to-date foreign outflow north of ₹2.2 lakh crore. So the correct framing, tighter than yesterday's, is that foreign selling has paused, not that foreign money is returning — domestic flows are still doing all the lifting, which is why mid- and small-caps again outpaced the frontline. A Jefferies survey of large funds found 61% still underweight India; the feeble buy-turn is consistent with that positioning. One genuinely next-session input sits overnight: the US Federal Reserve decision and dot-plot land near midnight IST tonight, after today's close and under new Chair Kevin Warsh, so nothing in today's session traces to it. Consensus is a hold at 3.50–3.75%, but the outcome is unverified — it is Thursday's input. The Geneva US–Iran signing follows on Friday the 19th.
