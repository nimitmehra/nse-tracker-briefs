## What Happened

### The RBI decision and Q4 GDP land this morning, both unconfirmed

The Reserve Bank's rate verdict comes at 10 AM and the fourth-quarter GDP release the same morning, so you are reading this around the actual announcements rather than after them. Going in, the consensus was a third straight hold at the 5.25% repo rate with a neutral stance — Business Standard, BusinessToday and Univest all flagged a defensive pause given the rupee at record lows and the West Asia energy shock. Q4 growth is seen near 7.3% and the full year near 7.4%, but the signed RBI statement and the official MoSPI number had not propagated into coverage at our write time, so both are carried as consensus, not fact. The bond market already positioned for a comfortable hold — the one-year yield fell 5.1 basis points and the ten-year 3.1. For the rate-sensitive book — Jio Financial and the newly-promoted India Shelter — the front-end relief is the constructive read into a hold; the dissent count and forward-guidance wording are what to read first when the statement lands.

### The rupee stays India-specific weak — the outflow channel is structural

The rupee held near 95.78 to the dollar even though the dollar barely moved and crude kept falling, which is the cleanest signal of the overnight session. With Brent down and the usual import-bill relief working in the rupee's favour, it should have firmed; instead it stayed weak, which isolates foreign-investor selling as the dominant force. The drag is now confirmed structural — foreign portfolio outflows have crossed ₹2 lakh crore for 2026 so far, and aggregate foreign ownership of Indian listed stocks sits at a fourteen-year low of 14.7%. Kotak frames this as India losing relative appeal versus emerging-market peers rather than a broad risk-off, with annual capital inflows collapsing from a $73 billion average to an expected outflow this year. The read for the principal is that a single RBI hold cannot reverse this — the rupee is a sentiment overhang on the whole book, a mild margin tailwind only for the export names like TD Power and Acutaas.

### India Shelter joins the portfolio track as a fresh BUY

India Shelter Finance, the affordable-housing lender, came back a BUY at conviction 7/10 with the stress test confirming the verdict, and it moves onto the portfolio watch track today. The thesis rests on best-in-cohort asset quality — bad loans improved to 1.20% of the book — the widest lending spread in the group on a falling cost of funds, and the cheapest valuation among peers at roughly 17 times earnings. The stress test confirmed all that but cut the proposed upgrade back to 7/10. The one risk the original work skipped is that fresh lending growth has stalled at +11% year-on-year for two straight quarters — the indicator the whole re-rating hangs on. The stock fell 2.49% Thursday on housing-finance softness into the rate day, with no own-name news. The deployment read is a tranche-one starter position now, with the real add gated on first-quarter lending growth re-accelerating above 20%, not on the June-29 shareholder ballot.

### Crude's Hormuz premium keeps unwinding — input-cost relief holds

Brent fell another 2.57% to $95.30 and WTI 3.13% to $93.01, extending the reversal of the supply-shock spike as Hormuz shipping traffic recovers, some vessels moving under US-military coordination though volumes stay below pre-conflict levels. For India, which imports about 85% of its crude, the continued easing relieves the import bill, refiner margins and the current-account pressure the rate committee was weighing. It reads through as input-cost relief for the fertiliser and engineering-contractor cohorts — Coromandel, plus Engineers India and Power Mech in the book — and it softens the inflation worry the RBI was carrying into this morning's decision. The risk is the premium re-firming on any single shipping setback, which keeps this a watch rather than a settled relief.
