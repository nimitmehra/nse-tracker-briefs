## What Happened

### Private-bank margins buckled — HDFC and Axis both fell ~5%
The day's damage came from two names. HDFC Bank fell 5.12% and Axis Bank 5.46% after both filed June-quarter results over the weekend, and both were sold for the same reason: their lending margin is shrinking. HDFC Bank's net interest margin — what it earns on loans minus what it pays for deposits — dropped to 3.26%, a record low, down from 3.38% the prior quarter, and its interest income missed the Street; the market looked past a 5% profit rise to the thinning margin. Axis Bank's headline profit rose 23%, but that was flattered by a 43.7% cut in bad-loan provisions; strip that out and its margin fell hard, to 3.46% from 3.62%, so the market sold the operating quarter, not the headline. Together these two top-weight banks did the bulk of Bank Nifty's 0.98% fall. The read is a margin-cycle signal for the whole large private-bank cohort — rate-cut pass-through plus a shift toward lower-yielding corporate loans — not a one-off miss.

### But the fall was narrow — money rotated into PSU banks, and domestic buyers absorbed it
This was not a risk-off day beneath the index. On the same session PSU banks rose 2.78%, the day's decisive winner, led by Punjab National Bank's 5.66% jump on a June-quarter profit up 213%; pharma rose 1.40% and mid- and small-caps both closed higher. Foreign investors sold ₹1,121 crore in cash, but domestic institutions bought ₹1,312 crore — the by-now-standard pattern where the home bid more than absorbs the foreign exit. With the fear gauge easing to 12.98, the honest framing is a stock-specific two-bank earnings day inside an otherwise calm, well-supported market: capital moved out of record-low-margin private banks straight into cheaper PSU banks printing triple-digit profit growth.

### Two macro binaries are now live into the week
Underneath the earnings noise, two scheduled risks armed further. First, the temporary US tariff arrangement on Indian goods lapses 24-July — now four days out — and the first-phase India-US trade pact is still at the "last 1%" of legal text with no signed document; it bears directly on the book's US-exporting names, Garware Hi-Tech Films most of all, then Welspun Corp's export leg and the IT cohort. Second, the weekend brought a hard US–Iran escalation: the ceasefire collapsed, Strait-of-Hormuz transit vessels were targeted, and Brent touched $90 before easing to ~$88.5. That flipped our oil-premium trigger to elevated. Rising crude is no longer a headline — it is a cost line already visible in tyre and auto-component margins, and the names in that channel are the ones to watch.

## Cascade Watch

**The private-bank margin edge fired, and it is a cohort signal, not a two-stock story.** The cause was HDFC Bank and Axis Bank both reporting shrinking lending margins for the June quarter — HDFC's at a record-low 3.26%. The mechanism is the large-private-bank net-interest-margin edge: rate-cut pass-through onto loans plus a mix shift into lower-yielding corporate lending compresses spreads across the cohort at once. The effect is a live read-across to the tracked financials — JIOFIN (T1) and IDFCFIRSTB (T2) sit in the same funding-cost channel, though both are differentiated (JIOFIN is fee- and asset-management-led; IDFCFIRSTB runs a higher-yield retail book targeting 6%+ margins). Read the coming August financial results against this margin-pressure backdrop.

**The intra-financials money-shift edge did the mirror trade.** The cause was the same private-bank sell-off. The mechanism is a valuation-and-earnings shift within financials: when the market dumps record-low-margin private banks, capital does not leave the sector — it moves to cheaper PSU banks printing faster profit growth. The effect showed cleanly today: Nifty PSU Bank +2.78%, PNB +5.66% on a 213% profit jump, Union Bank +4.43% — nine of twelve PSU-bank constituents up. This is money moving within the sector, not a financials-wide sell-off.

**The oil-premium edge escalated to elevated.** The cause was the weekend US–Iran escalation — ceasefire collapse, Hormuz transit vessels targeted, Brent to $90 intraday. The mechanism is the crude-input-cost edge: a sustained Brent premium lands as a margin line in tyre, auto-component, chemical and paint input baskets, and as a rupee import-bill headwind. The effect is a two-sided watch (escalation versus mediation) on the cost-exposed cohort — COROMANDEL, CEATLTD, BELRISE, EICHERMOT and VBL are the named cascade targets; CEAT's June-quarter profit crash last week is the live proof this channel is already biting. Tomorrow's read: whether crude holds above $85 or the mediation track pulls it back.

**The tariff-cliff edge is four days from a binary.** The cause is the unsigned first-phase US trade pact with the tariff deadline now on 24-July. The mechanism is the US-export-exposure edge: an adverse or missed outcome reverts most Indian US-bound goods to higher tariff rates. The effect is a two-sided setup on the most-levered names — Garware Hi-Tech Films (polyester/PET film) directly, Welspun Corp's India export leg, and the IT cohort by extension. A market this calm into an unsigned deadline is either pricing a resolution or under-pricing the tail.
