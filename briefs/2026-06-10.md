## What Happened

### A gap-up that faded, with breadth giving way underneath

Wednesday opened strong — about 600 Sensex points higher on overnight crude relief — but the lift drained through the session to leave Nifty 50 essentially flat at 23,214.95, down 0.12%. The real signal sat under the headline: large-caps held while the broader market sold off hard, with the Midcap index down 1.49% and the Smallcap down 1.33%. Tuesday's bank-led relief leg partially reversed, public-sector banks giving back 1.39% after Tuesday's 3.62% surge, and an overnight US-tech wobble — the Nasdaq off 0.97% after President Trump signalled strikes on Iran "might resume" — capped the upside before India could build on its open. For the principal the read is a pause inside the correction, not a fresh leg up: the relief trade has run, and breadth is the first thing to crack when it does.

### Fertilisers bid while metals took a beating

The day split cleanly into two stock-level themes. Fertilisers were the bid — Chambal Fertilisers up 5.04% and Coromandel up 4.07% — after a government official flagged that surging global prices could double the FY27 fertiliser-subsidy bill to about ₹3.4 trillion, with the monsoon's favourable advance adding to kharif nutrient demand. Metals were the rout: aluminium futures crashed 9.30%, dragging Hindalco down 3.47%, Hindustan Zinc 2.86% and our pipe-maker Welspun Corp 2.92%. The fertiliser read-through reaches one tracked name worth noting — Dhanuka, which we own, did NOT join the bid, because the subsidy story is a nutrient-demand (urea and DAP) event, not a crop-protection one. The metals weakness is a commodity-driven beta hit on Welspun, not a thesis event.

### West Asia held through a fresh scare, but nothing is signed

The crude relief that powered the open came from West Asia stepping back: the April ceasefire broadly held through the 7-8 June flare-up, Iran's halt of operations stayed in place, and Brent sat contained around $94. But the floor is rhetoric, not a settlement. Overnight, Trump signalled US strikes on Iran "might resume" after Iran reportedly targeted a US helicopter — no fresh strike materialised, but it was enough to knock US tech and cap India's session. Iran's suspension stays conditional on Israel halting its Lebanon offensive, which continues, and the Strait of Hormuz reopening is still being negotiated, not signed. Crude stays the single most thesis-load-bearing variable in the book, since India imports roughly 85% of its oil — and the war-premium can re-spike on any Lebanon or Hormuz flare-up. Wednesday's calm is a reprieve, not a resolution.
