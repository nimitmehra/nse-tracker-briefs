## What Happened

### Eicher Motors strikes a financing wedding

**Eicher Motors signs a 50:50 financing joint venture with Volvo.** Eicher Motors (EICHERMOT), the Royal Enfield maker and 54.4 percent owner of VE Commercial Vehicles, committed ₹750 crore of equity to the new vehicle on Thursday, with closing slated for the first half of 2027. Shares gained 1.10 percent at ₹6,968.00, still 14.92 percent off the 52-week high. The venture will finance Volvo and Eicher commercial trucks first; Royal Enfield retail financing sits in the second-stage scope. Mr Vinod Kumar Aggarwal — chief executive of VE Commercial Vehicles since 2018 and operator of the twenty-year Volvo-Eicher partnership — was elevated to Executive Vice-Chairman the same evening. Recurring fee-and-spread revenue at low capital intensity is the operating model. The cleanest structural Tier-1 catalyst this window has produced.

### Engineers India prints headlines, the audit prints risk

**Engineers India falls 7.78 percent on a receivables flag.** Engineers India (ENGINERSIN), the public-sector engineering consultancy that built much of the country's downstream refining capacity, posted FY26 profit growth of 35 percent — and lost the day's deepest Tier-1 cut on a session the broader tape rallied. FY26 revenue closed at ₹3,850 crore (up 27 percent) with profit of ₹833 crore; the board declared a ₹2.50 final dividend. The audit commentary — flagging unbilled and doubtful receivables — is what the market traded. Power Mech Projects (POWERMECH), printing a clean Q4 the previous evening, gained 3.16 percent on ₹57,811 crore of order backlog; Transrail Lighting (TRANSRAILL) gained 2.35 percent in sympathy. The capital-goods cohort split on receivables quality, not on headline profit. India's market regulator will want a closer look at the auditor's flag.

### Welspun Corp absorbs a halved bottom line

**Welspun Corp's Q4 profit halves on below-EBITDA items.** Welspun Corp (WELCORP), the line-pipe and steel-products manufacturer supplying the global oil-and-gas pipeline market, posted Q4 revenue up 10 percent to ₹4,313 crore and Q4 profit down 47 percent year on year to ₹372 crore. Shares slipped 3.15 percent in anticipation. The operating signal was less stark than the headline. Q4 EBITDA margin expanded year on year to 14.6 percent from 13.3 percent, and the consolidated global order book closed at ₹25,350 crore — roughly $2.6 billion, with execution running into FY28. The board still recommended a ₹5 final dividend. Friday's earnings call carries the burden of explaining the below-EBITDA items. The order book remains the deployment-calculus anchor.

### Dhanuka delivers a triple

**Dhanuka delivers profit, buyback and dividend on Monday.** Dhanuka Agritech (DHANUKA), the Gurugram-based agrochemical formulator at the centre of our recent thesis upgrade, delivered the Monday board meeting it had teed up. Q4 profit reached ₹97.77 crore (up 29.5 percent year on year); Q4 revenue of ₹483.33 crore exceeded the ₹480 crore threshold the system had been monitoring. The board approved a ₹70 crore buyback at ₹1,400 a share — a 27 to 33 percent premium to Monday's open — alongside a ₹2 final dividend. Shares rallied 14.76 percent intra-day Monday and are 7.80 percent higher over the week. BusinessToday carried a brokerage target revision on Thursday pointing to roughly 37 percent further upside. The Q4 thesis-event trigger has flipped from elevated to active. The buyback premium read as conviction; the price action confirmed.
