## What Happened

### The global AI-trade unwind was the trigger — landing on an extended, primed market
The session was made overnight, abroad. The trigger was a global AI-trade *position-unwind* — not a fresh earnings miss (Broadcom's miss was three weeks ago, on 3-4 June). The proximate causes: AI-competition anxiety after senior leadership departures at Alphabet, Korean regulators leaning on leveraged chip-linked products, and the liquidation of names that had run too far. **South Korea's KOSPI fell 8-10% and triggered its circuit breaker *twice*** (Samsung −8.8%, SK Hynix −11.6%), Nikkei −3.2%, Hong Kong/Shanghai ~−2%, Nasdaq futures −2% — the most crowded global trade de-risking at once. India, with no large listed chipmakers, sold off as a recipient of global risk-off (on a market already extended after a six-session rally and ripe for profit-booking): foreign-sensitive metals (−3.22%) and the IT cohort (−2.23%, the offshore proxy for global tech demand) took the brunt. India VIX jumped 8.59% to 13.94 — genuine, imported fear, the mirror of Monday's easing.

### The selloff was broad — domestic-positive but globally-overwhelmed; pharma the only refuge
This was the EM-rotation paradox in textbook form: **the India-specific news was good, and the market fell anyway.** Brent held a three-month low (the Iran/oil relief intact), the bond market was *firmer* (10Y eased to 6.84%, a 3-month low; the curve bull-flattened on a strong foreign bid — FPIs bought >$2.2bn of G-secs this month, a 15-month high, after the RBI's 5-Jun dollar-inflow measures), and June's flash PMIs — while softer (Composite 57.4 vs May's 59.3; manufacturing 54.5, a two-month low; services at a 17-month low (57.3)) — were still firmly in expansion. None of that mattered against the global tape. Thirteen of fifteen Nifty sectors closed red; the **only green was Pharma (+1.77%, a record high) and Healthcare (+0.54%)** — on a confluence of the US-FDA ifosfamide-supply catalyst, defensive rotation, and a weaker-rupee export tailwind (Indian pharma earns 70-90% abroad, so the softer rupee lifts its rupee earnings, which is why pharma rose while IT, the same kind of exporter, fell). Breadth was decisively negative — ~2,678 decliners to ~1,420 advancers; Midcap −0.89%, Smallcap −0.45%.

### Two de-confounding catches the desks flagged
Both intel desks independently corrected two tempting-but-wrong causal links. **(1)** Bharat Electronics' ₹1,081 cr defence order was a **22-June** event (+1% that day); on 23-June BEL actually **fell −2.62%** with the tape — the order is not a Tuesday driver. **(2)** HDFC Bank fell with the market as a heavyweight drag; the Keki Mistry interim-chair extension was an **18-June** event (the stock *rose* +1.5% then), not a fresh Tuesday negative. Neither gets attributed to Tuesday.
